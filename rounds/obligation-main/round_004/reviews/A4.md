## Claims that need proof

1. **Average-to-pointwise upgrade for the fourth moment (the deepest missing obligation).** None of A1/A2/A3 confronts this. The fourth-moment expansion $|S_2(D;X)|^4=\sum\beta\cdots e(\tfrac X4\tfrac{N}{\prod d})$ controls, after integrating over an $X$-window of length $\asymp\mathcal X$, the quantity $\frac1{\mathcal X}\int_{\mathcal X}^{2\mathcal X}|S_2|^4\,dX\ll\sum_{|N|\le cD^4/X}|\beta\cdots|$ -- an **average** over $X$. But M9-M2 requires the **pointwise** bound $|S_2(D;X)|\ll X^{1/4+\epsilon}$ for the specific $X$ at which $P(X)$ is evaluated. A purely absolute fourth moment at a single fixed $X$ collapses to the trivial $|S_2|\ll D\ll X^{1/2}$ (Cauchy budget: $|S_2|^2\le(\sum_h|\beta_h|)(\sum_h|\beta_h||V_h|^2)\ll(\log H)^2D^2$). So even a *complete* absolute $N=0$+near-collision bound at $X^{1+\epsilon}$ proves M9-M2 only in mean-fourth-power, and the upgrade to sup requires a separate, currently-unstated lemma (e.g. $|S_2(X_0)|^4\le\frac1{|I|}\int_I|S_2|^4+|I|\sup_I|S_2|^3|S_2'|$, with $|S_2'|\ll\frac{H}{D}\|\cdot\|$ and an optimized window $|I|$). This is exactly the "smoothing/unsmoothing" step the safety rules forbid skipping. **It should become an explicit obligation.**

2. **Unclassified $N=0$ mass bound** (A2's "$O(D^2\log^2 D)$"). This is a numerical extrapolation from $H=5,D=10$ -- far too small to distinguish $D^2\log^2$ from $D^{2.5}$. Unproven; correctly marked open, but the heuristic "does not threaten the exponent" is not yet justified.

3. **Near-collision absolute mass** (A1 Section 9, A2 obstruction 2). At $D\asymp X^{1/2}$ the tolerance $D^4/X\asymp X$ is enormous, and A2's own obstruction 2 notes an $N=1$ band could plausibly carry $O(D^3)$ mass, which would give $X^{3/4}$ and destroy the target. This is the genuine analytic frontier and is unproven on both sides.

## Possible errors or hidden assumptions

**A2 -- R5 refereeing uses the wrong norm (most dangerous error in the round).** A2's "proof" that the DDP trap is a "phantom menace" computes $\int|R(X)|^2dX\approx O(XD)$ and concludes RMS $\approx X^{1/4}$. Even granting the arithmetic, this is a **mean-square / RMS** statement: it bounds $\frac1{\mathcal X}\int_{\mathcal X}^{2\mathcal X}|R|^2\approx D$, i.e. $|R|\approx\sqrt D$ *on average over $X$*. R5-Full is a **pointwise** bound (because $P(X)$ is a pointwise error term and the conditional bridge is pointwise). Mean-square $\ne$ sup. Worse, this mean-square fact is essentially classical (the circle error is $\asymp R^{1/4}$ in mean square -- known unconditionally), so A2 has rederived a known easy statement and mislabeled it as resolving R5-Full's hard pointwise content.
