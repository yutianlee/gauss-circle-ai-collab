## Claims that look correct

1. **H4 source audit (A1).** The equations transcribed from the rendered Vaaler PDF are mathematically consistent with the project's notation. The coefficient $\alpha_{h,H}$ derived from the printed $(-2\pi i h)^{-1}$ factor, and the floor-compatible endpoint adjustment, are correct while the underlying PDF check was available. The Fejer kernel properties used in R5-Full are standard and match Vaaler's definitions.

2. **R5-Full product-count proof (A1).** The argument starts from $|R_H^F(t)|\le (2H+2)^{-1}K_H(t)$, retains the positive kernel, and bounds $\sum_{d\asymp D} K_H(T_d)/H$ via dyadic decomposition and the divisor bound $\#\{d\asymp D:\|T_d\|\le\Delta\}\ll_\epsilon (1+\Delta D)X^\epsilon$. The constant-shifting for $\rho=1,3$ is handled correctly. The result $\ll_\epsilon X^{1/4+\epsilon}$ uniformly for $X^{1/4}\le D\le X^{1/2}$ follows, provided the Fejer dyadic majorant constants are innocuous. This resolves the older arbitrary-coefficient concern.

3. **M2-beta algebra (A1, and used by all).** $C_h=e(h/4)-e(3h/4)=2i\,\chi_4(h)$ for odd $h$, and $\beta_{h,H}=-\frac{\Phi(|h|/(H+1))}{\pi|h|}\chi_4(|h|)1_{2\nmid h}$ is real, even, and consistent with the H4 coefficient convention. This is a direct algebraic consequence, correctly written.

4. **Denominator-paired exact resonance bound (all three agents).** After setting $d_1=d_2=a$, $d_3=d_4=b$, the zero-resonance condition reduces to $(h_1-h_2)b+(h_3-h_4)a=0$. With $g=(a,b)$, $a=ga'$, $b=gb'$, and $(a',b')=1$, the solution set is parametrized by $h_1-h_2=ta'$, $h_3-h_4=-tb'$ (up to sign, which is harmless). The absolute weighted mass product $|\beta_{h_1}\beta_{h_2}\beta_{h_3}\beta_{h_4}|$ is bounded by $1/(|h_1h_2h_3h_4|)$, and the harmonic-convolution estimate
$$
   L_H(q):=\sum_{\substack{1\le |h|\le H\\ 1\le|h+q|\le H}}\frac1{|h|\,|h+q|}\ll\frac{\log 2H}{\max(1,|q|)}
$$
   (proved by telescoping in A4 Lemma A) then yields $T_{t=0}\asymp D^2$ and $T_{t\ne0}\ll D(\log 2H)^2$. Consequently $T\ll D^2(\log 2H)^2\ll D^2X^\epsilon\ll X^{1+\epsilon}$. The proof is rigorous modulo the coefficient magnitude bound $|\beta_h|\ll 1_{2\nmid h}/|h|$ that depends on H4.

5. **A4's Lemma A and Lemma B.** The harmonic-convolution telescoping and the gcd bijection are elementary and unconditional. They are reusable building blocks.

6. **A2's pair-swapped family equivalence.** $d_1=d_4=a,\;d_2=d_3=b$ yields $(h_1-h_4)b-(h_2-h_3)a=0$, which is identical in form to the denominator-paired condition after permuting indices. The mass is therefore bounded by the same argument without new computation. This claim is correct.

7. **A4's sign-insensitivity observation.** The denominator-paired absolute-weight bound does not depend on the $\chi_4(h)$ signs; replacing them by arbitrary signs of equal magnitude leaves the bound unchanged. Therefore this diagonal piece alone cannot deliver the target $X^{1/4+\epsilon}$ cancellation; a signed ingredient is mandatory. This is an accurate logical deduction.

## Possible errors or hidden assumptions

- **H4 endpoint adaptation (A1).** The argument that $|\psi_F(n)-V_H(n)|=1/2$ and that the Fejer majorant exactly provides $1/2$ at integers is correct, but it assumes that $V_H(n)$ is computed as $0$ (which holds because the Fourier coefficients are odd). This should be stated explicitly in the proof draft.
- **Product-count for shifted legs (A1).** The passage from $\|(X/d+\rho)/4\|\le\Delta$ to $|X-d\ell|\ll\Delta D$ with $\ell\equiv-\rho\pmod4$ is valid, but one must ensure that the implied constant in the $|\cdot|\ll\Delta D$ does not depend on the particular $\ell$ in a way that breaks the sum over $r$. A standard divisor-bound argument handles this, and it is fine.
- **A2's semi-diagonal parametrization.** If the sums are written as $h_1+h_3=U$, $h_2+h_4=V$, the product of the two convolution sums (each over pairs of frequencies with a fixed sum) is not immediately separable into independent sums over $h_1,h_3$ and $h_2,h_4$ because the pairs are linked only through the global equation $bU=aV$. The derivation must carefully separate the sums: for fixed $a,b$, the condition fixes $U = (a/b)V$, and then the mass is $\sum_{V} (\sum_{h_1,h_3:\,h_1+h_3=U} \frac1{|h_1h_3|}) (\sum_{h_2,h_4:\,h_2+h_4=V} \frac1{|h_2h_4|})$ with $U = (a/b)V$ possibly non-integer; but the original equation was $bU = aV$, so $U$ and $V$ must be integers. Since $a,b$ are integers, the condition $bU=aV$ implies that $a$ divides $bU$, and with $\gcd(a,b)=1$, $a$ divides $U$. Write $U = a' t$, $V = b' t$ as in the difference case. This is exactly the same gcd splitting. So the parametrization $h_1+h_3 = t a'$, $h_2+h_4 = t b'$ is valid. Then the convolution sums over $h_1+h_3 = ta'$ and $h_2+h_4 = tb'$ are independent. The bound for the convolution $\sum_{h_1+h_3=K} 1/(|h_1||h_3|)$ is identical to $L_H(K)$ after the substitution $h_3\mapsto -h_3$ (since $|h_3| = |-h_3|$). So the semi-diagonal bound is indeed isomorphic, and A2's intuition is correct. A full proof would benefit from stating this substitution explicitly. Therefore, while the claim is correct, the presentation omitted this small but crucial step.

- **A4's lower bound sharpness.** The claim $T\asymp D^2$ assumes that $|\beta_h|\gg 1/|h|$ for a positive density of $h$ (e.g. $\Phi$ bounded below near $0$). The upper bound only requires $|\beta_h|\le 1/|h|$, which holds regardless. The sharpness is not required for the upper bound, but it is a useful diagnostic.

- **All agents assume the dyadic smooth weight $w_D(d)$ satisfies $|w_D(d)|\le C$.** That is a standing assumption in H1-H3 and is acceptable.

- **The H4 coefficient magnitude bound** is the single external dependency that underlies all denominator-paired (and likely the other paired) bounds. The magnitude bound $|\Phi(u)|\ll 1$ on $[0,1]$ is verified in the audit. However, the bound $|\beta_h|\ll 1/|h|$ also uses that $\Phi(0)=1$ and $\Phi$ is continuous, which is fine. No other hidden dependencies.
