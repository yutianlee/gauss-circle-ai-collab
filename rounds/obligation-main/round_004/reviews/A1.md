## Claims that look correct

### Claim ledger

1. **A2/A4 denominator-paired resonance reduction:** correct.

   With $d_1=d_2=a$ and $d_3=d_4=b$,

$$
   N
   =
   h_1ab^2-h_2ab^2+h_3a^2b-h_4a^2b
   =
   ab\big((h_1-h_2)b+(h_3-h_4)a\big).
$$

2. **A2/A4 gcd parameterization:** correct.

   From

$$
   (h_1-h_2)b'=-(h_3-h_4)a',
   \qquad (a',b')=1,
$$

   one gets

$$
   h_1-h_2=ta',
   \qquad
   h_3-h_4=-tb',
   \qquad t\in\mathbb Z.
$$

3. **A4 harmonic-convolution estimate:** correct in substance.

   The bound

$$
   L_H(q)\ll \frac{\log 2H}{\max(1,|q|)}
$$

   is a standard elementary telescoping estimate and is adequate for this subcase.

4. **A4 denominator-paired bound:** correct under the stated coefficient magnitude hypothesis.

   The clean state-level form should be

$$
   T_{\mathrm{dp}}(D;X)
   \ll_\epsilon D^2X^\epsilon
   \le X^{1+\epsilon},
   \qquad X^{1/4}\le D\le X^{1/2}.
$$

5. **A4 lower-bound caution:** correct.

   The $t=0$ contribution already gives $T_{\mathrm{dp}}\asymp D^2$ under mild nonvanishing of coefficients near $h=1$, so this subfamily cannot be improved below $D^2$ by absolute-value estimates.

6. **A2 pair-swapped family:** likely correct, but should be written as a separate lemma.

   For

$$
   d_1=d_4=a,\qquad d_2=d_3=b,
$$

   the equation becomes

$$
   (h_1-h_4)b+(h_3-h_2)a=0,

   which is denominator-paired after index relabeling.

7. **A2 semi-diagonal family:** likely correct as an absolute bound, but A2's proof is too compressed for promotion.

   For

   the equation becomes

   (h_1+h_3)b-(h_2+h_4)a=0.

   This is controlled by the same convolution estimate with sums instead of differences, but the $k=0$ case is $h_1=-h_3$, $h_2=-h_4$, not $h_1=h_3$, $h_2=h_4$. The bound is still plausible.

8. **A3 diagnostic-only stance:** correct.

## Claims that need proof

$$

   The equation

$$

$$
   \sum_{\substack{h_1+h_3=q\\1\le |h_1|,|h_3|\le H}}

   including the $q=0$ case, parity restrictions, two-sided signs, and endpoint truncation.

3. **A2's pair-swapped proof needs a short separate lemma.**

   It is probably a pure relabeling of denominator-paired, but this should be written explicitly to avoid sign mistakes in the $+,-,+,-$ fourth-moment convention.

## Possible errors or hidden assumptions

### A2

1. **Status overpromotion.** A2 proposes `proved_internal` for `M9-M2-denominator-paired-weighted-bound`; this should be downgraded to `derived_under_assumptions` until H4 is promoted.

2. **Semi-diagonal equality case is misstated by analogy.** In the semi-diagonal family the $k=0$ case gives $h_1=-h_3$ and $h_2=-h_4$, not equality of the paired indices. The mass bound should still hold, but the proof must state the correct zero-sum case.

3. **R5-Full is not an $L^2$ theorem in the current proof graph.** The accepted R5 route is the positive Fejer majorant plus product-count argument. An $L^2$ residual discussion is not enough to reconcile the old H5r/DDP trap.

4. **DDP trap language is imprecise.** A2 says the divisor-product fourth-moment barrier "slightly exceeds strict $O(X)$ without epsilon." Since the proof graph allows $X^\epsilon$, logarithms alone are not the real obstruction. The actual trap is loss of positivity and reduction to arbitrary coefficient reciprocal sums.

5. **Numerical taxonomy is not evidence unless materialized.** A2 mentions toy numerical masses but does not provide committed scripts, table schemas, precision logs, or commands.

### A3

1. **Code-level error in exponential notation.** The visible code calls `mp.e(...)`. In `mpmath`, `mp.e` is the Euler constant object, not the project's additive character. The script should define

$$
   e(t)=\exp(2\pi i t)
$$

   explicitly, for example as `exp2pii(t) = mp.exp(2*mp.pi*mp.j*t)`.

2. **M1 phase appears wrong.** The visible skeleton uses $hX/(4d)$ for $\mathcal M_1$, while the project's official $\mathcal M_1$ phase is $hX/d$.

3. **Float parsing may destroy exact integer arithmetic.** Arguments such as `--X 1e6` parsed as float should not feed exact $N$ enumeration. Exact computations should parse integer strings into `int` or `sympy.Integer`.

4. **Full fourth-moment enumeration is computationally explosive.** A naive enumeration over $(h_i,d_i)$ scales like $H^4D^4$. A3 should use hashing by rational values, numerator buckets, or denominator-paired specialized enumeration for nontrivial ranges.

5. **Proposed evidence paths are not yet positive evidence.** A3's own output says the bundle is to be executed later; therefore the state patch must not add those file paths as positive evidence until files exist and pass.

6. **R5 diagnostics are currently too generic.** A3 says the exact residual formula depends on H4 and R5 source audit. That is correct, but it means the R5 script cannot validate R5-Full yet.

### A4

1. **H4 dependency remains load-bearing.** A4 correctly flags this, but the judge patch must preserve it.

2. **The lemma is unsigned.** It does not use $\chi_4$ cancellation and cannot imply the endpoint estimate for $\mathcal M_2$.

3. **It handles only one exact $N=0$ subfamily.** It does not prove the diagonal-core obligation.

4. **Lower bound needs a nonvanishing coefficient assumption.** A4's upper bound only needs $|\beta_h|\ll1/|h|$, but the lower bound $T\asymp D^2$ needs $\Phi$ nonzero on a positive-density small-$h$ set. That is fine as a caution but not part of the upper-bound proof.

5. **Near-collision scaling should be normalized carefully.** For the denominator-paired family,

$$
   N=abL,\qquad L=(h_1-h_2)b+(h_3-h_4)a.
$$

   Thus the general near-collision threshold

$$
   |N|\le C_0D^4/X
$$

   becomes

$$
   |L|\le C_0D^2/X,
$$

   since $ab\asymp D^2$. Because $D\le X^{1/2}$, this linear tolerance is $O(1)$. Future denominator-paired near-collision work should use this sharper normalized threshold.

## Suggested synthesis

The judge should accept the denominator-paired proof in the following conservative form:

**Lemma: denominator-paired exact resonance, H4-dependent form.** Assume

$$
|\beta_{h,H_D}|\ll \frac{1_{2\nmid h}}{|h|},
\qquad
|w_D(d)|\le 1,
\qquad
X^{1/4}\le D\le X^{1/2},
\qquad
H_D\asymp DX^{-1/4}.
$$

Then

$$
\sum_{\substack{
a,b\asymp D\\
1\le |h_i|\le H_D\\
(h_1-h_2)b+(h_3-h_4)a=0
}}
|\beta_{h_1}\beta_{h_2}\beta_{h_3}\beta_{h_4}|
|w_D(a)|^2|w_D(b)|^2
\ll_\epsilon D^2X^\epsilon
\le X^{1+\epsilon}.
$$

**Proof skeleton to insert into the proof draft:**

1. Set $g=(a,b)$, $a=ga'$, $b=gb'$, $(a',b')=1$.
2. Parameterize

$$
   h_1-h_2=ta',
   \qquad
   h_3-h_4=-tb'.
$$

3. Define

$$
   L_H(q)
   =
   \sum_{\substack{1\le |h|\le H\\1\le |h+q|\le H}}
   \frac1{|h|\,|h+q|}.
$$

4. Use

$$
   L_H(0)\ll 1,
   \qquad
   L_H(q)\ll \frac{\log 2H}{|q|}\quad(q\ne0).
$$

5. For fixed $a,b$,

$$
   \sum_t L_H(ta')L_H(tb')
   \ll 1+\frac{(\log 2H)^2}{a'b'}
   \ll (\log 2H)^2.
$$

6. Summing over $a,b\asymp D$ gives

$$
   T\ll D^2+(\log 2H)^2
   \sum_{g\le D}
   \sum_{\substack{a',b'\asymp D/g\\(a',b')=1}}
   \frac1{a'b'}
   \ll D^2+D(\log 2H)^2.
$$

7. Since $D\ge X^{1/4}$ and $\log H\ll_\epsilon X^\epsilon$, conclude

$$
   T\ll_\epsilon D^2X^\epsilon.
$$

This proof is sufficiently explicit for a state promotion to `derived_under_assumptions`.
