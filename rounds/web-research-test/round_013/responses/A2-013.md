## Detailed reasoning: H12 Trace Invariance and Conjugacy Cycles

Spectral norm bounds often proceed via the method of moments, bounding the largest eigenvalue of the Hermitian matrix $K^\chi = U^* K U$ by computing the trace of its $m$-th power, utilizing the identity $\|A\|_{\rm op} = \lim_{m \to \infty} (\operatorname{Tr}((AA^*)^m))^{1/(2m)}$.

[PROVED] Let $m \in \mathbb{N}$ be an arbitrary integer cycle length. We evaluate the trace of $(U^* K U)^m$:
$$
\operatorname{Tr}((U^* K U)^m) = \operatorname{Tr}((U^* K U)(U^* K U) \cdots (U^* K U)).
$$
Because $U U^* = U^2 = I_{\mathcal{V}_D}$, all internal unitary pairs cancel identically:
$$
\operatorname{Tr}((U^* K U)^m) = \operatorname{Tr}(U^* K^m U).
$$
By the cyclic commutativity of the trace operator, $\operatorname{Tr}(A B) = \operatorname{Tr}(B A)$. Let $A = U^*$ and $B = K^m U$:
$$
\operatorname{Tr}(U^* K^m U) = \operatorname{Tr}(K^m U U^*) = \operatorname{Tr}(K^m I) = \operatorname{Tr}(K^m).
$$

**Diagnostic Boundary:**
This identity proves that counting closed collision cycles of any length $m$ over the full index set $\mathcal{D}_{\rm odd}$ annihilates the spatial character. The sum of the weighted paths through the graph represented by $K^\chi$ is identically equal to the sum of the weighted paths through the unsigned graph $K$. Expanding this into coordinates reveals the arithmetic mechanism: a trace evaluates closed paths $d_1 \to d_2 \to \dots \to d_m \to d_1$. The sign weight accumulated along this path is $\chi_4(d_1)^2 \chi_4(d_2)^2 \dots \chi_4(d_m)^2$. Because every vertex is entered and exited exactly once in a closed cycle, the square of the character is identically $+1$ at every vertex, annihilating all spatial oscillation.

However, this is a narrow structural theorem. It does *not* imply that all trace methods are blind. If a statistic uses a non-conjugacy form, such as $\operatorname{Tr}(K_1 U K_2 U)$ where $K_1 \neq K_2$ are sub-block matrices, the cyclic permutation gives $\operatorname{Tr}(K_2 U K_1 U)$, which does not trigger the $U^2 = I$ collapse. Bipartite graphs and unclosed paths retain sign signatures.

## Detailed reasoning: C3-Affine and Rational Parity Geometry

We repair the stylized C3 parity-collapse diagnostic by formalizing it directly on the geometry of the odd-lattice Poisson transform, fulfilling Objective 3. Let the dual frequency domain be the half-integer lattice $\Lambda = \frac{1}{2}\mathbb{Z}$, which arises as the support for the Fourier transform of a function restricted to odd integers.

Define the dual parity signature generated by the Poisson summation on odd integers as:
$$
\sigma(\xi) = \frac{1}{2}(-1)^{2\xi} = \frac{1}{2} e^{2\pi i \xi}, \qquad \xi \in \Lambda.
$$
Note that since $\xi \in \frac{1}{2}\mathbb{Z}$, $2\xi \in \mathbb{Z}$, making the real-valued alternating oscillation well-defined. We evaluate the persistence of this oscillation under generalized affine spacing shifts $\xi_2 = a\xi_1 + q$.

**Case 1: C3-Translation (A-Process Shift)**
Let $a = 1$ and $q \in \Lambda$. This models a standard Weyl differencing (A-process) step where the dual spacing lattice is shifted.
$$
\sigma(\xi_1)\sigma(\xi_1+q) = \frac{1}{4} (-1)^{2\xi_1} (-1)^{2\xi_1 + 2q} = \frac{1}{4} (-1)^{4\xi_1 + 2q} = \frac{1}{4} (-1)^{4\xi_1} (-1)^{2q}.
$$
Because $\xi_1 \in \frac{1}{2}\mathbb{Z}$, $4\xi_1 \in 2\mathbb{Z}$, which is an even integer. Therefore, $(-1)^{4\xi_1} = 1$ identically for all $\xi_1 \in \Lambda$.
$$
\sigma(\xi_1)\sigma(\xi_1+q) = \frac{1}{4} (-1)^{2q}.
$$
[PROVED] Translation shifts identically factor out the internal spatial variable $\xi_1$. An A-process on the uniform dual lattice degenerates the parity coefficient to a global constant dependent only on the shift distance $q$, rendering it character-blind.

**Case 2: C3-Odd-Dilation (Rational Fractional Shift)**
Let the transformation be an odd dilation $a \in 2\mathbb{Z}+1$, and $q = 0$. This models a fractional spacing step mapping the lattice via an odd multiplier.
$$
\sigma(\xi_1)\sigma(a\xi_1) = \frac{1}{4} (-1)^{2\xi_1} (-1)^{2a\xi_1} = \frac{1}{4} (-1)^{2\xi_1(1+a)}.
$$
Since $a$ is an odd integer, $1+a = 2k$ for some integer $k$.
$$
(-1)^{2\xi_1(1+a)} = (-1)^{4\xi_1 k}.
$$
Since $4\xi_1 \in 2\mathbb{Z}$, $4\xi_1 k$ is even, yielding $(-1)^{4\xi_1 k} = 1$.
$$
\sigma(\xi_1)\sigma(a\xi_1) = \frac{1}{4}.
$$
[PROVED] Odd rational dilations unconditionally annihilate the parity oscillation.

**Case 3: C3-Even-Dilation (Parity Preservation)**
Let $a \in 2\mathbb{Z}$ be an even integer, and $q = 0$.
$$
\sigma(\xi_1)\sigma(a\xi_1) = \frac{1}{4} (-1)^{2\xi_1(1+a)}.
$$
Since $a$ is even, $1+a$ is an odd integer. The parity of $2\xi_1(1+a)$ matches the parity of $2\xi_1$.
$$
\frac{1}{4} (-1)^{2\xi_1(1+a)} = \frac{1}{4} (-1)^{2\xi_1} = \frac{1}{2} \sigma(\xi_1).
$$
[PROVED] Even rational dilations structurally preserve the spatial parity oscillation.

This analysis bounds the "odd-lattice failure" hypothesis. It proves that while generic continuous spacing or pure translation Weyl differencing collapses the parity, explicit rational collision structures (such as Bombieri--Iwaniec matrices restricted to even dilations) formally retain the sign data.

$$

Substitute $u = 4v+r$, so $v = (u-r)/4$ and $dv = du/4$. The integral becomes:
$$

Thus, $G_4(m) = 2i\chi_4(m)$ identically for all integers $m$. The exact transformed sum is:
