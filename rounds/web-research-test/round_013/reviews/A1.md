A2's modulo-$4$ Poisson calculation for H13 is formally correct up to the usual Fourier-sign convention. Splitting $d=4k+r$, $r\in\{1,3\}$, gives a Gauss factor

$$

and the transformed integral has phase

The Hessian degeneracy of the resulting phase $\sqrt{Xhn}$ is correct and remains a strong guardrail against generic full-rank two-dimensional stationary phase or generic full-rank decoupling.

A3's M9b modeling needs more care. Splitting $h=4q+r$ gives

$$
e(hX/(4d))=e((q+r/4)X/d).
$$

This is a fractional-frequency shift in the outer variable. It is not automatically equivalent to replacing $F(x)$ by a shifted denominator such as $1/(4x+\beta)$. One may either treat $q+\beta$ as a shifted frequency, split arithmetic progressions in $h$, or absorb $e(\beta X/d)$ into the $d$-weight; the last option creates a highly oscillatory "weight" and is not bounded-variation in the usual Li--Yang sense. This is a real theorem-extension gap.

## Possible errors or hidden assumptions

A2 overstates several diagnostics. Q1-Spectral does not limit "standard continuous $L^2$ decoupling" in general; it only blocks arguments that reduce the signed problem to a diagonal-unitary conjugate and then use a unitarily invariant norm or absolute-value matrix. A decoupling argument formulated before this matrix abstraction may not be covered by Q1-Spectral.

A2's CRI statistic is called "proved" as an evasion of $U^*KU$, but only the algebraic fact that it is a bipartite cross-residue form is proved. Whether it produces cancellation is completely open. In particular, a standard double-large-sieve or operator-norm bound on the bipartite matrix $B$ would again erase the sign advantage.

A2's H13 language sometimes implies a "sign-preserving route." The transform is sign-preserving at the formal Poisson level, but after stationary phase the dual phase is Hessian-degenerate and any direct A-process on the dual $\chi_4(m)$ risks the same parity collapse previously identified. H13 should remain exploratory.

A3's stationary-phase formula mixes notations in one place. With $e(t)=e^{2\pi i t}$, the leading term should be written schematically as

$$
I(-m)\sim \frac{w_D(u_0)}{\sqrt{2m/u_0}}e\left(2\sqrt{kXm}+\frac18\right),
$$

not as an expression simultaneously using $e(\cdot)$ and explicit $2\pi i$ exponentials. This is a notation issue, but it matters for code and signs.

A3's R5 numerical prediction that normalized residual ratios should be bounded by a small constant such as $5$ is heuristic. The rigorous statement is $O_\epsilon(X^\epsilon)$ after normalization by $X^{1/4}$, not a uniform small numerical constant.

A3's code sketches are useful but need edge-case corrections: define $H_D\ge1$ explicitly on the long-block boundary; avoid invoking Vaaler on short blocks $D<X^{1/4}$; and verify negative-frequency handling separately for M9b, where $\chi_4(h)$ and the $1/(4d)$ phase interact with conjugation.
