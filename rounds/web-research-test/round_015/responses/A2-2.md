## Q1-Spectral: Bounded-Scope Operator Norm Diagnostic

[PROVED]

We formalize the Q1-Spectral diagnostic to precisely define the limits of operator-norm-based spacing methods.

Let $D \ge 1$ be a dyadic scale. Define the finite index set of odd denominators in the dyadic block:
$$
\mathcal{D}_{\text{odd}} = \{d \in \mathbb{Z} : D \le d < 2D, \; 2 \nmid d\}.
$$
Let $\mathcal{V}_D = \ell^2(\mathcal{D}_{\text{odd}})$ be the finite-dimensional complex vector space of functions $v: \mathcal{D}_{\text{odd}} \to \mathbb{C}$, equipped with the standard inner product:
$$
\langle v, w \rangle = \sum_{d \in \mathcal{D}_{\text{odd}}} \overline{v_d} w_d.
$$
Define the linear operator $U: \mathcal{V}_D \to \mathcal{V}_D$ by diagonal multiplication with the spatial character:
$$
(Uv)_d = \chi_4(d) v_d.
$$
Since $\chi_4(d) \in \{-1, 1\}$ for all $d \in \mathcal{D}_{\text{odd}}$, we have $\overline{\chi_4(d)} = \chi_4(d)$ and $\chi_4(d)^2 = 1$. The adjoint $U^*$ satisfies:
$$
\langle U^* v, w \rangle = \langle v, U w \rangle = \sum_{d \in \mathcal{D}_{\text{odd}}} \overline{v_d} \chi_4(d) w_d = \sum_{d \in \mathcal{D}_{\text{odd}}} \overline{\chi_4(d) v_d} w_d = \langle U v, w \rangle.
$$
Thus, $U^* = U$. Furthermore, $(U^* U v)_d = \chi_4(d)^2 v_d = v_d$, which implies $U^* U = I$. Therefore, $U$ is a diagonal unitary involution.

Let $K: \mathcal{V}_D \to \mathcal{V}_D$ be any linear operator, representing a spacing matrix or Gram matrix arising from a Cauchy--Schwarz step over the frequency variable $h$. The operator norm of $K$ is defined as:
$$
\|K\|_{\text{op}} = \sup_{\|v\|_2=1} \|Kv\|_2.
$$
Consider the unitarily conjugated operator $U^* K U$. Its operator norm is:
$$
\|U^* K U\|_{\text{op}} = \sup_{\|v\|_2=1} \|U^* K U v\|_2.
$$
Because $U^*$ is a unitary operator, it preserves the $\ell^2$ norm, meaning $\|U^* y\|_2 = \|y\|_2$ for any vector $y \in \mathcal{V}_D$. Applying this to $y = K U v$, we obtain:
$$
\|U^* K U v\|_2 = \|K U v\|_2.
$$
As $v$ ranges over the unit sphere in $\mathcal{V}_D$, the vector $w = Uv$ also ranges over the entire unit sphere, because $U$ is a unitary bijection. Therefore:
$$
\sup_{\|v\|_2=1} \|K U v\|_2 = \sup_{\|w\|_2=1} \|Kw\|_2 = \|K\|_{\text{op}}.
$$
We conclude that $\|U^* K U\|_{\text{op}} = \|K\|_{\text{op}}$.

**Scope of the Diagnostic:**
This lemma strictly blocks any analytic method that bounds a signed quadratic form $v^* K v$ (where $v_d = \chi_4(d) w_d$) by separating the vector norm from the operator norm via the inequality $|v^* K v| \le \|v\|_2^2 \|K\|_{\text{op}}$. If the method relies entirely on bounding $\|K\|_{\text{op}}$, it is provably character-blind. This extends to spectral radius bounds, Schur tests, Gershgorin circle theorems, and Frobenius norm bounds applied to $U^* K U$, as all these unitarily invariant norms will yield identical values for $K$ and $U^* K U$.

This lemma does **not** block methods that evaluate the bilinear form $v^* K v$ directly without passing to the supremum over all vectors. It does not block methods that exploit the specific smoothness or arithmetic structure of the weight vector $w_D$. It does not block cross-residue interference statistics where the matrix is partitioned into non-square blocks.

## H12: Narrow Trace Invariance for Cyclic Statistics

$$

$$
\tilde{K}_{d_1, d_2} = \chi_4(d_1) K_{d_1, d_2} \chi_4(d_2).
$$
The trace of $\tilde{K}^m$ is $\operatorname{Tr}(\tilde{K}^m) = \operatorname{Tr}((U^* K U)^m)$. Since $U U^* = I$, we can expand the matrix power:
$$
(U^* K U)^m = (U^* K U)(U^* K U) \dots (U^* K U) = U^* K^m U.
$$
By the cyclic property of the trace, $\operatorname{Tr}(A B) = \operatorname{Tr}(B A)$, we have:
$$
\operatorname{Tr}(U^* K^m U) = \operatorname{Tr}(U U^* K^m) = \operatorname{Tr}(I K^m) = \operatorname{Tr}(K^m).

$$
\phi''(v_0) = \frac{2hX}{(2\sqrt{hX/m})^3} = \frac{2hX}{8(hX/m)^{3/2}} = \frac{m^{3/2}}{4(hX)^{1/2}}.
