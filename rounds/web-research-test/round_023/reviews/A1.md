## Most valuable input from others

### A2

A2's strongest contribution is the clean diagnosis of two standard Cauchy--Schwarz routes for

$$
\mathcal M_2(D;X)
=
4\sum_{1\le |h|\le H_D}\alpha_{h,H_D}C_hB_h(D;X),
\qquad
C_h=e(h/4)-e(3h/4).
$$

The unweighted $h$-Cauchy route has diagonal size

$$
H_DD\asymp D^2X^{-1/4},
$$

which reaches $X^{3/4}$ at $D\asymp X^{1/2}$, exceeding the squared target $X^{1/2+\epsilon}$. The weighted $|\alpha_h|$ route has acceptable diagonal size $D\log^2H_D$, but replaces $C_h$ by $|C_h|^2$, losing the $\chi_4(h)$ sign and leaving only odd-frequency support. This is a precise, bounded-scope obstruction to a common proof route.

A2's fourth-moment direction is also valuable. The exact expansion of

$$
\left|
\sum_h\alpha_hC_hB_h(D;X)
\right|^4
$$

retains the product of residue signs

$$
C_{h_1}\overline{C_{h_2}}C_{h_3}\overline{C_{h_4}},
$$

which on odd frequencies is a scalar multiple of

$$
\chi_4(h_1)\chi_4(h_2)\chi_4(h_3)\chi_4(h_4).
$$

The target scale

$$
\mathcal Q_4(D;X)\ll_\epsilon X^{1+\epsilon}
$$

is correctly calibrated for fourth-root extraction to $\mathcal M_2\ll X^{1/4+\epsilon}$. The output correctly downgrades the current diagonal-family bounds to "derived under assumptions" rather than a complete proof.

A2's CRI statistic is a useful falsification tool for the frequency-character leg. Splitting

$$
\mathcal M_2=8i(\Sigma_1-\Sigma_3)
$$

with $\Sigma_r$ over $h\equiv r\pmod4$ isolates the question of whether the two residue classes interfere constructively or destructively. The normalized test

$$
R_{\rm CRI}
=
\frac{|\Sigma_1-\Sigma_3|^2}{|\Sigma_1|^2+|\Sigma_3|^2}
$$

is the right diagnostic: values near $1$ suggest little usable residue interference; values substantially below $1$ justify deeper algebraic analysis.

## Claims that look correct

1. **A2's unweighted $h$-Cauchy diagonal obstruction for $\mathcal M_2$ is correct under the displayed normalization.** The scalar factor is $\asymp H_D$, the diagonal in the expanded kernel is $\asymp D$, and with $H_D\asymp DX^{-1/4}$ this gives $D^2X^{-1/4}$, i.e. $X^{3/4}$ at $D\asymp X^{1/2}$. This is not a lower bound for $\mathcal M_2$; it is a failure of that upper-bound route.

2. **A2's weighted $|\alpha_h|$ diagonal estimate is correct and route-specific.** The diagonal is $D(\sum|\alpha_h|)^2\asymp D\log^2H_D$, acceptable at $D\le X^{1/2}$. The cost is that $C_h\overline{C_h}=|C_h|^2$ erases $\chi_4(h)$ and leaves only odd $h$.

3. **A2's Q1-Spectral and H12 statements are correct in bounded scope.** If a character enters only through diagonal unitary conjugation $U=\operatorname{diag}(\chi_4(d))$, then $\|U^*KU\|_{\rm op}=\|K\|_{\rm op}$; pure cyclic trace statistics are similarly blind. These diagnostics block operator-norm-only, Frobenius, Schur/Gershgorin, absolute-majorant, and pure cyclic-trace arguments after such a reduction, but not direct signed bilinear forms, open-path moments, CRI, or non-conjugacy traces.

4. **A3's coefficient conjugacy and $\Phi$ asymmetry checks are correct.** The Vaaler coefficient satisfies

$$
\alpha_{-h,H}=\overline{\alpha_{h,H}},
$$

and numerical implementations should not assume $\Phi(1-u)=\Phi(u)$.

5. **The Li--Yang black-box endpoint non-import remains correct.** The arXiv page verifies the paper's title, authors, v2 date, DOI, and abstract mechanism: Bombieri--Iwaniec, a new first-spacing estimate, and Huxley's second-spacing results. The local TeX/source audit still indicates that the published theorem reaches $\theta^*=0.314483\ldots$, not $1/4$, so it does not cover the endpoint height $H_D\asymp DX^{-1/4}$ as a black box.

6. **Vaaler metadata are externally verified.** Project Euclid/AMS metadata identify Jeffrey D. Vaaler, "Some extremal functions in Fourier analysis," *Bull. Amer. Math. Soc.* 12(2):183--216, April 1985. The theorem-level notation translation should still be verified against the rendered uploaded PDF in the proof draft.

## Claims that need proof

1. **A2's fourth-moment route needs a full exact-resonance taxonomy.** The pair-swapped, semi-diagonal, and denominator-paired families are only selected subfamilies. A complete proof needs to classify all solutions of

$$
\frac{h_1}{d_1}-\frac{h_2}{d_2}
+\frac{h_3}{d_3}-\frac{h_4}{d_4}=0
$$

under dyadic restrictions, odd-frequency restrictions, signs of the $h_i$, zero-frequency exclusions, and actual Vaaler weights.

2. **A2's denominator-paired estimate needs endpoint repair.** The parameterization

$$
h_1-h_2=ac,\qquad h_3-h_4=-bc
$$

creates sums with denominators such as

$$
\frac{1}{|h_1|\,|h_1-ac|\,|h_3|\,|h_3+bc|}.
$$

The draft suppresses the cases where $h_1=ac$, $h_3=-bc$, or where these values lie near the truncation boundary. Those singular or near-singular cases need to be separated before the estimate can be promoted.

3. **Near-collision bounds are the main unresolved part.** The fourth-moment target is not just exact diagonal classification. One also needs to bound near-resonances after clearing denominators, with phase distance on the relevant scale. This is the likely analytic bottleneck of the fourth-moment route.

4. **CRI is not yet a sufficient theorem.** A bound on $\Sigma_1\overline{\Sigma_3}$ alone is not enough; the relevant quantity is

$$
|\mathcal M_2|^2
=
64\left(|\Sigma_1|^2+|\Sigma_3|^2
-2\Re(\Sigma_1\overline{\Sigma_3})\right).
$$

The required result is that this whole combination is $\ll X^{1/2+\epsilon}$, not merely that the cross term is nonzero.

5. **A3's source-location claims still need final rendered-page transcription.** The metadata for Vaaler are verified externally, but final proof text should quote Theorem 6, Section 7 definitions, and Theorem 18 directly from the PDF page images, including coefficient sign and residual constant.

6. **A3's numerical claims need reproducible tables.** Symbolic checks and tiny examples are useful for debugging, but they are not endpoint evidence. R5, M9, CRI, BSOS, and M2 kernel checks need exact scripts or tables at $X=10^6,10^8$ or similarly informative scales.

## Possible errors or hidden assumptions

1. **A3's statement that A2 had not supplied exact fourth-moment formulas is stale.** In the reviewed Round 23 bundle, A2 does supply a fourth-moment formula and a calibration addendum. A3's next pass should use A2's formula rather than deferring the classification task.

2. **A2's "smooth dependence on $h$" rationale for CRI is weak at the endpoint.** The phase increment from $h$ to $h+2$ is

$$
\frac{X}{2d},
$$

which is $\asymp X^{1/2}$ when $D\asymp X^{1/2}$ and therefore wraps many times. Correlation between $\Sigma_1$ and $\Sigma_3$ is possible, but it is arithmetic, not a simple smoothness consequence. The CRI ratio should decide this numerically before it becomes a serious analytic target.

3. **Two-sided versus one-sided $h$ conventions need to be frozen.** A2's M2 kernels are sometimes written with $1\le h\le H_D$ and sometimes with $1\le |h|\le H_D$. For fourth moments and CRI, this affects conjugations, real/imaginary recombinations, and constants. Constants do not affect exponents, but sign placement does.

4. **Fourth-root extraction needs to track what $\mathcal Q_4$ actually denotes.** If $\mathcal Q_4=|\sum_h\alpha_h C_hB_h|^4$, the implication to $\mathcal M_2$ is immediate up to the prefactor $4^4$. If $\mathcal Q_4$ is a selected signed sub-expansion or truncated family, it is only a diagnostic.

5. **A3's toy R5 numerical value should not be used without recomputation.** The output contains only rough hand/symbolic examples, and small previous R5 examples in this project have had normalization errors. The next round should compute R5 from the raw Fejer definition with exact resonance detection, not quote hand estimates.

6. **The Li--Yang Case A condition containing $M<T^{-7/16}$ remains suspicious.** The local TeX may indeed contain that sign, but for large $T$ and $M\ge1$ it is effectively vacuous. The endpoint non-import conclusion does not depend on this lower-bound branch; it follows already from the upper restriction $H\le MT^{-49/164}$ and the final $\theta^*$ height. Still, the final source map should quote the typeset statement exactly and mark the unusual branch as likely typographical or vacuous.
