## Possible errors or hidden assumptions

1. **A2's weighted normalisation diagonal missing constant factor.** The weighted normalisation uses
   $$|\mathcal M_2|^2\le 16 L \sum_h |\alpha_h|\,|C_h|^2|B_h|^2.$$
   Expanding $|B_h|^2$ and taking $d_1=d_2$, the inner factor $|C_h|^2=4$; the sum over $h$ of $|\alpha_h|$ is $L\asymp\log H_D$. Multiplying by $L$ (the outer scalar) gives $L^2\asymp\log^2 H_D$, not $L$. A1's earlier draft had $\log^2$; A2's statement "$\mathcal S_{\rm diag}^{({\rm weighted})}\asymp D\log^2 H_D$" is correct. The ledger entry claims "proved" but the intermediate step of writing $L^2$ could be misreported; the final scaling is nevertheless harmless.

2. **A2's fourth-moment product of $C_h$ factors may omit even $h$.** The raw definition of $\mathcal M_2$ includes $C_h$, which is zero for even $h$. A2's fourth-moment expression retains $C_h$ factors; the terms with any even $h$ vanish automatically because $C_h=0$. This is mathematically fine but may inflate the apparent number of terms in a counting argument; it should be stated that the sum over $h_i$ is effectively over odd $h_i$.

3. **A2's CRI split uses only odd $h$; even $h$ automatically excluded because $C_h=0$. The $\Sigma_r$ sums are over $h\equiv r\pmod4$, which includes even residues? A2 wrote $h\equiv r\pmod4$ with $r\in\{1,3\}$; that's fine, only odd $h$ contribute because $C_h$ would be zero for even $h$, but the definition of $\Sigma_r$ itself does not include $C_h$; it's derived from $\mathcal M_2$ which has $C_h$, so the sum over $h$ in $\mathcal M_2$ includes only odd $h$. A2's definition of $\Sigma_r$ should be restricted to odd $h$ or should incorporate $\chi_4(h)$ automatically; but it uses $\alpha_h$ without $\chi_4$, and the later use in $\mathcal M_2=8i(\Sigma_1-\Sigma_3)$ requires summing over all $h$ (odd) with the appropriate $\chi_4$ factor. Actually if $\Sigma_r$ sums over all $h\equiv r\pmod4$ (including even $h$), then $h\equiv1\pmod4$ includes $1,5,9,\dots$ which are odd, and $h\equiv3\pmod4$ are odd; even residues $0,2$ are not included, so it's fine. So no hidden error.

4. **A2's denominator-paired count may assume $h_i>0$ without handling negative $h$ explicitly.** The Vaaler sums run over both positive and negative frequencies. The fourth-moment expansion should be over signed $h_i$. The parameterisation $h_1-h_2=ac$ etc. may need to account for sign choices and the absolute values in the coefficient weights. A2's local audit addendum notes this and asks for explicit handling; therefore the claim remains provisional.

5. **R5-Full's shifted-leg cap uses $W_{C\Delta}$ with an absolute constant $C$.** Using $W_\Delta$ with a scaled argument is harmless, but the exact constant in the bound may affect the final estimate by a fixed factor, not an exponent. This is permissible.
