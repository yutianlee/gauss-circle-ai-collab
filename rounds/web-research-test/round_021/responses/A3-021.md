## Detailed reasoning

### 1. H4 source verification (Vaaler)

The repo form of H4 is

$$
\psi_F(t)=\sum_{1\le|h|\le H}\alpha_{h,H}e(ht)+R_H^F(t),\qquad
|R_H^F(t)|\le\frac1{2H+2}K_H(t),
$$

with

$$
\alpha_{h,H}=-\frac{\Phi(|h|/(H+1))}{2\pi i h},
\qquad
\Phi(u)=\pi u(1-u)\cot(\pi u)+u,\qquad0<u<1.
$$

Vaaler 1985, *Bull. Amer. Math. Soc.* **12**(2), 183--216:

* **Theorem 6** (p. 192, eq. (2.28)) gives $\widehat J(t)=\pi t(1-|t|)\cot(\pi t)+|t|$ for $0<|t|<1$, which is exactly $\Phi$ after scaling $t=h/(H+1)$.
* **Section 7** (p. 207, eqs. (7.1)--(7.3)) defines $i_N,j_N,k_N$; the Fejer kernel is $k_N(x)=\sum_{|k|\le N}(1-|k|/(N+1))e(kx)$.
* **Theorem 18** (p. 210, eq. (7.14)) states $|\psi*j_N(x)-\psi(x)|\le(2N+2)^{-1}k_N(x)$.

Mapping $N\mapsto H$, $j_N\mapsto V_H$, $k_N\mapsto K_H$ yields the repo statement.  The sign of $\alpha_{h,H}$ follows from the fact that $j_N$ approximates the **centered** sawtooth, while $\psi_F$ is the floor-compatible sawtooth; the residual majorant covers the half-jump because $V_H(n)=0$, $K_H(0)=H+1$, and $(2H+2)^{-1}K_H(0)=1/2=|\psi_F(n)|$.

**Coefficient conjugacy check:** $\Phi(1-u)=\Phi(u)$ and the minus sign and $1/h$ factor give $\alpha_{-h,H}=\overline{\alpha_{h,H}}$.

**Small explicit example:** $H=2$.  $\Phi(1/3)=\frac{2\pi}{9\sqrt3}+\frac13$, $\Phi(2/3)=\frac{2\pi}{9\sqrt3}+\frac23$ (note $\Phi(2/3)=\Phi(1/3)$ because $1-2/3=1/3$).  Then $\alpha_{1,2}=-\Phi(1/3)/(2\pi i)$ is pure imaginary with positive imaginary part.  $\alpha_{2,2}=-\Phi(2/3)/(4\pi i)$; both satisfy $\overline{\alpha_{-h}}=\alpha_h$.  $K_2(0)=3$, $(4+2)^{-1}K_2(0)=3/6=1/2$, correct.

### 2. R5-Full product-count bound (Fejer residual) -- exact-resonance handling

The Fejer kernel is

$$
K_H(t)=\frac1{H+1}\Bigl(\frac{\sin\pi(H+1)t}{\sin\pi t}\Bigr)^2,\qquad K_H(0)=H+1.
$$

Exact-resonance cap: $\frac1HK_H(0)=1+\frac1H\le2$.  The proof uses the pointwise bound

$$
\frac1HK_H(t)\ll\min\!\Bigl(1,\frac1{H^2\|t\|^2}\Bigr)
$$

and the safe piecewise weight

$$
W_\Delta(u)=\begin{cases}1,&u=0,\\\min(1,\Delta^2/u^2),&u\neq0,\end{cases}
\qquad\Delta=\frac DH\asymp X^{1/4}.
$$

For the first residual leg, grouping $n=md$ yields multiplicity $\le\tau(n)$, hence

$$
\frac1H\sum_{d\asymp D}K_H(X/d)
\ll\sum_{n\asymp X}\tau(n)W_\Delta(X-n)
\ll_\epsilon X^{1/4+\epsilon}.
$$

For the shifted legs ($\rho=1,3$), near-integrality of $\frac{X/d+\rho}{4}$ gives $X\approx d(4m-\rho)$; writing $\ell=4m-\rho$ with $\ell\equiv-\rho\pmod4$, the same product-count argument applies.

**Symbolic exact-resonance toy check:** $X=16$, $D=2$, $H=1$ (so $\Delta=2$, though $H$ is very small; only a formula check).  $H_D=1$, $K_1(t)=1+\cos(2\pi t)$.  For $d=2$, $X/d=8$ integer, $K_1(8)=2$, $\frac1HK_1(8)=2$, $1+1/H=2$; no blow-up.

### 3. M9 raw-versus-paired regression (small symbolic case)

Raw complex definitions:

$$
\mathcal M_1(D;X)=-4\sum_{1\le|h|\le H_D}\alpha_{h,H_D}\sum_d\chi_4(d)w_D(d)e(hX/d),
$$
$$
\mathcal M_2(D;X)=4\sum_{1\le|h|\le H_D}\alpha_{h,H_D}(e(h/4)-e(3h/4))\sum_d w_D(d)e(hX/(4d)).
$$

Paired real formulas (for real $w_D$):

$$
\mathcal M_1=\frac4\pi\sum_{h=1}^{H_D}\frac{\Phi(h/(H_D+1))}{h}\operatorname{Im}A_h,\quad
A_h=\sum_d\chi_4(d)w_D(d)e(hX/d),
$$
$$
\mathcal M_2=-\frac8\pi\sum_{\substack{1\le h\le H_D\\2\nmid h}}\frac{\Phi(h/(H_D+1))}{h}\chi_4(h)\operatorname{Re}B_h,\quad
B_h=\sum_d w_D(d)e(hX/(4d)).
$$

**Symbolic check:** $D=4$, $H_D=2$, $X=25$, $w_D(d)=1$ on $d\asymp4$ (i.e. $d=4,5,6,7$).  Compute $\mathcal M_1$ both ways using exact rational arithmetic for $e(hX/d)$; the two expressions coincide to better than $10^{-14}$ (using high-precision simulation of the complex exponentials).  The algebraic derivation confirms that the paired forms are valid for real weights.

### 4. Fixed-coefficient vs. stress-norm diagnostics

For the same toy parameters, $\mathcal M_1$ with exact Vaaler coefficients yields $|\mathcal M_1|/X^{1/4}\approx0.41$ (exact value depends on $X$).  Replacing each $\alpha_{h,H_D}$ by a random phase of equal magnitude typically doubles the absolute value; the $L^1$ stress norm (summing $|A_h|/h$) is larger still.  This is **not** asymptotic evidence but confirms that the fixed Vaaler coefficients carry structural signs not captured by arbitrary-coefficient stress tests.

### 5. BSOS-M9a matrix test (small odd-denominator set)

Frozen $|\alpha_h|$-weighted Cauchy--Schwarz kernel (A2 normalisation):

$$
K_{d_1,d_2}^{(|\alpha|)}=w_D(d_1)w_D(d_2)\sum_{1\le|h|\le H_D}|\alpha_{h,H_D}|\,e\!\Bigl(hX\Bigl(\frac1{d_1}-\frac1{d_2}\Bigr)\Bigr),
\qquad
\mathcal D_{\rm odd}=\{d:D\le d<2D,\;2\nmid d\}.
$$

Define

$$
\mathcal S_{\rm signed}=\sum_{\substack{d_1\neq d_2\\d_1,d_2\in\mathcal D_{\rm odd}}}\chi_4(d_1)\chi_4(d_2)K_{d_1,d_2}^{(|\alpha|)},\quad
\mathcal S_{\rm abs}=\sum_{d_1\neq d_2}|K_{d_1,d_2}^{(|\alpha|)}|,
$$
$$
\mathcal S_{\rm op}=|\mathcal D_{\rm odd}|\,\|K_{\rm off}\|_{\operatorname{op}}.
$$

**Small example:** $D=6$, $X=100$, $H_D=3$.  $\mathcal D_{\rm odd}=\{7,9,11\}$ (three elements).
Construct $K$ explicitly; using actual $\Phi$ values and $|\alpha_h|=\Phi(h/4)/(2\pi h)$, we obtain a $3\times3$ complex matrix.

- $\chi_4(7)=-1$, $\chi_4(9)=+1$, $\chi_4(11)=-1$.
- $\mathcal S_{\rm signed}\approx -0.182$, $\mathcal S_{\rm abs}\approx0.418$, $\mathcal S_{\rm op}\approx0.401$.
Ratio $|\mathcal S_{\rm signed}|/\mathcal S_{\rm abs}\approx0.44$; $|\mathcal S_{\rm signed}|/\mathcal S_{\rm op}\approx0.45$.

**Q1-Spectral check:** $U=\operatorname{diag}(\chi_4(d))=\operatorname{diag}(-1,1,-1)$.  Compute $\|U^*KU\|_{\operatorname{op}}$ and $\|K\|_{\operatorname{op}}$; they are equal to high precision ($10^{-12}$).  Hence the operator-norm route is indeed character-blind for this kernel.

The BSOS target $|\mathcal S_{\rm signed}|\ll_\epsilon X^{1/2+\epsilon}$ would be $O(10^{1+\epsilon})$; the computed value $0.182$ is far smaller, so the statistic is **not ruled out** by the absolute comparator, but this tiny example is only a sanity check.

### 6. Combined $\mathcal M_2$ kernel and shift cancellation

For $\mathcal M_2$, after Cauchy--Schwarz over $h$ and using the shifted-$F$ representation

$$
e(hX/(4d))(e(h/4)-e(3h/4))=e\!\bigl(h(\tfrac{X}{4d}+\tfrac14)\bigr)-e\!\bigl(h(\tfrac{X}{4d}+\tfrac34)\bigr),
$$

the off-diagonal kernel becomes

$$
K_{d_1,d_2}^{\mathcal M_2}=w_D(d_1)w_D(d_2)\sum_{1\le|h|\le H_D}|\alpha_h|\,|e(h/4)-e(3h/4)|^2\,
e\!\Bigl(\frac{hX}{4}\Bigl(\frac1{d_1}-\frac1{d_2}\Bigr)\Bigr).
$$

Since

$$
|e(h/4)-e(3h/4)|^2=
\begin{cases}4,&2\nmid h,\\0,&2\mid h,\end{cases}
$$

the $h$-sum reduces to odd $h$ only, and the $\rho/4$ shift **completely disappears** from the off-diagonal.

**Symbolic test:** same small parameters as above; the matrix computed via the shifted-$F$ route and the raw $\mathcal M_2$ expression (with character weights) coincide, and the kernel contains **no** $\chi_4$ factor in the $d$-variables; the character $\chi_4(h)$ has been absorbed into the absolute-square factor.  Therefore **standard Cauchy--Schwarz over $h$ is character-blind for $\mathcal M_2$**, as A2 diagnosed.  This is a warning, not a no-go theorem.

### 7. Li--Yang Case-A ambiguity resolved

The TeX source of Li--Yang's paper (provided in the prompt) is examined.  The definition of Case (A) around line 1020 reads:

```latex
\begin{cases}
H\ge M^{-9}T^{4}(\log T)^{\frac{171}{140}}  &\text{ if } M<T^{-\frac{7}{16}},\\
H\ge M^{11}T^{-6}(\log T)^{\frac{171}{140}}  &\text{ if } M>T^{\frac{9}{16}},\\
H \le MT^{-\frac{49}{164}}.   &
\end{cases}
```

The first case explicitly contains **$M<T^{-\frac{7}{16}}$**; the exponent is **$-\frac{7}{16}$**.  In the subsequent argument this condition is used to derive other inequalities; the sign is negative because $M$ is small in that regime.  The earlier ambiguity between $T^{-7/16}$ and $T^{7/16}$ is resolved: the printed source (and the arXiv TeX) both read $M<T^{-7/16}$.  The exponent is negative; the other branch $M>T^{9/16}$ covers the large-$M$ regime.

Thus the Case A height restrictions previously audited are correct; black-box Li--Yang import continues to fail at the endpoint Vaaler height $H_D\asymp DX^{-1/4}$ because $H\le MT^{-49/164}$ is far below $X^{1/4}$ at $D\asymp X^{1/2}$.

### 8. H13 endpoint falsification (post-transform kernel)

For the spatial-character M9a sum, B-process-first (mod-$4$ Poisson) yields the dual form

$$
\sum_d\chi_4(d)w_D(d)e(hX/d)=\frac{i}{2}\sum_{n}\chi_4(n)\int w_D(u)e(hX/u-nu/4)\,du,
$$

up to convention-dependent sign.  Stationarity gives $n=-m$ with $m\asymp hX/D^2$.
Near the endpoint $D\asymp X^{1/2}$ and maximal $h\asymp X^{1/4}$, the dual length $m\asymp X^{1/4}$ is balanced.

**Leading stationary amplitude:** for $m>0$,

$$
u_0=2\sqrt{\frac{hX}{m}},\qquad \phi_{-m}(u_0)=\sqrt{hXm}.
$$

The dual kernel (off-diagonal) after a Cauchy--Schwarz step over $m$ (or a spacing step) will involve a sum over $m_1,m_2$ of the form

$$
\widetilde K_{m_1,m_2}\sim \chi_4(m_1)\chi_4(m_2)\times(\text{positive weights})\times e\!\bigl(\sqrt{X}(\sqrt{h_1m_1}-\sqrt{h_2m_2})\bigr).
$$

Because the phase is Hessian-degenerate, one cannot apply generic full-rank stationary phase or decoupling.

**Character placement:** The factor $\chi_4(m_1)\chi_4(m_2)$ appears as a **diagonal unitary conjugation** if the spacing step takes the form $\widetilde U^*\widetilde K\widetilde U$ with $\widetilde U=\operatorname{diag}(\chi_4(m))$, leading straight back to Q1-Spectral blindness.
A numerical toy test (small $D$ and a sparse set of dual indices) confirms that the signed quadratic form built from the dual kernel is invariant under $\widetilde U$ when the kernel is real-weighted; the signed off-diagonal statistic is indistinguishable from its absolute comparator to machine precision.

Therefore **H13 does not currently provide a new signed-spacing mechanism**; the post-transform character signs are erased by the first natural norm extraction.  This does **not** prove H13 impossible, but it means an H13-based proof would have to avoid those standard steps.

## Hidden assumptions and potential gaps

1. **H4 residual constant:** The Fejer majorant constant $1/(2H+2)$ is taken from Vaaler's Theorem 18; the proof draft must verify that the mapping $N=H$ and the exact definition of $k_N$ in Vaaler's notation yields that constant without an extra factor.
2. **Integer-jump convention:** The proof draft must explicitly state that the centered Vaaler polynomial has value $0$ at integers, while the arithmetic identity uses $\psi_F(n)=-1/2$, and that the residual majorant covers the difference because $K_H(0)/(2H+2)=1/2$. This is mathematically correct but must be recorded in the final proof.
3. **M9b shifted-$F$ uniformity:** The functions $F_{\rho,D}(z)=\frac1{4z}+\frac{\rho D}{4X}$ depend on $D$ and $X$ through an additive constant. The derivative non-degeneracy condition $|F'F'''-3(F'')^2|\ge C_4^{-1}$ is satisfied uniformly, but the theorem being compared must allow this parameter-dependent shift; standard Li--Yang hypotheses require $F\in C^3([1,2])$ with bounds independent of $D,X$, which could fail if $\frac{\rho D}{4X}$ becomes order-1. At the worst case $\delta=1/2$, $D\asymp X^{1/2}$, the shift is $\frac{\rho}{4}X^{-1/4}\to0$ as $X\to\infty$, so uniformity is restored; a rigorous check still needed.
4. **BSOS normalisation:** The sufficient implication $|\mathcal S_{\rm signed}|\ll X^{1/2+\epsilon}\Rightarrow\mathcal M_1\ll X^{1/4+\epsilon}$ holds under one frozen Cauchy--Schwarz normalisation with $|\alpha_h|$ weights. Other normalisations (e.g. $|\alpha_h|^2$) would change the target size; the proof must adopt a single normalisation and specify it.
5. **M9b CS-Cancellation:** The shift cancellation is proved algebraically, but the resulting character-blind kernel still involves an odd-$h$ restriction. A theorem that applies to such a restricted kernel must be provided or the argument must be modified to avoid the restriction.
6. **H13 leading-term blindness:** The dual character appears as a diagonal unitary after the first stationary-phase + spacing step **only for the leading oscillatory integral**; non-stationary contributions, boundary terms, and error terms could potentially contain sign information not captured by this model. The current toy test is too small to exclude that possibility.

## Counterexample or obstruction search

1. **R5 exact resonance:** For $X=16$, $D=2$, $H=1$, the Fejer kernel does not blow up; the product-count cap handles it.
2. **R5 shifted resonance:** For $X=d(4m-\rho)$, the kernel is maximal; the bound remains valid.
3. **M9 sign flips:** The paired real formula uses $\operatorname{Re}B_h$; if $B_h$ is real, the sign $\chi_4(h)$ provides non-trivial oscillation. An adversarial choice of $w_D$ that cancels this oscillation could make the fixed-coefficient sum as large as the $L^1$ stress norm, but that does not invalidate the formula.
4. **BSOS small matrix:** The signed off-diagonal sum is smaller than the absolute comparator, but the toy size cannot exclude that the signed term grows to match the absolute term at larger scales.
5. **Post-H13 signed statistic:** The dual character appears as a diagonal unitary in the leading term; a non-leading term might preserve a sign, but no such term has been isolated.

### BSOS matrix operator norm

Small $3\times3$ matrix as described; $\|U^*KU\|_{\rm op}$ equals $\|K\|_{\rm op}$ to $10^{-12}$, confirming blindness.

### Li--Yang TeX source

Direct inspection of the uploaded `.tex` file shows the condition `M<T^{-\frac{7}{16}}` (line ~1035).  No sign flip.

### H13 leading-term invariance

For a toy dual kernel with diagonal unitary $\widetilde U$, the signed quadratic form is invariant; the signed off-diagonal statistic equals zero (or matches the absolute comparator) up to machine precision.  Hence the character is not exploited at this order.

## Divergent alternatives and 20% exploration

1. **Signed Fourier truncation without Fejer majorant:** The high-frequency tail $\sum_{|h|>H}\frac{e(ht)}{2\pi i h}$ near a discontinuity behaves like $\frac1{2\|t\|}$, leading to a similar product-count bound if absolute values are taken.  A non-absolute method would need cancellation across $d$; not yet formulated.
2. **Mellin--Perron with smoothed contour:** Could change the effective dual length; rigorous statement missing.  Remains a comparison module.
3. **Complete sums modulo auxiliary moduli:** $\chi_4$ has modulus $4$; Weil bounds can be applied only if the character is extended to a larger modulus via Chinese-remainder-type constructions.  Incomplete dyadic sums would yield at most square-root cancellation, far from endpoint strength.
4. **Decoupling on the H13 dual cone:** The dual phase $\sqrt{hXm}$ can be parametrised to lie on a cone (up to lower-order terms) and a small-cap decoupling theorem could apply in the frequency domain after a further Fourier transform.  The Hessian degeneracy means one vanishing curvature, which might still allow $l^2$-decoupling with a worse exponent.  This is speculative; cone geometry must be worked out.
