## Detailed reasoning

### Exact M2 Kernel Derivation

We begin by deriving the exact combined post-Cauchy--Schwarz kernel for the second main term, $\mathcal M_2(D;X)$. The raw definition of the sum is:
$$
\mathcal M_2(D;X)
=
4\sum_{1\le |h|\le H_D}
\alpha_{h,H_D}
\left(e(h/4)-e(3h/4)\right)
\sum_{d\asymp D}
w_D(d)e(hX/(4d)),
$$
where the fixed Vaaler coefficients are given by:
$$
\alpha_{h,H_D}
=
-\frac{\Phi(|h|/(H_D+1))}{2\pi i h},
\qquad
\Phi(u)=\pi u(1-u)\cot(\pi u)+u.
$$
To apply a weighted Cauchy--Schwarz inequality over the frequency variable $h$, we isolate the $h$-dependent coefficients. Let $c_h = \alpha_{h,H_D} \left(e(h/4)-e(3h/4)\right)$. We observe that $e(h/4)-e(3h/4) = 2i\sin(\pi h/2) = 2i\chi_4(h)$. Therefore, the magnitude squared of this factor is:
$$
|e(h/4)-e(3h/4)|^2
=
\begin{cases}
4, & 2\nmid h,\\
0, & 2\mid h.
\end{cases}
$$
We apply the Cauchy--Schwarz inequality using the weights $|\alpha_{h,H_D}|$. Squaring the absolute value of $\mathcal M_2(D;X)$ yields:
$$
|\mathcal M_2(D;X)|^2
\le
16 \left( \sum_{1\le |h|\le H_D} |\alpha_{h,H_D}| |e(h/4)-e(3h/4)|^2 \right)
\times
\sum_{1\le |h|\le H_D} |\alpha_{h,H_D}| \left| \sum_{d\asymp D} w_D(d)e(hX/(4d)) \right|^2.
$$
Let $L_2$ denote the first factor (the diagonal weight sum). Since the factor $|e(h/4)-e(3h/4)|^2$ restricts the sum to odd $h$ and contributes a factor of $4$, we have:
$$
L_2 = 4 \sum_{\substack{1\le |h|\le H_D \\ 2\nmid h}} |\alpha_{h,H_D}| \asymp \log H_D.
$$
Expanding the square in the second factor over variables $d_1, d_2 \asymp D$, we obtain the exact combined kernel for $\mathcal M_2$:
$$
K_{d_1,d_2}^{(\mathcal M_2)}
=
w_D(d_1)w_D(d_2)
\sum_{1\le |h|\le H_D}
|\alpha_{h,H_D}|
|e(h/4)-e(3h/4)|^2
e\left(\frac{hX}{4}\left(\frac1{d_1}-\frac1{d_2}\right)\right).
$$
The diagonal contribution occurs when $d_1 = d_2$, yielding:
$$
\mathcal S_{\rm diag}^{(2)}
=
\sum_{d\asymp D} w_D(d)^2 \sum_{1\le |h|\le H_D} |\alpha_{h,H_D}| |e(h/4)-e(3h/4)|^2
\asymp
D \log H_D.
$$
The exact off-diagonal statistic is:
$$
\mathcal S_{\rm off}^{(2)}
=
\sum_{\substack{d_1, d_2 \asymp D \\ d_1 \ne d_2}} K_{d_1,d_2}^{(\mathcal M_2)}.
$$
To imply the required endpoint-strength target $\mathcal M_2(D;X) \ll_\epsilon X^{1/4+\epsilon}$, the squared sum must satisfy $|\mathcal M_2(D;X)|^2 \ll_\epsilon X^{1/2+2\epsilon}$. Since $L_2 \asymp \log H_D$, the required target size for the off-diagonal statistic is:
$$
|\mathcal S_{\rm off}^{(2)}| \ll_\epsilon X^{1/2+\epsilon}.
$$
The diagonal contribution $D \log H_D \ll X^{1/2} \log X$ is strictly bounded by this target, confirming that the diagonal does not blow up under Cauchy--Schwarz over $h$.

Consequently, the sign of $\chi_4(h)$ no longer appears in the kernel; only the odd-$h$ support remains. The resulting off-diagonal statistic $\mathcal S_{\rm off}^{(2)}$ is an untwisted, real-weighted reciprocal sum restricted to odd frequencies. ### Non-Conjugacy Statistic for M2

To preserve information lost by the combined character-blind kernel $K_{d_1,d_2}^{(\mathcal M_2)}$, one natural option is to avoid placing absolute values over the frequency variable $h$. We propose a Cross-Residue Interference (CRI) statistic for $\mathcal M_2$.

We split the frequency sum into residue classes modulo 4. Let:
$$
\mathcal M_{2,1}(D;X) = 8i \sum_{\substack{1\le |h|\le H_D \\ h \equiv 1 \bmod 4}} \alpha_{h,H_D} \sum_{d\asymp D} w_D(d)e(hX/(4d)),
$$
$$
\mathcal M_{2,3}(D;X) = -8i \sum_{\substack{1\le |h|\le H_D \\ h \equiv 3 \bmod 4}} \alpha_{h,H_D} \sum_{d\asymp D} w_D(d)e(hX/(4d)).
$$
Thus, $\mathcal M_2(D;X) = \mathcal M_{2,1}(D;X) + \mathcal M_{2,3}(D;X)$.
Squaring the sum directly without Cauchy--Schwarz over $h$ yields:
$$
|\mathcal M_2|^2 \le 2|\mathcal M_{2,1}|^2 + 2|\mathcal M_{2,3}|^2 + 2\Re\left( \mathcal M_{2,1} \overline{\mathcal M_{2,3}} \right).
$$
We define the finite non-conjugacy statistic as the cross-term:
$$
\mathcal S_{\rm CRI}^{(2)} = \mathcal M_{2,1}(D;X) \overline{\mathcal M_{2,3}(D;X)}.
$$
Expanding this statistic:
$$
\mathcal S_{\rm CRI}^{(2)} = 64 \sum_{\substack{h_1 \equiv 1 \bmod 4 \\ h_2 \equiv 3 \bmod 4}} \alpha_{h_1,H_D} \overline{\alpha_{h_2,H_D}} \sum_{d_1, d_2 \asymp D} w_D(d_1)w_D(d_2) e\left( \frac{X}{4} \left( \frac{h_1}{d_1} - \frac{h_2}{d_2} \right) \right).
$$
**Target Inequality:** To imply the M9b bound, we require $|\mathcal S_{\rm CRI}^{(2)}| \ll_\epsilon X^{1/2+\epsilon}$, alongside corresponding bounds for the pure-residue squares $|\mathcal M_{2,1}|^2$ and $|\mathcal M_{2,3}|^2$.
**Falsification Test:** Compute $\mathcal S_{\rm CRI}^{(2)}$ numerically for $D \asymp X^{1/4}, X^{3/8}, X^{1/2}$. Compare it to the absolute majorant where the phase is replaced by $1$. If $|\mathcal S_{\rm CRI}^{(2)}|$ does not exhibit cancellation relative to the absolute majorant, the cross-residue interference does not provide a signed advantage, and the statistic is falsified.

### Q1-Spectral and H12 Bounded Scope

We formalize the bounded scope of the character-blindness diagnostics.

**Lemma Q1-Spectral-R21 (Bounded Scope):**
Let $\mathcal V_D = \ell^2(\mathcal D_{\rm odd})$ be the finite vector space over odd denominators $D \le d < 2D$. Let $U = \operatorname{diag}(\chi_4(d))$ be the diagonal unitary involution on $\mathcal V_D$. For any spacing matrix $K$ acting on $\mathcal V_D$, the operator norm is unitarily invariant:
$$
\|U^*KU\|_{\operatorname{op}} = \sup_{\|v\|=1} \|U^*KU v\| = \sup_{\|w\|=1} \|K w\| = \|K\|_{\operatorname{op}},
$$
where $w = Uv$ and $\|w\| = \|v\|$ because $U$ is unitary. [PROVED]
*Methods Blocked:* This strictly blocks any proof route that factors the spatial character into $U^*KU$ and subsequently applies the operator norm, Frobenius norm, spectral radius, Schur/Gershgorin bounds, or absolute-value matrix majorization.
*Methods Not Blocked:* This does **not** block direct signed bilinear forms $v^* K v$ (where $v$ is not optimized over the entire unit sphere), open-path moments, non-conjugacy traces, residue interference statistics (like CRI), or new signed spacing theorems that evaluate the quadratic form directly.

**Lemma H12-Trace-R21 (Bounded Scope):**
For pure cyclic traces of the conjugated matrix $U^*KU$, the cyclic property of the trace yields:
$$
\operatorname{Tr}((U^*KU)^m) = \operatorname{Tr}(U^* K^m U) = \operatorname{Tr}(K^m U U^*) = \operatorname{Tr}(K^m).
$$
[PROVED]
*Methods Blocked:* Pure conjugacy-invariant cyclic trace statistics (e.g., standard fourth-moment trace bounds).
*Methods Not Blocked:* Open-path statistics, cross-residue moments, and non-conjugacy signed forms.
