### 2. Proof-draft insertion: R5-Full-R20

**Lemma R5-Full-R20.** Let $X\ge2$ be real, let

$$
X^{1/4}\le D\le X^{1/2},
$$

and let $H$ be an integer satisfying

$$
H\asymp D X^{-1/4}.
$$

Let $w_D$ be a dyadic weight supported on $d\asymp D$ with $|w_D(d)|\le C_w$. Then, for every $\epsilon>0$,

$$
\frac1H
\sum_{d\asymp D}|w_D(d)|K_H(X/d)
\ll_\epsilon X^{1/4+\epsilon},
$$

and for $\rho\in\{1,3\}$,

$$
\frac1H
\sum_{d\asymp D}|w_D(d)|
K_H\left(\frac{X/d+\rho}{4}\right)
\ll_\epsilon X^{1/4+\epsilon}.
$$

Consequently, assuming H4-R20, the full Fejer residual from both sawtooth legs contributes

$$
\ll_\epsilon X^{1/4+\epsilon}
$$

after dyadic summation.

The proof uses the standard Fejer bound

$$
K_H(t)
\ll
\min\left(H,\frac1{H\|t\|^2}\right),
$$

hence

$$
\frac1H K_H(t)
\ll
\min\left(1,\frac1{H^2\|t\|^2}\right).
$$

Set

$$
\Delta=\frac{D}{H}.
$$

Since $H\asymp D X^{-1/4}$,

$$
\Delta\asymp X^{1/4}.
$$

Use the exact-resonance-safe cap

$$
W_\Delta(u)=
\begin{cases}
1,&u=0,\\
\min\left(1,\frac{\Delta^2}{u^2}\right),&u\ne0.
\end{cases}
$$

For the first residual leg, choose $m=m(d)$ nearest to $X/d$; if there is a tie, choose either nearest integer. If $X/d\in\mathbb Z$, choose $m=X/d$. Since $d\asymp D$,

$$
\left\|\frac Xd\right\|
=
\frac{|X-md|}{d}
\asymp
\frac{|X-md|}{D}.
$$

Thus

$$
\frac1H K_H(X/d)
\ll
W_\Delta(X-md).
$$

Group by

$$
n=md.
$$

For $d\asymp D$ and $m$ nearest to $X/d$, one has $n\asymp X$ up to harmless endpoint enlargement. The number of representations $n=md$ with $d\asymp D$ is at most $\tau(n)$. Therefore

$$
\frac1H\sum_{d\asymp D}|w_D(d)|K_H(X/d)
\ll
\sum_{n\asymp X}\tau(n)W_\Delta(X-n).
$$

Using

$$
\tau(n)\ll_\epsilon n^\epsilon
$$

and dyadic decomposition in $|X-n|$,

$$
\sum_{n\asymp X}\tau(n)W_\Delta(X-n)
\ll_\epsilon
X^\epsilon
\left(
\Delta+1
+
\sum_{j\ge1}2^{-j}\Delta
\right)
\ll_\epsilon X^{1/4+\epsilon}.
$$

For the shifted legs, choose $m$ nearest to $(X/d+\rho)/4$. Then

$$
\left\|\frac{X/d+\rho}{4}\right\|
=
\frac{|X-d(4m-\rho)|}{4d}.
$$

Put

$$
\ell=4m-\rho.
$$

Then

$$
\ell\equiv-\rho\pmod4,
$$

and the near-resonance condition becomes

$$
X\approx d\ell.
$$

In the active range and for $X$ sufficiently large, $\ell>0$ and $\ell\asymp X/D$. Bounded small $X$ cases are absorbed into constants. Grouping by $n=d\ell$ again gives divisor multiplicity, and the congruence restriction on $\ell$ only reduces the number of representations.

The zero Fejer mode is included in this positive kernel argument. Separately, it contributes

$$
\frac{D}{H}\asymp X^{1/4}
$$

per active dyadic block, so it is also harmless after logarithmic dyadic summation. Both frequency signs are already contained in the real even kernel $K_H$.

### 7. Q1-Conjugacy-Blindness-R20 and H12-Cyclic-Trace-Blindness-R20

Let

$$
U=\operatorname{diag}(\chi_4(d))
$$

on the finite Hilbert space $\ell^2(\mathcal D_{\rm odd})$. Since $\chi_4(d)^2=1$ for odd $d$, $U$ is unitary and $U^*=U$.

**Q1-Conjugacy-Blindness-R20.** If a method inserts the character only by replacing $K$ with

$$
U^*KU,
$$

and then estimates using a unitarily invariant norm, then

$$
\|U^*KU\|_{\operatorname{op}}=\|K\|_{\operatorname{op}}.
$$

The same blindness holds for singular values, Frobenius norm, spectral radius, Schur/Gershgorin estimates after absolute values, and absolute-value matrix majorants. This is a diagnostic under the stated matrix-reduction hypothesis, not a global obstruction to signed bilinear methods.

**H12-Cyclic-Trace-Blindness-R20.** For pure cyclic trace statistics,

$$
\operatorname{Tr}((U^*KU)^m)=\operatorname{Tr}(K^m).
$$

This blocks only pure conjugacy-invariant cyclic traces. It does not block open-path statistics, non-conjugacy cross-residue forms, or direct signed estimates.

### 10. Comparison modules: Mellin--Perron, signed Fourier, and Bessel smoothing

Mellin--Perron remains a comparison module because the Dirichlet series

$$
4\zeta(s)L(s,\chi_4)
$$

preserves arithmetic before contour manipulation, but the known contour shift and functional equation route reconstructs Voronoi/Hardy/Bessel-type oscillatory sums. No current lemma shows that the truncation error or moment estimates avoid M9-strength input.

Signed Fourier truncation remains a comparison module because it formally preserves $\chi_4$ in the high-frequency tail, but no endpoint-strength signed tail estimate is known. Bounding the tail absolutely recreates stress-norm or $L^1$ difficulties.

Bessel smoothing remains a calibration module because it checks normalizations and recovers classical $R^{2/3}$-scale bounds through smoothing/unsmoothing. It is not currently the main path to the conjectural endpoint in this repo.

6. **Exact resonances.** The cap $W_\Delta(0)=1$ is necessary. Expressions such as $\Delta^2/u^2$ must not be used at $u=0$.

7. **M9 paired formulas.** They require real weights and symmetric positive/negative frequency cutoffs. Complex weights require rederivation.

8. **BSOS normalization.** BSOS-M9a is tied to one frozen $|\alpha_h|$-weighted Cauchy--Schwarz kernel. Using an unweighted, $|\alpha_h|^2$, or simplified $1/h$ kernel changes the statistic.

9. **M9b is not M9a.** The second leg has a different character placement. The combined Cauchy--Schwarz-over-$h$ kernel loses the $\rho/4$ shift and becomes odd-$h$ restricted but character-blind.

10. **Li--Yang range map.** The final $\theta^*$ map is safe. The raw Case A/B table remains pending because of the Case A sign/typographical ambiguity.

11. **Numerical evidence.** Small computations are regression checks only. They do not support asymptotic claims.

12. **H13.** Formal preservation of a dual character does not imply a usable estimate. A standard operator-norm step can erase the dual character.

## Counterexample or obstruction search

1. **Exact Fejer spike obstruction.** If $X/d\in\mathbb Z$, then $K_H(X/d)=H+1$, but

$$
\frac1H K_H(X/d)=1+\frac1H=O(1).
$$

The $W_\Delta(0)=1$ cap handles this. No R5 obstruction appears.

2. **Shifted exact resonance.** If

$$
\frac{X/d+\rho}{4}\in\mathbb Z,
$$

then

$$
X=d(4m-\rho).
$$

The product-count proof sees this as one divisor representation with $\ell\equiv-\rho\pmod4$. No additional multiplicity appears.

3. **Divisor-rich $X$.** Even if $X$ has many divisors, the R5 grouping uses $\tau(n)\ll_\epsilon n^\epsilon$. This absorbs divisor-rich cases at the $X^\epsilon$ level. Numerical stress tests should still include highly composite $X$ to catch implementation errors.

4. **Operator-norm erasure.** If an M9a spacing proof reduces the signed form to $U^*KU$ and then estimates $\|U^*KU\|_{\operatorname{op}}$, the character is erased. This is a proved diagnostic, not a universal obstruction.

5. **M9b standard Cauchy--Schwarz loss.** The combined Cauchy--Schwarz-over-$h$ kernel for $\mathcal M_2$ is character-blind and odd-$h$ restricted. This suggests that a standard divisor-type theorem at endpoint strength would be needed for this route.

6. **H13 Hessian degeneracy.** The dual phase

$$
\sqrt{Xhm}
$$

has zero continuous Hessian determinant, so generic full-rank stationary phase or generic full-rank decoupling does not apply. Round 20 records this guardrail.

7. **Toy computation warning.** A small test at $X=100$, $D=10$, $H=3$ shows the paired formulas agree with the raw formulas but does not indicate asymptotic behavior.

## Divergent alternatives and 20% exploration

### Candidate non-conjugacy statistic for $\mathcal M_2$

The standard Cauchy--Schwarz-over-$h$ route for $\mathcal M_2$ loses the frequency character because $|e(h/4)-e(3h/4)|^2$ is $4$ on odd $h$ and $0$ on even $h$. A possible way to avoid that loss is to pair the $h\equiv1\pmod4$ and $h\equiv3\pmod4$ residues before squaring.

Use the paired real formula:

$$
\mathcal M_2(D;X)
=
-\frac8\pi
\sum_{\substack{1\le h\le H_D\\2\nmid h}}
\frac{\Phi(h/(H_D+1))}{h}
\chi_4(h)\operatorname{Re}B_h(D;X),
$$

where

$$
B_h(D;X)=\sum_d w_D(d)e(hX/(4d)).
$$

Write $h=4q+r$, $r\in\{1,3\}$, and define

$$
c_r(q)=\frac{\Phi((4q+r)/(H_D+1))}{4q+r}.
$$

Then the residue-paired block is

$$
\mathfrak P_2(D;X)
=
\sum_q
\operatorname{Re}
\sum_d
w_D(d)
\left[
c_1(q)e((4q+1)X/(4d))
-
c_3(q)e((4q+3)X/(4d))
\right].
$$

Equivalently,

$$
\mathfrak P_2(D;X)
=
\sum_q
\operatorname{Re}
\sum_d
w_D(d)e(qX/d)
\left[
c_1(q)e(X/(4d))
-
c_3(q)e(3X/(4d))
\right].
$$

This statistic is finite, exact up to endpoint conventions in the $q$ range, and does not pass through $|C_h|^2$. It preserves a residue-pair cancellation mechanism that the standard Cauchy--Schwarz kernel destroys.

A possible target is:

$$
|\mathfrak P_2(D;X)|\ll_\epsilon X^{1/4+\epsilon}
$$

uniformly for active $D$.

This target would imply the $\mathcal M_2$ estimate after restoring the harmless constant $8/\pi$ and endpoint terms. The needed theorem would be an endpoint reciprocal-sum estimate for the paired fractional-frequency phases

$$
e((q+1/4)X/d)
\quad\text{and}\quad
e((q+3/4)X/d)
$$

with the correlated coefficient pair $c_1(q),c_3(q)$.

A quick falsification test is to compute, for endpoint-scale blocks $D\asymp X^{1/2}$,

$$
\mathfrak P_2(D;X),
\qquad
\mathfrak P_{2,\rm abs}(D;X)
=
\sum_q
\left|
\sum_d
w_D(d)e(qX/d)
\left[
c_1(q)e(X/(4d))
-
c_3(q)e(3X/(4d))
\right]
\right|,
$$

and compare the ratio. If the signed statistic tracks the absolute statistic across square, nonsquare, near-square, and divisor-rich $X$, this paired-residue route should be deprioritized.

### H13 endpoint-only falsification note

The H13 transform for the spatial-character sum has the schematic form

$$
\sum_d\chi_4(d)w_D(d)e(hX/d)
=
c\sum_n\chi_4(n)
\int w_D(u)e(hX/u-nu/4)\,du,
$$

with convention-dependent constant $c$. For the active sign $n=-m<0$,

$$
u_0=2\sqrt{\frac{hX}{m}},
\qquad
m\asymp \frac{hX}{D^2},
\qquad
\phi_{-m}(u_0)=\sqrt{hXm}.
$$

At the endpoint

$$
D\asymp X^{1/2},
\qquad
h\asymp H_D\asymp X^{1/4},
$$

the dual length is

$$
m\asymp X^{1/4}.
$$

This is the only naturally balanced H13 range. The immediate falsification test is to build the finite post-H13 kernel with stationary amplitudes and then check whether the dual character appears only as

$$
\widetilde U=\operatorname{diag}(\chi_4(m)).
$$

If the first spacing step replaces the signed form by a norm of $\widetilde U^*\widetilde K\widetilde U$, Q1-Spectral reappears and H13 has not avoided the character-blindness mechanism.
