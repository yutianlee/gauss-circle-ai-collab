## Possible errors or hidden assumptions

The main technical issue in A2's $\mathcal M_2$ derivation is a normalization ambiguity in the Cauchy--Schwarz step. There are two equivalent-looking ways to distribute the factor

$$
g_h=e(h/4)-e(3h/4)
$$

between the two Cauchy--Schwarz factors:

1. Use

$$
\left|\sum_h \alpha_h g_h S_h\right|^2
\le
\left(\sum_h|\alpha_h|\right)
\left(\sum_h|\alpha_h||g_h|^2|S_h|^2\right),
$$

which puts $|g_h|^2$ inside the kernel.

2. Use

$$
\left|\sum_h \alpha_h g_h S_h\right|^2
\le
\left(\sum_h|\alpha_h||g_h|^2\right)
\left(\sum_h|\alpha_h||S_h|^2\right),
$$

which puts $|g_h|^2$ only in the first scalar factor.

A2's displayed inequality and its displayed kernel appear to mix these two conventions. The character-blindness conclusion is robust because both conventions reduce $\chi_4(h)$ to odd-$h$ support, but the exact kernel, diagonal scalar, and off-diagonal statistic should be normalized consistently before A3 implements it. This should be repaired in the next proof-draft update.

A3's most serious algebraic error is the apparent claim that $\Phi(1-u)=\Phi(u)$. With

$$
\Phi(u)=\pi u(1-u)\cot(\pi u)+u,
$$

one has

$$
\Phi(1-u)=1-u-\pi u(1-u)\cot(\pi u),
$$

which is not equal to $\Phi(u)$ in general. For example,

$$
\Phi(1/3)=\frac{2\pi}{9\sqrt3}+\frac13,
\qquad
\Phi(2/3)=-\frac{2\pi}{9\sqrt3}+\frac23.
$$

This does not invalidate the coefficient conjugacy $\alpha_{-h,H}=\overline{\alpha_{h,H}}$, because that conjugacy uses the real value of $\Phi(|h|/(H+1))$ and the sign change in $h$, not symmetry around $1/2$. But it should be corrected before any numerical implementation.

A3's statement that the Li--Yang Case A sign ambiguity is resolved should not be imported into the lemma bank without a line-by-line comparison between the TeX, the typeset PDF, and the surrounding proof logic. The official arXiv page verifies authors, revision, and method, but the internal Case A condition should be audited directly from the source/PDF.

A2's phrasing that certain Cauchy--Schwarz directions are "mathematically obstructed" is acceptable for the displayed normalizations, but not as a global statement. The obstruction is to those specific norm-extraction routes, not to direct bilinear estimates, open-path moments, non-conjugacy traces, or a new signed spacing theorem.

A3's "exact rational/modular arithmetic near Fejer resonances" should be phrased carefully. Resonance detection and congruence logic can be exact, but the complex phases $e(hX/d)$ for general real or rational $X/d$ are not rational objects; numerical phase evaluation still requires high precision.
