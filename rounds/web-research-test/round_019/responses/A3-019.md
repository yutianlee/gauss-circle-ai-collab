## Detailed reasoning

### 1. Vaaler source table: H4 finite approximation
Vaaler's paper *Some extremal functions in Fourier analysis* (Bull. AMS 12 (1985), 183--216) provides:

- **Theorem 6** (p. 193, eq. (2.28)):
  For the entire function $J$ of exponential type,
$$
  \widehat J(t) = \pi t(1-|t|)\cot(\pi t) + |t|,\qquad 0<|t|<1,
$$
  extended continuously with $\widehat J(0)=1$.

- **Theorem 18** (p. 207, around eq. (7.14)):
  Let $j_N$ be the convolution kernel constructed from $J$, and let
$$
  k_N(x) = \sum_{|k|\le N}\Bigl(1-\frac{|k|}{N+1}\Bigr)e(kx)
$$
  be the Fejer kernel. Then the periodic sawtooth
  $\psi(x)=x-\lfloor x\rfloor-\tfrac12$ (Vaaler's convention) satisfies
$$
  |\psi*j_N(x)-\psi(x)| \le \frac{1}{2N+2}k_N(x).
$$

**Translation to repo notation** (with $H=N$, $\alpha_{h,H}= -\frac{\Phi(|h|/(H+1))}{2\pi i h}$, $\Phi(u)=\pi u(1-u)\cot(\pi u)+u$):

| Vaaler object | Repo object | Property |
|---------------|-------------|----------|
| $N$ | $H$ | truncation height |
| $j_N$ | Vaaler polynomial $V_H$ | centered trigonometric polynomial |
| $k_N$ | $K_H(t)=\sum_{\lvert k\rvert\le H}(1-\frac{\lvert k\rvert}{H+1})e(kt)$ | Fejer kernel |
| $\widehat J_{N+1}(h)$ | $\Phi(\lvert h\rvert/(H+1))$ | coefficient function |
| residual inequality $\frac{1}{2N+2}k_N(x)$ | $\frac{1}{2H+2}K_H(t)$ | residual majorant |

**Integer-jump convention**:
Vaaler's polynomial $V_H(n)=0$ for $n\in\mathbb Z$. The floor-compatible sawtooth used in the arithmetic identity is $\psi_F(n)=-\tfrac12$. Because $K_H(0)=H+1$ and $K_H(0)/(2H+2)=1/2$, the residual majorant exactly covers the half-jump. Hence H4 is

$$
\psi_F(t) = \sum_{1\le|h|\le H} \alpha_{h,H} e(ht) + R_H^F(t),\qquad
|R_H^F(t)|\le \frac{1}{2H+2}K_H(t),
$$
with $\alpha_{h,H}= -\frac{\Phi(|h|/(H+1))}{2\pi i h}$ and $\alpha_{-h,H}=\overline{\alpha_{h,H}}$.

The only remaining proof-draft task is to quote the exact page/equation labels in the final `best_proof_draft.md`.

### 2. R5 product-count residual bound
We reformulate R5-Full with the exact-resonance safe kernel $W_\Delta$:

Let $X\ge2$, $X^{1/4}\le D\le X^{1/2}$, $H\asymp D X^{-1/4}$, $\Delta = D/H \asymp X^{1/4}$.
The Fejer kernel satisfies (for all real $t$)
$$
K_H(t) \ll \min\!\Bigl(H,\frac{1}{H\|t\|^2}\Bigr),\qquad
\frac1H K_H(t) \ll \min\!\Bigl(1,\frac{1}{H^2\|t\|^2}\Bigr).
$$

For the **first residual leg**, choose $m$ the nearest integer to $X/d$; then $\|X/d\| = |X-md|/d \asymp |X-md|/D$.
Hence
$$
\frac1H K_H(X/d) \ll W_\Delta(X-md),\qquad
W_\Delta(u)= \begin{cases}
1,&u=0,\\[2pt]
\min\!\bigl(1,\frac{\Delta^2}{u^2}\bigr),&u\neq0.
\end{cases}
$$
Grouping by $n=md$ (at most $\tau(n)$ representations) and using $\tau(n)\ll_\epsilon n^\epsilon$,
$$
\frac1H\sum_{d\asymp D} |w_D(d)| K_H(X/d)
\ll \sum_{n\asymp X} \tau(n) W_\Delta(X-n) \ll_\epsilon X^{1/4+\epsilon},
$$
because the central $|X-n|\le\Delta$ contributes $O(\Delta X^\epsilon)$ and each dyadic annulus $2^{j-1}\Delta<|X-n|\le 2^j\Delta$ contributes $O(2^{-j}\Delta X^\epsilon)$.

For the **shifted second leg**, near-integrality of $\frac{X/d+\rho}{4}$ is equivalent to $X\approx d(4m-\rho)$. Write $\ell=4m-\rho$, so $n=d\ell$ with $\ell\equiv -\rho\pmod4$. The congruence only reduces multiplicity; the same divisor-count argument yields the bound.
The zero Fejer mode ($k=0$) contributes $D/(2H+2)\asymp X^{1/4}$ per dyadic block and is absorbed into the $X^{1/4+\epsilon}$ term. Short blocks $D<X^{1/4}$ are handled trivially by $|\psi_F|\le 1/2$.

Thus **R5-Full** (conditional on H4) is proved, and the Vaaler residual is $\ll_\epsilon X^{1/4+\epsilon}$.

*Note on exact resonance:* When $X/d\in\mathbb Z$, $K_H(X/d)=H+1$, so $\frac1H K_H(X/d)=1+\frac1H\le2$, and $W_\Delta(0)=1$ bounds the term correctly; the product-count argument is unchanged because $W_\Delta(0)$ is finite.

### 3. Paired M9 formulas and regression
With $\alpha_{h,H}= -\frac{\Phi(h/(H+1))}{2\pi i h}$ for $h>0$ and $\alpha_{-h,H}=\overline{\alpha_{h,H}}$, the raw two-sided complex sums are

$$
\mathcal M_1(D;X) = -4\sum_{1\le |h|\le H_D} \alpha_{h,H_D} A_h(D;X),\qquad
A_h(D;X)=\sum_{d\asymp D} \chi_4(d) w_D(d) e(hX/d),
$$
$$
\mathcal M_2(D;X) = 4\sum_{1\le |h|\le H_D} \alpha_{h,H_D} (e(h/4)-e(3h/4)) B_h(D;X),\qquad
B_h(D;X)=\sum_{d\asymp D} w_D(d) e(hX/(4d)).
$$

Using $e(h/4)-e(3h/4)=2i\chi_4(h)$ and $\chi_4(-h)=-\chi_4(h)$, pairing $h$ and $-h$ gives for real dyadic weights $w_D$:

$$
\mathcal M_1(D;X) = \frac4\pi \sum_{1\le h\le H_D} \frac{\Phi(h/(H_D+1))}{h} \Im A_h(D;X),
$$
$$
\mathcal M_2(D;X) = -\frac8\pi \sum_{\substack{1\le h\le H_D\\2\nmid h}} \frac{\Phi(h/(H_D+1))}{h} \chi_4(h) \Re B_h(D;X).
$$

These paired forms are algebraically equivalent to the raw definitions under the stated conventions.
**Regression test:** For any implementation, compute both raw and paired forms; they must agree to within machine precision. The raw forms remain canonical if conventions change.

### 4. Fixed-coefficient vs. stress norms
The active M9 targets use the **actual Vaaler coefficients** $\alpha_{h,H_D}$.
Arbitrary-coefficient versions (replace $\alpha_{h,H_D}$ by arbitrary $u_h$ with $|u_h|\le 1/h$) or $L^1$ stress norms (where absolute values are taken inside the $h$-sum) are **over-estimates** and are **not created by the Vaaler reduction**. They remain stress-tests only.

The trivial bound for $\mathcal M_1$ (taking absolute values) is $O(D\log H_D)$; at $D\asymp X^{1/2}$ this is $\asymp X^{1/2}\log X$, far above the conjectural $X^{1/4+\epsilon}$. Hence **genuine cancellation** is required, and the **fixed coefficient structure** must be exploited.

### 5. Q1/BSOS matrix (frozen weighted Cauchy--Schwarz kernel)
Following A2's frozen normalization:

$$
K_{d_1,d_2}^{(|\alpha|)} = w_D(d_1) w_D(d_2) \sum_{1\le |h|\le H_D} |\alpha_{h,H_D}|\, e\!\Bigl(hX\Bigl(\frac1{d_1}-\frac1{d_2}\Bigr)\Bigr).
$$

On the odd-denominator space $\mathcal V_D=\ell^2(\{d:D\le d<2D,\;2\nmid d\})$ define $U=\operatorname{diag}(\chi_4(d))$. Because $U$ is unitary, $\|U^*K^{(|\alpha|)}U\|_{\mathrm{op}} = \|K^{(|\alpha|)}\|_{\mathrm{op}}$.

Consequently any method that bounds the signed quadratic form **only through operator norm, Frobenius norm, spectral radius, Schur/Gershgorin, or absolute-value matrices is character-blind**. This is a **proved diagnostic**, not a global no-go theorem.

The BSOS signed statistic is
$$
\mathcal S_{\mathrm{signed}} = \sum_{d_1\neq d_2} \chi_4(d_1)\chi_4(d_2) K_{d_1,d_2}^{(|\alpha|)},
$$
with comparators $\mathcal S_{\mathrm{abs}} = \sum_{d_1\neq d_2} |K_{d_1,d_2}^{(|\alpha|)}|$ and $\mathcal S_{\mathrm{op}} = |\mathcal D_{\mathrm{odd}}| \|K^{(|\alpha|)}_{\mathrm{off}}\|_{\mathrm{op}}$.
A bound $|\mathcal S_{\mathrm{signed}}| \ll_\epsilon X^{1/2+\epsilon}$ would be **sufficient for the weighted Cauchy--Schwarz path** to M9a, but **no such bound has been proved**, and BSOS remains a falsification statistic.

### 7. H13 sign convention and amplitude
Under $e(t)=e^{2\pi i t}$, the modulo-$4$ Poisson summation for M9a yields the dual phase
$$
\phi_k(u) = \frac{hX}{u} - \frac{k u}{4}.
$$
Stationary points occur only for $k<0$; write $k=-m$ with $m>0$. Then
$$
u_0 = 2\sqrt{\frac{hX}{m}},\qquad
\phi_{-m}(u_0) = \sqrt{hXm}.
$$
The active dual length is
$$
m \asymp \frac{hX}{D^2}.
$$
At maximal Vaaler height $h\asymp H_D\asymp D X^{-1/4}$ and $D=X^\delta$,
$$
m \asymp X^{3/4-\delta}.
$$

The dual phase $\sqrt{Xhm}$ has **zero continuous Hessian determinant**:
$$
\Phi(h,m)=\sqrt{Xhm},\qquad
\nabla^2\Phi = \frac{1}{4} \sqrt{\frac{X}{h m}}
\begin{pmatrix} -m/h & 1 \\ 1 & -h/m \end{pmatrix},
\qquad \det\nabla^2\Phi = 0.
$$
Hence **generic full-rank stationary phase or decoupling cannot be applied**.

The **first-step falsification**: if the next spacing step (e.g., Cauchy--Schwarz) places the dual $\chi_4(m)$ only into a diagonal unitary conjugation and then uses an operator-norm or absolute-value bound, Q1-Spectral reappears and the character is erased. H13 remains viable only if a genuinely signed statistic survives.

## Hidden assumptions and potential gaps

- **Vaaler's residual majorant covers the floor-compatible half-jump** only because the sawtooth value at integers is $-\tfrac12$ and $V_H(n)=0$; must be explicitly noted in proof-draft.
- **R5 product-count** uses the pointwise Fejer bound $K_H(t)\ll \min(H,1/(H\|t\|^2))$; this bound is elementary but must be stated with an absolute constant. The dyadic-tail proof for the divisor sum assumes $X$ large and $\Delta\gg1$; for small $X$ the bound is trivial.
- **Short blocks $D<X^{1/4}$** are handled by $|\psi_F|\le 1/2$; the total contribution is $O(X^{1/4}\log X)$.
- **M9 requires genuine cancellation**; the trivial bound $D\log H_D$ is too large; no cancellation mechanism is yet proved.
- **M9b shifted-$F$** looks like a simple constant shift, but theorems may require $F$ to be independent of $T$ and $M$; the parameter-dependent shift $\rho D/(4X)$ may violate hypotheses unless explicitly checked.
- **H13 dual phase** has zero Hessian; standard stationary phase integrals are handled, but **double-sum estimates cannot rely on generic decoupling**.
- **BSOS** is defined on the off-diagonal of the weighted kernel; its normalization and relation to $\mathcal M_1$ require a concrete Cauchy--Schwarz inequality with known constants, which is not yet written.

## Counterexample or obstruction search

1. **R5 exact-resonance**: when $X/d\in\mathbb Z$, $K_H$ is maximal; the product-count with $W_\Delta(0)=1$ absorbs it. A potential failure would be if many $d$ simultaneously hit exact integers, but the number of such $d$ is bounded by the divisor function, so the contribution is negligible.
2. **H13 degeneracy**: the phase $\sqrt{Xhm}$ is degenerate; any claim of exponential decay in the dual variable would require a Hessian non-degenerate after a stationary-phase reduction, which is impossible.
3. **Li--Yang black-box**: if one erroneously replaces $H\le D X^{-1/4}$ by $H\le D X^{-\theta^*}$, the bound would give $P(X)\ll X^{\theta^*+\epsilon}$, which is weaker than the conjectural $X^{1/4+\epsilon}$.
4. **BSOS blindness**: if the signed form is treated as $U^*KU$, then operator norm equals unsigned norm; any claim of gain from $\chi_4$ through such a route is false.

### Numerical verification (asymptotic)
- R5 residual: the sum $\frac1H\sum_{d\asymp D} K_H(X/d)$ is bounded by $C X^{1/4} \log X$ for $X\ge X_0$, by the divisor-count argument. For any concrete $X$, the bound can be made effective with explicit constants; a symbolic computation for $X=10^6$, $D=1000$, $H\approx31.6$ would give a small numeric value, but we do not execute code.
- M9 raw vs paired: for any implementation, the two forms must match to machine precision; this is a regression requirement, not an analytic proof.

### Li--Yang line-match
All labels referenced above were checked against the TeX source; the shift $F_{\rho,D}(z)=\frac1{4z}+\frac{\rho D}{4X}$ satisfies $F'F'''-3(F'')^2 = -\frac{3}{8}z^{-6}$, matching the non-degeneracy condition. However, the function $F$ in Li--Yang's theorem is assumed to be **independent of $T,M$** (only depends on $m/M$ through $F(m/M)$); the dependence on $D$ and $X$ may require a uniform version of the theorem. This gap must be explicitly closed.

## Divergent alternatives and 20% exploration

1. **Signed Fourier truncation**: the signed tail $\sum_{|h|>H} \frac{e(ht)}{h}$ might preserve $\chi_4$ better than the Fejer majorant, but near discontinuities it suffers from a small-denominator barrier similar to the Fejer kernel. A rigorous bound would require an $L^1$ estimate for the truncated Dirichlet kernel, which likely reduces to the same product-count difficulty.
2. **Mellin--Perron / Voronoi**: the functional-equation route likely reconstructs Hardy--Voronoi--Bessel phases of type $2\pi\sqrt{nX}$, which are as hard as the original reciprocal sums. However, a **smoothed Perron with a well-placed contour** could potentially exploit subconvexity for $L(s,\chi_4)$; this is a known but extremely difficult direction, not pursued in this repo.
3. **B-process-first (H13)**: remains an exploratory track; the dual character is preserved, but the degenerate Hessian and the reappearance of H7 after direct differencing make it unlikely to provide endpoint gains without a new signed spacing theorem.

*Exploratory suggestion*: Test a **double-large-sieve inequality that is not unitarily invariant**, e.g., using a $q$-norm for $q\neq2$ on the $h$-side to preserve $\chi_4$ signs; this would require a non-standard extension of the Bombieri--Iwaniec framework.
