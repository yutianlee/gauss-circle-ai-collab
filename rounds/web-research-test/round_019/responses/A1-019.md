## Detailed reasoning

### 1. H4-R19: source-normalized finite Vaaler approximation

Use the convention

$$
e(t)=e^{2\pi i t}.
$$

Vaaler's Theorem 18 states that the trigonometric polynomial $\psi*j_N$ satisfies

$$
|\psi*j_N(x)-\psi(x)|
\le
(2N+2)^{-1}k_N(x),
$$

with accompanying formulas (7.13)--(7.17) on printed page 210. The coefficient function used in the Vaaler polynomial comes from Vaaler's Theorem 6, equation (2.28), for the Fourier transform of $J$; prior source audits identify this printed page as page 192 and the formula as

$$
\widehat J(t)=\pi t(1-|t|)\cot(\pi t)+|t|,
\qquad 0<|t|<1.
$$

The exact source labels are repeatedly recorded in the local proof-infrastructure packets as Theorem 6, equation (2.28), and Theorem 18, equations (7.13)--(7.17), especially (7.14).

The repo translation is:

$$
H=N,
$$

$$
K_H(t)=k_N(t)
=
\sum_{|k|\le H}
\left(1-\frac{|k|}{H+1}\right)e(kt)
=
\frac1{H+1}
\left(\frac{\sin\pi(H+1)t}{\sin\pi t}\right)^2,
$$

and

$$
\Phi(u)=\pi u(1-u)\cot(\pi u)+u,
\qquad 0<u<1.
$$

The Vaaler polynomial is

$$
V_H(t)
=
\sum_{1\le |h|\le H}\alpha_{h,H}e(ht),
$$

where

$$
\alpha_{h,H}
=
-\frac{\Phi(|h|/(H+1))}{2\pi i h}.
$$

The sign is fixed by the ordinary Fourier series

$$
t-\lfloor t\rfloor-\frac12
=
-\sum_{h\ne0}\frac{e(ht)}{2\pi i h}
$$

away from discontinuities, together with $\Phi(u)\to 1$ as $u\to0^+$. For $h>0$,

$$
\alpha_{h,H}
=
\frac{i}{2\pi h}\Phi\left(\frac{h}{H+1}\right).
$$

Because $\Phi$ is real-valued on $(0,1)$,

$$
\alpha_{-h,H}
=
-\frac{\Phi(h/(H+1))}{2\pi i(-h)}
=
\frac{\Phi(h/(H+1))}{2\pi i h}
=
\overline{\alpha_{h,H}}.
$$

Thus the correct conjugacy is

$$
\boxed{\alpha_{-h,H}=\overline{\alpha_{h,H}}.}
$$

The negative-conjugate relation is an algebra error.

The centered sawtooth convention in Vaaler differs from the floor-compatible convention used in the arithmetic identity. Define

$$
\psi_C(t)=
\begin{cases}
t-\lfloor t\rfloor-\frac12,& t\notin\mathbb Z,\\
0,& t\in\mathbb Z.
\end{cases}
$$

The repo uses

$$
\psi_F(t)=t-\lfloor t\rfloor-\frac12
$$

for all real $t$, so

$$
\psi_F(n)=-\frac12,
\qquad
\psi_C(n)=0.
$$

At nonintegers, $\psi_F=\psi_C$. At integers, the Vaaler polynomial satisfies

$$
V_H(n)=0
$$

because the $h$ and $-h$ terms cancel. Also,

$$
K_H(0)=H+1,
\qquad
\frac{K_H(0)}{2H+2}=\frac12.
$$

Therefore the same Fejer majorant covers the floor-compatible half-jump:

$$
|\psi_F(n)-V_H(n)|=\frac12
=
\frac{K_H(0)}{2H+2}.
$$

The proof-draft statement should be:

**H4-R19. Finite Vaaler approximation with floor-compatible residual.** For every integer $H\ge1$ and every real $t$,

$$
\psi_F(t)
=
\sum_{1\le |h|\le H}\alpha_{h,H}e(ht)
+
R_H^F(t),
$$

where

$$
\alpha_{h,H}
=
-\frac{\Phi(|h|/(H+1))}{2\pi i h},
$$

and

$$
|R_H^F(t)|
\le
\frac1{2H+2}K_H(t).
$$

Status: external theorem dependency from Vaaler, with the centered-to-floor-compatible endpoint conversion supplied above.

### 2. R5-Full-R19: completed Fejer residual lemma

**Lemma R5-Full-R19.** Let $X\ge2$ be real. Let

$$
X^{1/4}\le D\le X^{1/2},
$$

and let $H$ be an integer satisfying

$$
c_0D X^{-1/4}\le H\le C_0D X^{-1/4}
$$

for fixed constants $0<c_0\le C_0$. Let $w_D$ be a dyadic weight supported on $d\asymp D$ with $|w_D(d)|\le C_w$. Then, for every $\epsilon>0$,

$$
\frac1H
\sum_{d\asymp D}
|w_D(d)|K_H(X/d)
\ll_\epsilon X^{1/4+\epsilon},
$$

and for each $\rho\in\{1,3\}$,

$$
\frac1H
\sum_{d\asymp D}
|w_D(d)|
K_H\left(\frac{X/d+\rho}{4}\right)
\ll_\epsilon X^{1/4+\epsilon}.
$$

Consequently, assuming H4-R19, all Vaaler residual contributions from both sawtooth legs in H3 sum to

$$
\ll_\epsilon X^{1/4+\epsilon}.
$$

#### Proof: Fejer kernel bound

The Fejer kernel satisfies

$$
K_H(t)
\ll
\min\left(H,\frac1{H\|t\|^2}\right),
$$

where

$$
\|t\|=\min_{m\in\mathbb Z}|t-m|.
$$

Hence

$$
\frac1H K_H(t)
\ll
\min\left(1,\frac1{H^2\|t\|^2}\right).
$$

Set

$$
\Delta=\frac{D}{H}.
$$

Since $H\asymp D X^{-1/4}$,

$$
\Delta\asymp X^{1/4}.
$$

Define the exact-resonance cap

$$
W_\Delta(u)=
\begin{cases}
1,&u=0,\\
\min\left(1,\frac{\Delta^2}{u^2}\right),&u\ne0.
\end{cases}
$$

This definition is essential. It avoids singular notation at exact resonances and records that integer Fejer spikes are bounded at the correct capped scale.

#### First residual leg

Consider

$$
R_1(D;X)
=
\frac1H
\sum_{d\asymp D}
|w_D(d)|K_H(X/d).
$$

For each $d$, choose an integer $m=m(d)$ nearest to $X/d$. If there is a tie, choose either nearest integer. If $X/d$ is an integer, choose $m=X/d$.

For $d\asymp D$,

$$
\left\|\frac Xd\right\|
=
\left|\frac Xd-m\right|
=
\frac{|X-md|}{d}
\asymp
\frac{|X-md|}{D}.
$$

Therefore

$$
\frac1H K_H(X/d)
\ll
W_\Delta(X-md).
$$

Let

$$
n=md.
$$

For fixed $n$, the number of pairs $(m,d)$ with $n=md$ and $d\asymp D$ is at most $\tau(n)$. Relevant $n$ satisfy $n=X+O(D)$, but it is harmless to sum over all $n\ge1$. Thus

$$
R_1(D;X)
\ll
\sum_{n\ge1}
\tau(n)W_\Delta(X-n).
$$

For the relevant range $n\asymp X$, use

$$
\tau(n)\ll_\epsilon n^\epsilon\ll_\epsilon X^\epsilon.
$$

Small $n$ terms are absorbed into the constant for $X\ge2$. Hence

$$
R_1(D;X)
\ll_\epsilon
X^\epsilon
\sum_{n\in\mathbb Z}W_\Delta(X-n).
$$

The central range $|X-n|\le \Delta$ contributes $O(\Delta+1)$. The dyadic annulus

$$
2^j\Delta<|X-n|\le 2^{j+1}\Delta
$$

contributes

$$
O(2^j\Delta)\cdot O(2^{-2j})
=
O(2^{-j}\Delta).
$$

Summing $j\ge0$ gives

$$
\sum_{n\in\mathbb Z}W_\Delta(X-n)
\ll \Delta+1.
$$

Since $\Delta\asymp X^{1/4}$,

$$
R_1(D;X)
\ll_\epsilon X^{1/4+\epsilon}.
$$

This covers real $X$, integer $X$, near-integer $X$, and exact divisor resonances $d\mid X$.

#### Shifted residual legs

For $\rho\in\{1,3\}$, consider

$$
R_{2,\rho}(D;X)
=
\frac1H
\sum_{d\asymp D}
|w_D(d)|
K_H\left(\frac{X/d+\rho}{4}\right).
$$

Let $m=m(d)$ be a nearest integer to

$$
\frac{X/d+\rho}{4}.
$$

Then

$$
\left\|
\frac{X/d+\rho}{4}
\right\|
=
\frac{|X-d(4m-\rho)|}{4d}
\asymp
\frac{|X-d(4m-\rho)|}{D}.
$$

Set

$$
\ell=4m-\rho.
$$

Then

$$
\ell\equiv-\rho\pmod4.
$$

For $X$ sufficiently large in the active range $D\le X^{1/2}$, the nearest integer $m$ is positive and hence $\ell>0$. For bounded $X$, the assertion is absorbed into the implicit constant. If a tie at the boundary could choose a nonpositive $\ell$, choose the positive nearest integer instead; the two choices differ only at exact half-integer ties and do not affect the estimate.

Therefore

$$
\frac1H
K_H\left(\frac{X/d+\rho}{4}\right)
\ll
W_\Delta(X-d\ell).
$$

For fixed $n=d\ell$, the number of pairs $(d,\ell)$ with $d\asymp D$ and $\ell\equiv-\rho\pmod4$ is at most $\tau(n)$. The congruence restriction only reduces the number of admissible factorizations. The same summation gives

$$
R_{2,\rho}(D;X)
\ll_\epsilon X^{1/4+\epsilon}.
$$

#### Zero Fejer mode and both signs

If one expands $K_H$,

$$
K_H(t)=
1+
\sum_{1\le |k|\le H}
\left(1-\frac{|k|}{H+1}\right)e(kt),
$$

the zero mode contributes

$$
\frac1H\sum_{d\asymp D}|w_D(d)|
\ll
\frac{D}{H}
\asymp X^{1/4}.
$$

The nonzero modes are already controlled by the positive kernel estimate above. Both signs of $k$ are included in the real nonnegative Fejer kernel.

#### Short blocks and dyadic summation

For $D<X^{1/4}$, do not apply Vaaler. Instead use

$$
|\psi_F(t)|\le\frac12
$$

for each sawtooth term. The total contribution of all such short blocks is

$$
O\left(\sum_{d\le X^{1/4}}1\right)
=
O(X^{1/4})
$$

for each leg, with a harmless constant for the two shifted terms.

The number of dyadic blocks is $O(\log X)$, and all logarithmic losses are absorbed into $X^\epsilon$.

This proves R5-Full-R19 conditional on H4-R19.

### 6. M9b-ShiftedF-R19: theorem-extension checklist

The safest comparison form for M9b avoids treating $\chi_4(h)$ as a rough periodic bounded-variation weight. Use the identity

$$
e(hX/(4d))(e(h/4)-e(3h/4))
=
e\left(h\left(\frac{X}{4d}+\frac14\right)\right)
-
e\left(h\left(\frac{X}{4d}+\frac34\right)\right).
$$

Write $d=Dz$. Then each shifted phase has the form

$$
e\left(\frac{hX}{D}F_{\rho,D}(z)\right),
\qquad
\rho\in\{1,3\},
$$

where

$$
F_{\rho,D}(z)
=
\frac1{4z}+\frac{\rho D}{4X}.
$$

Derivative check:

$$
F_{\rho,D}'(z)=-\frac1{4z^2},
$$

$$
F_{\rho,D}''(z)=\frac1{2z^3},
$$

$$
F_{\rho,D}'''(z)=-\frac3{2z^4},
$$

and

$$
F_{\rho,D}'F_{\rho,D}'''-3(F_{\rho,D}'')^2
=
-\frac3{8z^6}.
$$

Thus the derivative size and nondegeneracy constants are uniform on $z\in[1,2]$ and are unaffected by the additive shift $\rho D/(4X)$.

The theorem-extension checklist is:

1. The theorem must allow $F$ depending on $D$ and $X$ through an additive constant, with uniform derivative constants.
2. The theorem must allow the actual Vaaler $h$ weights. On dyadic $h\asymp H_0$, the factor $H_0\alpha_{h,H_D}$ is smooth and bounded-variation in $h/H_0$ away from $h=0$, but the global $1/h$ weight requires dyadic subdivision.
3. The theorem must cover endpoint height

$$
H_0\le H_D\asymp D X^{-1/4}.
$$

4. Positive and negative frequencies must be paired or handled symmetrically. A one-sided theorem must be applied to the paired real formulation or to separate positive and negative blocks.
5. Treating $\chi_4(h)$ as a continuous bounded-variation $h$-weight is not acceptable over the whole dyadic interval; its variation is proportional to $H$. Use shifted-$F$ or split $h\bmod4$.

Status: open theorem-extension target. Derivative compatibility is verified; theorem-level applicability remains unproved.

splitting $d\bmod4$ and applying Poisson should give a Gauss factor proportional to $\chi_4(n)$ and integrals of the form

$$
I_n(h,D)=
\int w_D(u)e(hX/u-nu/4)\,du
$$

up to convention-dependent constants.

With phase

$$
\phi_n(u)=\frac{hX}{u}-\frac{nu}{4},
$$

the active stationary sign is $n=-m<0$, and then

$$
u_0=2\sqrt{\frac{hX}{m}},
$$

$$
\phi_{-m}(u_0)=\sqrt{hXm},
$$

and the active dual length is

$$
m\asymp \frac{hX}{D^2}.
$$

At maximal Vaaler height

$$
h\asymp H_D\asymp D X^{-1/4},
$$

and with $D=X^\delta$,

$$
m\asymp X^{3/4-\delta}.
$$

Thus H13 is near-balanced only near

$$
D\asymp X^{1/2},
$$

where $m\asymp X^{1/4}$.

## Counterexample or obstruction search

1. **Exact Fejer resonance.** If $X/d\in\mathbb Z$, then $K_H(X/d)=H+1$. The residual term is still safe because $(1/H)K_H(X/d)\asymp1$, captured by $W_\Delta(0)=1$. This should be tested for divisor-rich $X$.

2. **Shifted resonance.** If

$$
\frac{X/d+\rho}{4}\in\mathbb Z,
$$

then

$$
X=d(4m-\rho).
$$

The proof reduces this to product counting with $\ell=4m-\rho\equiv-\rho\pmod4$. Exact shifted resonances are capped by $W_\Delta(0)=1$.

3. **Divisor-rich $X$.** The worst possible multiplicity in R5 is bounded by $\tau(n)\ll_\epsilon n^\epsilon$. Divisor richness does not threaten R5, but it remains a good numerical stress test.

4. **M9 fixed-coefficient growth.** The route fails if there exists a sequence of dyadic blocks with

$$
|\mathcal M_i(D;X)|\gg X^{1/4+c}
$$

for some $c>0$. No such sequence is known from the current workflow, but no theorem rules it out.

5. **Operator-norm sign erasure.** If an attempted M9 proof applies Cauchy--Schwarz and then estimates a matrix of the form $U^*KU$ by operator norm, Frobenius norm, Schur/Gershgorin, cyclic trace, or absolute values, the $\chi_4$ sign has been erased at that step.

6. **H13 dual sign erasure.** If the H13 transform is followed by a Cauchy--Schwarz step that places $\chi_4(m)$ only in a diagonal unitary, H13 has not created a sign-sensitive route.

7. **Li--Yang endpoint mismatch.** At $D=X^{1/2}$, the Vaaler endpoint height is $H=X^{1/4}$. Raw Case A reaches only $X^{33/164}$ and raw Case B reaches only $X^{1/6}$ in the relevant branch. This blocks black-box import, not all future Bombieri--Iwaniec variants.

## Divergent alternatives and 20% exploration

### Alternative A: one signed non-conjugacy statistic for M9a

A possible escape from Q1-Spectral is to avoid estimating $U^*KU$ by a unitarily invariant norm. Freeze the Cauchy--Schwarz kernel

$$
K_{d_1,d_2}
=
w_D(d_1)w_D(d_2)
\sum_{1\le |h|\le H_D}
|\alpha_{h,H_D}|
e\left(hX\left(\frac1{d_1}-\frac1{d_2}\right)\right),
$$

on

$$
\mathcal D_{\rm odd}=\{d:D\le d<2D,\;2\nmid d\}.
$$

The signed quadratic form is

$$
\mathcal Q_{\rm signed}
=
\sum_{d_1,d_2\in\mathcal D_{\rm odd}}
\chi_4(d_1)\chi_4(d_2)K_{d_1,d_2}.
$$

The diagonal contribution is

$$
\mathcal Q_{\rm diag}
=
\sum_d K_{d,d}
\asymp
D\sum_{1\le |h|\le H_D}|\alpha_{h,H_D}|
\asymp D\log H_D.
$$

Since the outer Cauchy--Schwarz also contributes a factor $\sum|\alpha_h|\asymp\log H_D$, an off-diagonal target of the form

$$
\mathcal Q_{\rm off,signed}
\ll_\epsilon X^{1/2+\epsilon}
$$

is the natural scale for this normalization. The quick falsification test is numerical: compare the signed off-diagonal statistic to

$$
\mathcal S_{\rm abs}
=
\sum_{d_1\ne d_2}|K_{d_1,d_2}|
$$

and to

$$
|\mathcal D_{\rm odd}|\|K_{\rm off}\|_{\operatorname{op}}.
$$

If the signed statistic is consistently of the same order as the absolute or operator-norm comparators, this particular statistic is unlikely to help. If it is much smaller in the endpoint regime, it deserves a proof attempt.
