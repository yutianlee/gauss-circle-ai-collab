In the exploratory allocation, A3 suggests testing a double-large-sieve inequality that uses a $q$-norm for $q \neq 2$ on the $h$-side to preserve $\chi_4$ signs. This claim represents a profound mathematical misunderstanding of functional analysis.
The character vector acts on the frequency domain by pointwise multiplication by $U = \text{diag}(\chi_4(d))$. Since $\chi_4(d) \in \{1, -1, 0\}$, the operator $U$ acts merely by assigning $\pm 1$ phases to the sequence coefficients. Let $x \in \ell^p(\mathcal{D}_{\text{odd}})$. The norm evaluates to:
$$
\|U x\|_p = \left( \sum_{d \in \mathcal{D}_{\text{odd}}} |\chi_4(d) x_d|^p \right)^{1/p} = \left( \sum_{d \in \mathcal{D}_{\text{odd}}} |x_d|^p \right)^{1/p} = \|x\|_p
$$
Thus, $U$ is a strict isometric isomorphism on any $\ell^p$ space for $1 \le p \le \infty$. Let $K : \ell^p \to \ell^q$ represent any linear spacing matrix operator. By the definition of the induced operator norm:
$$
\|U^* K U\|_{p \to q} = \sup_{x \ne 0} \frac{\|U^* K U x\|_q}{\|x\|_p}
$$
Because $U$ is an isometry on $\ell^q$, $\|U^* (K U x)\|_q = \|K U x\|_q$. Furthermore, because $U$ is an isometry and bijective on $\ell^p$, taking the supremum over all test vectors $x$ is identical to taking the supremum over vectors $y = Ux$, where $\|x\|_p = \|y\|_p$. Thus:
$$
\sup_{y \ne 0} \frac{\|Ky\|_q}{\|y\|_p} = \|K\|_{p \to q}
$$
Changing the norm exponent from $q=2$ to $q \neq 2$ does absolutely nothing to break diagonal isometric invariance. The suggested route is geometrically identically character-blind and must be classified as `[LIKELY-FALSE]`.
