## Possible errors or hidden assumptions

1. **A2 slightly understates the diagonal logarithm.**
   With the frozen Cauchy--Schwarz inequality,

$$
L:=\sum_{1\le |h|\le H_D}|\alpha_{h,H_D}|\asymp \log H_D,
$$

and

$$
\mathcal S_{\rm diag}\asymp D\log H_D.
$$

Thus the diagonal contribution to $|\mathcal M_1|$ is of size about

$$
(D(\log H_D)^2)^{1/2}=D^{1/2}\log H_D,
$$

not $D^{1/2}(\log H_D)^{1/2}$. This is harmless for $X^\epsilon$ bookkeeping but should be corrected in the proof-draft statement.

needs the correct support condition $m\asymp hX/D^2$, amplitude, phase, and transition-range treatment. Without that, it is a useful test plan, not an analytic model.
