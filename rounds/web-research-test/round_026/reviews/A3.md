  The "$X^{5/4}$" heuristic for $D\asymp X^{1/2}$ is a uniform-fiber counting argument that ignores the $1/|h|$ weights, gcd structure, parity, and dyadic cutoffs. It is not a lower bound and requires numerical stress-testing and a signed companion.

  The derivation for the denominator-paired family assumes that sums of the form $\sum_h 1/|h(h+\Delta)|$ behave like $(1/|\Delta|)\log|\Delta|$ uniformly, and that truncation-edge cases contribute negligibly. ## Possible errors or hidden assumptions

* **A2's pseudocode uses a simplified weight.**
  The code replaces $|\beta_h|$ (which involves $|\alpha_h C_h|$) by a proxy $1/|h|$, and does not yet include the exact Vaaler taper $\Phi(h/(H_D+1))$. This will affect the mass in bins where $h$ is close to $H_D$, although the logarithmic bounds may still hold.

* **A2's bound for the semi-diagonal family.**
  The estimate $\asymp D \log^3 H_D$ implicitly assumes that the variables $h_1,h_2,h_3,h_4$ range over a symmetric two-sided interval and that the condition $h_1-h_2+h_3-h_4=0$ does not introduce extra correlations that would increase the weighted sum. While the order of magnitude is plausible, a more careful verification is advisable before the bound is marked `[PROVED]`.

* **Convention inconsistency between A1 and A2.**
  A2 freezes a two-sided frequency convention for the fourth moment but uses a one-sided paired-real convention for CRI. The $S_2$ relation $S_2=2i(\Sigma_1-\Sigma_3)$ holds only under a consistent two-sided convention with the appropriate signed sum. If $\Sigma_r$ is defined via the one-sided $\operatorname{Re}$ form, the constants and sign factors differ; A3 will need an explicit translation table to avoid mismatches.

* **A1's R5 proof implicitly assumes bounded overlap of dyadic weights.**
  If the partition does not have bounded overlap, the logarithmic factor could be larger than $O(\log X)$. The proof draft should state the overlap constant explicitly.

* **A1's M9-Pair formulas assume real dyadic weights and real-weight treatment of positive/negative frequencies.**
  For complex weights the raw two-sided definitions should be used; the warning is noted but should appear in the theorem statement.
