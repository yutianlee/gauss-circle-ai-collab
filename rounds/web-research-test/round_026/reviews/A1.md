## Possible errors or hidden assumptions

A2 sometimes describes the fourth-moment signs as preserving the "spatial character." In $\mathcal M_2$ the character is in the frequency variable through $C_h=2i\chi_4(h)$ on odd $h$. The wording should be corrected to "frequency-character signs."

A2's "rigorous exact-resonance taxonomy" claim is too strong. The output itself still labels several components as derived under assumptions or heuristic. The Stage C synthesis should promote only the algebraic expansion, the resonance integer, the CRI identity, and named-family mass checks. It should not promote a complete $N=0$ classification.

A2's CRI convention drifts between two-sided and one-sided paired-real conventions. Both can be valid, but the lemma bank needs one frozen convention with all constants. Under the two-sided convention one writes

$$
S_2=2i(\Sigma_1-\Sigma_3),
$$

whereas under the paired-real convention the $\Sigma_r^R$ definition uses real parts and a different scalar. Mixing these conventions will corrupt numerical comparisons.

A2's $\Delta$-method character absorption claim is underspecified. Writing

$$
\chi_4(h)=\frac{e(h/4)-e(3h/4)}{2i}
$$

does allow conversion into additive phases, but saying this works "if $c$ is a multiple of $4$" is not a full modulus analysis. If $4\nmid c$, the correct modulus is an lcm-type modulus and the coprimality/complete-sum structure changes.

A3's numerical evidence is still too small. Values at $X=16,25,36$ and one H13 model at $X=256$ are useful debugging checks but cannot support asymptotic claims about M9. A3 is careful not to claim asymptotic evidence, but the final judge synthesis should explicitly record these as formula-regression data only.

A3's pseudocode uses a floating-style `t.is_integer()` check. That is not robust for Fejer spikes. Exact rational or modular detection should be used, as A3 itself notes elsewhere.

A3's R5 example with $H=2$ when the natural height is $H_D\asymp1$ is acceptable as a stress example, but it should not be presented as the canonical local choice without the constant-factor convention being stated.
