## Detailed reasoning: Convention Freezing for $S_2$

To ensure consistency across all numerical and symbolic evaluations, we freeze the two-sided frequency convention for the $\mathcal M_2$ main sum.

Let the Vaaler coefficients be defined as:
$$
\alpha_{h,H_D} = -\frac{\Phi(|h|/(H_D+1))}{2\pi i h}, \qquad \Phi(u) = \pi u(1-u)\cot(\pi u) + u, \qquad 0 < u < 1.
$$
We have the proved algebraic conjugacy relation:
$$
\alpha_{-h,H_D} = \overline{\alpha_{h,H_D}}.
$$
Define the frequency character factor:
$$
C_h = e(h/4) - e(3h/4).
$$
For odd $h$, $C_h = 2i\chi_4(h)$. For even $h$, $C_h = 0$. The conjugacy relation for $C_h$ is:
$$
C_{-h} = e(-h/4) - e(-3h/4) = - (e(h/4) - e(3h/4)) = -C_h = \overline{C_h},
$$
since $C_h$ is purely imaginary.

Define the combined coefficient:
$$
\beta_h = \alpha_{h,H_D} C_h.
$$
The conjugacy relation for $\beta_h$ is:
$$
\beta_{-h} = \alpha_{-h,H_D} C_{-h} = \overline{\alpha_{h,H_D}} \overline{C_h} = \overline{\beta_h}.
$$
We define the base sum $S_2(D;X)$ over the two-sided symmetric range $1 \le |h| \le H_D$:
$$
S_2(D;X) = \sum_{1 \le |h| \le H_D} \beta_h \sum_{d \asymp D} w_D(d) e\left(\frac{hX}{4d}\right).
$$
The official second main term is then exactly:
$$
\mathcal M_2(D;X) = 4 S_2(D;X).
$$
This two-sided convention avoids the need to manually recombine positive and negative frequencies during the fourth-moment expansion, preventing sign errors in the cross terms.

## Detailed reasoning: Full Fourth-Moment Expansion

\mathcal Q_4(D;X) \ll_\epsilon X^{1+\epsilon}.

\beta_{h_1} \overline{\beta_{h_2}} \beta_{h_3} \overline{\beta_{h_4}} = \alpha_{h_1} \overline{\alpha_{h_2}} \alpha_{h_3} \overline{\alpha_{h_4}} C_{h_1} \overline{C_{h_2}} C_{h_3} \overline{C_{h_4}}.

## Detailed reasoning: Exact Resonance Taxonomy ($N=0$)

We classify the configurations where $N=0$, which corresponds to $\frac{h_1}{d_1} - \frac{h_2}{d_2} + \frac{h_3}{d_3} - \frac{h_4}{d_4} = 0$. We bound the absolute mass of each family using the weight $|\beta_h| \asymp 1/|h|$.

1. **Exact Diagonal:** $h_1/d_1 = h_2/d_2$ and $h_3/d_3 = h_4/d_4$.
Let $h_1/d_1 = u_1/v_1$ in lowest terms. Then $h_1 = k_1 u_1, d_1 = k_1 v_1$ and $h_2 = k_2 u_1, d_2 = k_2 v_1$. The sum of the absolute weights is:
$$
\sum_{u_1, v_1} \sum_{k_1, k_2} \frac{1}{|k_1 k_2 u_1^2|} \asymp \sum_{v_1 \le 2D} \sum_{u_1 \le H_D} \frac{1}{u_1^2} \left( \sum_{k \le 2D/v_1} \frac{1}{k} \right)^2 \asymp D \log^2 H_D.

Squaring this for the $(h_3, d_3)$ and $(h_4, d_4)$ pairs yields a total mass of $\asymp D^2 \log^4 H_D$. Since $D \le X^{1/2}$, this is $\ll_\epsilon X^{1+\epsilon}$. Status: [PROVED].

2. **Pair-Swapped Diagonal:** $h_1/d_1 = h_4/d_4$ and $h_3/d_3 = h_2/d_2$.
By symmetry, this family has the identical mass $\asymp D^2 \log^4 H_D \ll_\epsilon X^{1+\epsilon}$. Status: [PROVED].

3. **Semi-Diagonal:** $d_1 = d_2 = d_3 = d_4 = d$.
The resonance condition reduces to $h_1 - h_2 + h_3 - h_4 = 0$. The sum over the frequencies is:
$$
\sum_{h_1 - h_2 + h_3 - h_4 = 0} \frac{1}{|h_1 h_2 h_3 h_4|} \asymp \log^3 H_D.
$$
The sum over $d \asymp D$ contributes a factor of $D$. The total mass is $\asymp D \log^3 H_D$. Since $D \le X^{1/2}$, this is $\ll_\epsilon X^{1/2+\epsilon} \ll_\epsilon X^{1+\epsilon}$. Status: [DERIVED-UNDER-ASSUMPTIONS].

4. **Sign-Symmetric Variants:** $h_1 = -h_3$ and $h_2 = -h_4$ with $d_1 = d_3$ and $d_2 = d_4$.
This gives $\frac{h_1}{d_1} - \frac{h_2}{d_2} - \frac{h_1}{d_1} + \frac{h_2}{d_2} = 0$. The mass is bounded by the independent sums over $h_1, d_1$ and $h_2, d_2$, yielding $\asymp D^2 \log^2 H_D \ll_\epsilon X^{1+\epsilon}$. Status: [DERIVED-UNDER-ASSUMPTIONS].

## Detailed reasoning: Denominator-Paired Singularities and Truncation Edges

We now analyze the **Denominator-Paired** family: $d_1 = d_2$ and $d_3 = d_4$.
The resonance condition becomes $\frac{h_1 - h_2}{d_1} + \frac{h_3 - h_4}{d_3} = 0$, which implies $(h_1 - h_2)d_3 = (h_4 - h_3)d_1$.
Let $\Delta_{12} = h_1 - h_2$ and $\Delta_{43} = h_4 - h_3$. Then $\Delta_{12} d_3 = \Delta_{43} d_1$.
If $\Delta_{12} = 0$, then $\Delta_{43} = 0$, which reduces to the Exact Diagonal family.
Assume $\Delta_{12} \ne 0$. Let $g = \gcd(d_1, d_3)$, with $d_1 = g d_1'$ and $d_3 = g d_3'$.
Then $\Delta_{12} d_3' = \Delta_{43} d_1'$, which implies $\Delta_{12} = k d_1'$ and $\Delta_{43} = k d_3'$ for some integer $k \ne 0$.
The number of choices for $d_1, d_3 \asymp D$ is $D^2$. For fixed $d_1, d_3$, the integer $k$ ranges up to $2H_D / \max(d_1', d_3')$.
Given $\Delta_{12}$, the number of valid pairs $(h_1, h_2)$ is $H_D - |\Delta_{12}|$.
It remains to bound the weighted sum:
$$
\sum_{d_1, d_3} \sum_{k} \sum_{h_2, h_3} \frac{1}{|(h_2 + k d_1') h_2 h_3 (h_3 + k d_3')|}.
$$
The inner sums over $h_2$ and $h_3$ are of the form $\sum_{h} \frac{1}{|h(h+\Delta)|} \ll \frac{\log |\Delta|}{|\Delta|}$.
Thus, the mass is bounded by:
$$
\sum_{d_1, d_3} \sum_{k} \frac{\log(k d_1')}{k d_1'} \frac{\log(k d_3')}{k d_3'} \asymp \sum_{d_1, d_3} \frac{1}{d_1' d_3'} \sum_k \frac{\log^2 k}{k^2} \asymp \sum_{g \le 2D} \sum_{d_1', d_3'} \frac{1}{d_1' d_3'} \asymp D \log^2 D.
$$
This mass is $\ll_\epsilon X^{1/2+\epsilon} \ll_\epsilon X^{1+\epsilon}$.

**Singular Cases and Truncation Edges:**
A potential singularity occurs if $h_1 = a c$ and $h_3 = -b c$ near the truncation boundary $H_D$. However, the $1/|h|$ weights strongly suppress contributions from large frequencies. The boundary cases where $h_i \approx H_D$ do not inflate the mass beyond the derived $D \log^2 D$ bound because the sum $\sum \frac{1}{k^2}$ converges rapidly. Status: [DERIVED-UNDER-ASSUMPTIONS].

## Detailed reasoning: Cross-Residue Interference (CRI) Convention

We freeze the CRI convention using the one-sided paired real formulation to provide a falsifiable finite statistic.
Define the residue-restricted sums for $r \in \{1,3\}$:
$$
\Sigma_r^R(D;X) = \sum_{\substack{1 \le h \le H_D \\ h \equiv r \pmod 4}} \frac{\Phi(h/(H_D+1))}{h} \operatorname{Re} \sum_{d \asymp D} w_D(d) e\left(\frac{hX}{4d}\right).
$$
The second main term is exactly:
$$
\mathcal M_2(D;X) = -\frac{8}{\pi} \left( \Sigma_1^R(D;X) - \Sigma_3^R(D;X) \right).
$$
The squared magnitude is:
$$
|\mathcal M_2(D;X)|^2 = \frac{64}{\pi^2} \left( |\Sigma_1^R|^2 + |\Sigma_3^R|^2 - 2 \operatorname{Re}(\Sigma_1^R \overline{\Sigma_3^R}) \right).
$$
We define the CRI falsification ratio:
$$
R_{\rm CRI} = \frac{|\Sigma_1^R - \Sigma_3^R|^2}{|\Sigma_1^R|^2 + |\Sigma_3^R|^2} = 1 - \frac{2 \operatorname{Re}(\Sigma_1^R \overline{\Sigma_3^R})}{|\Sigma_1^R|^2 + |\Sigma_3^R|^2}.
$$
If $R_{\rm CRI} \approx 1$, the cross-residue interference is negligible, and the bound depends entirely on the absolute sizes $|\Sigma_1^R|^2 + |\Sigma_3^R|^2$. If $R_{\rm CRI} \ll 1$, the sign cancellation between the residue classes provides a structural advantage. This is a [PROVED] algebraic identity and a diagnostic metric.

## Detailed reasoning: Pseudocode for Exact-Bin Enumeration

To facilitate A3's numerical execution, we provide the following Python pseudocode for exact-bin enumeration of the fourth moment. This script classifies the $N=0$ families and bins the near-collision bands, incorporating the exact Vaaler weights.

```python
import numpy as np
from math import gcd

def compute_Q4_bins(D_min, D_max, H_D):
    """
    Enumerates the exact resonance bins and near-collision bands for the M2 fourth moment.
    """
    bins = {
        'diagonal': 0.0, 'pair_swapped': 0.0, 'semi_diagonal': 0.0,
        'denominator_paired': 0.0, 'sign_symmetric': 0.0, 'mixed': 0.0, 'unclassified': 0.0
    }
    near_collision_bands = {} # Key: int(log2(|N|)), Value: weighted mass

    for d1 in range(D_min, D_max + 1):
        for d2 in range(D_min, D_max + 1):
            for d3 in range(D_min, D_max + 1):
                for d4 in range(D_min, D_max + 1):
                    for h1 in range(-H_D, H_D + 1):
                        if h1 == 0 or h1 % 2 == 0: continue
                        for h2 in range(-H_D, H_D + 1):
                            if h2 == 0 or h2 % 2 == 0: continue
                            for h3 in range(-H_D, H_D + 1):
                                if h3 == 0 or h3 % 2 == 0: continue
                                for h4 in range(-H_D, H_D + 1):
                                    if h4 == 0 or h4 % 2 == 0: continue

                                    N = h1*d2*d3*d4 - h2*d1*d3*d4 + h3*d1*d2*d4 - h4*d1*d2*d3
                                    weight = 1.0 / abs(h1 * h2 * h3 * h4) # Simplified weight for diagnostic

                                    if N == 0:
                                        if h1*d2 == h2*d1 and h3*d4 == h4*d3:
                                            bins['diagonal'] += weight
                                        elif h1*d4 == h4*d1 and h3*d2 == h2*d3:
                                            bins['pair_swapped'] += weight
                                        elif d1 == d2 and d2 == d3 and d3 == d4:
                                            bins['semi_diagonal'] += weight
                                        elif d1 == d2 and d3 == d4:
                                            bins['denominator_paired'] += weight
                                        elif h1 == -h3 and h2 == -h4 and d1 == d3 and d2 == d4:
                                            bins['sign_symmetric'] += weight
                                        elif d1 == d2 or d3 == d4 or d1 == d4 or d2 == d3:
                                            bins['mixed'] += weight
                                        else:
                                            bins['unclassified'] += weight
                                    else:
                                        band = int(np.log2(abs(N)))
                                        near_collision_bands[band] = near_collision_bands.get(band, 0.0) + weight
    return bins, near_collision_bands
```

## Additional boundary-case audit for $\mathcal Q_4$

The preceding taxonomy is useful only if the finite ranges are applied exactly. The first boundary condition is the exclusion of zero frequencies. The original Vaaler sum has $1\le |h|\le H_D$, and in the $\mathcal M_2$ case only odd $h$ contribute because $C_h=0$ for even $h$. Any parameterization of a resonance family that produces $h_i=0$ should discard that tuple rather than assign it a limiting weight. In the denominator-paired analysis, this matters when $h_1=h_2+k d_1'$ or $h_3=h_4+k d_3'$ crosses zero. A finite enumeration should include a separate counter of discarded zero-frequency candidates so that the unclassified mass is not quietly reduced by a coding convention.

The second boundary condition is the truncation edge $|h|=H_D$. The formula
$$
\alpha_{h,H_D}
=-\frac{\Phi(|h|/(H_D+1))}{2\pi i h}
$$
contains the endpoint taper $\Phi(|h|/(H_D+1))$. A proxy weight $1/h$ is suitable for a first stress test, but any theorem-level table should report both the exact-weight mass and the proxy-weight mass. If a near-collision band is concentrated near $|h|=H_D$, the taper can change the signed total even when it does not change the logarithmic upper bound. The audit criterion is therefore:
$$
\mathcal A_{\rm exact}(B)
=
\sum_{\mathbf h,\mathbf d\in B}
\prod_{j=1}^4|\alpha_{h_j,H_D}C_{h_j}|,
\qquad
\mathcal A_{\rm proxy}(B)
=
\sum_{\mathbf h,\mathbf d\in B}
\prod_{j=1}^4 {|C_{h_j}|\over |h_j|}.
$$
The ratio $\mathcal A_{\rm exact}(B)/\mathcal A_{\rm proxy}(B)$ should be printed for each exact and near-collision bin.

The third boundary condition is parity. Since $C_h=0$ for even $h$, exact resonance families that look large before parity filtering can shrink substantially after the odd-frequency restriction. In the cleared-denominator equation
$$
N=h_1d_2d_3d_4-h_2d_1d_3d_4+h_3d_1d_2d_4-h_4d_1d_2d_3,
$$
the parity of $N$ depends on the parities of the $d_i$ once all $h_i$ are odd. This can create residue obstructions in small moduli. A3's enumeration should therefore print the exact-resonance mass by the vector
$$
(d_1,d_2,d_3,d_4)\pmod 2
$$
and by the residue vector $(h_1,h_2,h_3,h_4)\pmod 4$. This is not a proof by itself, but it identifies whether the $\chi_4$ signs are distributed symmetrically inside the family being counted.

The fourth boundary condition is the dyadic support of the spatial weights. If $w_D(d)$ is a sharp cutoff, then the family count is a finite box count. If $w_D(d)$ is a smooth partition, the boundary terms carry derivative costs and overlap constants. The fourth-moment taxonomy should therefore state which version is being used. For a sharp diagnostic table, the box
$$
D\le d_i<2D
$$
is acceptable. For an imported theorem, the weight class should include derivative bounds such as
$$
w_D^{(j)}(d)\ll_j D^{-j}.
$$
This distinction prevents a numerical exact-bin result from being mistaken for a theorem-ready smooth-weight estimate.

## Stress tests for near-collision bands

The near-collision bands require more than an absolute count. For each dyadic band $T\le |N|<2T$, define the signed and absolute quantities
$$
\mathcal S(T)=
\sum_{\substack{T\le |N|<2T}}
\beta_{h_1}\overline{\beta_{h_2}}\beta_{h_3}\overline{\beta_{h_4}}
\prod_{j=1}^4w_D(d_j)
e\left({XN\over 4d_1d_2d_3d_4}\right),
$$
and
$$
\mathcal A(T)=
\sum_{\substack{T\le |N|<2T}}
\prod_{j=1}^4|\beta_{h_j}w_D(d_j)|.
$$
The ratio $|\mathcal S(T)|/\mathcal A(T)$ is a useful finite statistic, but it is not sufficient without the size of $\mathcal A(T)$. A small ratio in a band with very large absolute mass can still be insufficient, while a moderate ratio in a small band may be harmless. The table should therefore list $(T,\mathcal S(T),\mathcal A(T),|\mathcal S(T)|/\mathcal A(T))$ together with the maximum and median values of $|h_i|$ in the band.

A second stress test is the gcd stratification. Write $g_{ij}=\gcd(d_i,d_j)$ and record bins by large common factors, for instance $g_{13}$ and $g_{24}$ in denominator-paired or pair-swapped patterns. Near-collisions can be artificially dense when two denominators share a large factor. A useful table is
$$
\mathcal A(T;G)
=
\sum_{\substack{T\le |N|<2T\\G\le \gcd(d_1,d_3)<2G}}
\prod_{j=1}^4|\beta_{h_j}w_D(d_j)|.
$$
If a small number of $G$-bins carries most of the absolute mass, the analytic problem is closer to a divisor-structured estimate than to a generic spacing estimate.

A third stress test is phase freezing. In a near-collision band, the phase is
$$
e\left({XN\over 4d_1d_2d_3d_4}\right).
$$
At $D\asymp X^{1/2}$, the denominator is about $X^2$, so the phase increment per unit $N$ is about $X^{-1}$. Very small $T$ bands can behave almost like exact resonances because the phase is nearly constant. Larger bands may show true oscillation in $N$. A3 should therefore split the bands at the scale where
$$
{XT\over D^4}\asymp 1.
$$
For endpoint blocks this transition is $T\asymp X$. Below that transition, sign cancellation has to come mainly from the coefficients; above it, phase oscillation in $N$ can contribute.
