## Most valuable input from others

**A2.** The most useful contribution is the Q1-Spectral diagnostic. On the odd-denominator dyadic space

$$
\mathcal D_{\mathrm{odd}}=\{d:D\le d<2D,\ 2\nmid d\},
\qquad
\mathcal V_D=\ell^2(\mathcal D_{\mathrm{odd}}),
$$

multiplication by $\chi_4(d)$ is the diagonal unitary

$$
U_{d_1,d_2}=\delta_{d_1,d_2}\chi_4(d_1).
$$

Thus $U^*U=I$, and any argument depending only on $\|U^*KU\|_{\mathrm{op}}$ cannot exploit the spatial character. This is a good, narrow diagnostic for operator-norm, Schur, Gershgorin, absolute-value, and spectral-radius-only spacing arguments. It should be added as a proved algebraic diagnostic, not as a global no-go theorem. The A2 output under review explicitly centers M9 and Q1-Spectral.

A2's H13 B-process-first transform is also useful. For

$$
\mathcal S(h,D)=\sum_d \chi_4(d)w_D(d)e(hX/d),
$$

splitting $d=4u+r$, $r\in\{1,3\}$, and applying Poisson in $u$ gives a dual integral of the form

$$
\mathcal S(h,D)
=
\frac{i}{2}\sum_{m\in\mathbb Z}\chi_4(m)
\int_{\mathbb R}w_D(v)e\left(\frac{hX}{v}-\frac{mv}{4}\right)\,dv,
$$

up to the fixed Fourier-sign convention. The stationary range has $m<0$, and after writing $m=-n$ the dual length is

$$
n\asymp \frac{hX}{D^2}.
$$

The resulting leading phase is proportional to $\sqrt{hXn}$ and has degenerate continuous Hessian in $(h,n)$. This is a useful transform and guardrail, but it is not an estimate for M9.

## Claims that look correct

1. **A2's Q1-Spectral algebra is correct in its stated scope.** On odd denominators, $\chi_4(d)^2=1$, so the diagonal character matrix is unitary. Therefore

$$
\|U^*KU\|_{\mathrm{op}}=\|K\|_{\mathrm{op}}.
$$

This proves blindness for methods that reduce the signed quadratic form to a conjugated operator norm or absolute-value matrix. It does not rule out signed bilinear forms, non-conjugacy open paths, or trace statistics that are not cyclic conjugacy invariants.

2. **A2's pure trace-conjugacy observation is correct.** If the signed matrix is literally $U^*KU$, then

$$
\operatorname{Tr}((U^*KU)^m)=\operatorname{Tr}(K^m)
$$

by cyclicity of trace and $UU^*=I$. This is useful, but only for pure conjugacy-invariant trace moments.

3. **A2's H13 Poisson transform has the right structure.** The modulo-$4$ Poisson transform transfers the spatial character into a Gauss factor, hence a dual $\chi_4(m)$ factor. The dual length $m\asymp hX/D^2$ is consistent with earlier H8/C2 calculations.

4. **A2's warning about Hessian degeneracy is correct.** For a dual phase of the schematic form

$$
\Phi(h,m)=C\sqrt{Xhm},
$$

one has

$$
\det \nabla^2\Phi=0.
$$

Therefore generic full-rank two-dimensional stationary phase or decoupling cannot be invoked directly.

5. **A3's integer-discontinuity check for H4 is correct.** The floor-compatible sawtooth has $\psi(n)=-1/2$. The centered Vaaler trigonometric polynomial vanishes at integer $n$, while

$$
K_H(0)=H+1,
$$

so the residual bound

$$
|R_H(n)|\le \frac{K_H(0)}{2H+2}=\frac12
$$

covers the half-jump. This is exactly the kind of endpoint check the proof draft needs.

6. **A3's R5 product-count mechanism remains sound, conditional on H4.** The Fejer kernel bound

$$
\frac1H K_H(t)\ll \min\left(1,\frac{1}{H^2\|t\|^2}\right)
$$

reduces residual spikes to near-products $md\approx X$ or $(4m-\rho)d\approx X$. Grouping by the product and using $\tau(n)\ll_\epsilon n^\epsilon$ gives the desired $X^{1/4+\epsilon}$ scale. The remaining task is a complete R5-Full write-up over all dyadic blocks and both sawtooth legs.

7. **A3's Li--Yang endpoint mismatch is correct at the black-box theorem level.** Li--Yang's work gives the right structural reciprocal-sum class, but the published theorem does not provide the endpoint height $H\asymp M T^{-1/4}$. The abstract and source metadata support the general method description.

8. **A3's separation of the two stationary-phase scales is correct.** In the integral

$$
I(\xi)=\int w_D(u)e\left(\frac{kX}{u}-\xi u\right)\,du,
$$

the active dual length and stationary parameter are distinct:

$$
M_{\mathrm{dual}}\asymp \frac{kX}{D^2},
\qquad
\Lambda\asymp \frac{kX}{D}.
$$

This distinction should remain in the lemma bank.

## Possible errors or hidden assumptions

1. **A2's factorial/divisor alignment repair contains a scaling inconsistency.** If $X_K=P_K^2$, then $P_K=X_K^{1/2}$, not $X_K^{1/4}$. The robust conclusion should instead be stated using the divisor bound: exact critical divisors inside $[X^{1/4},X^{1/2}]$ are at most $\tau(X)\ll_\epsilon X^\epsilon$ for integer $X$, so exact divisor alignment alone cannot produce a dense obstruction. The primorial construction should be removed or rewritten.

2. **A2's PNT-in-arithmetic-progressions discussion is unnecessary.** For $\chi_4$, interval sums are controlled by periodicity modulo $4$; unsmoothed interval sums are $O(1)$ and smooth sums follow by summation by parts. No prime number theorem input is needed.

3. **A2 overstates some diagnostics.** "Any double large sieve inequality or Bombieri--Iwaniec metric" is too broad. The proved statement covers operator-norm-only, spectral-radius-only, Schur/Gershgorin, and absolute-value reductions. A signed-form or non-conjugacy spacing estimate is not ruled out.

4. **A2's rational/affine C3 claims need a concrete analytic grid.** Clearing denominators in a rational transformation may change spacing, weights, and summation geometry. Treat the odd/even dilation conclusions as diagnostics until connected to the actual M9 or H13 variables.

5. **A2's H13 dual phase is not automatically helpful.** The phase $\sqrt{Xhm}$ is Hessian-degenerate. That blocks generic full-rank tools and pushes the problem into discrete spacing or special algebraic resonance counting, not into a simpler endpoint estimate.

6. **A3 likely underestimates the M9b theorem-extension gap.** In the Li--Yang form, a coefficient $g(h/H)$ usually has controlled bounded variation on a continuous interval. A periodic sequence $\chi_4(h)$ cannot simply be inserted into such a weight with a uniform BV norm. Splitting $h=4q+r$ gives phases of the form

$$
e((q+r/4)X/d),
\qquad r\in\{1,3\},
$$

or an arithmetic-progression $h$-sum. Either route needs an explicit theorem variant. The prior H5b-Shift gap should remain open.

7. **A3's C2-SPU nonstationary bound may be overcompressed.** A bound like

$$
|I(-m)|\le C_N D\min(1,\Lambda^{-N},(mD)^{-N})
$$

requires a uniform lower bound for the derivative on the support after excluding transition zones. It should be proved with a partition of unity and explicit derivative lower bounds.

8. **No actual numerical data have been produced.** A3 gives good scripts/protocols, but the repo still needs tables for R5 and M9. These tests are not optional now that M9 is the main bottleneck.

9. **A3's Mellin--Perron/Hecke exploration is too vague for theorem use.** Any subconvexity or moment input must apply to the full Dirichlet-series product $4\zeta(s)L(s,\chi_4)$ with the correct Perron kernel, contour height, and truncation error. A fixed Dirichlet $L$-function alone is not a normalized replacement for the circle problem.
