## Claims that look correct

1. **H4 integer-jump coverage** (A1). At integers, the Vaaler polynomial vanishes, $K_H(0)/(2H+2)=1/2$, and $|\psi(n)|=1/2$; hence the Fejer majorant exactly bridges the convention difference.
2. **R5 blockwise bound** (A1). The Fejer pointwise estimate $K_H(t)\ll\min(H,1/(H\|t\|^2))$ and the product-counting argument are valid, and the bound $\sum_{n}\min(1,\Delta^2/|X-n|^2)\ll\Delta+1$ is elementary.
3. **Bridge theorem** (A1). The proof correctly sums over dyadic blocks and absorbs the $\log X$ factor.
4. **Q1-Spectral equality** (A2). For any Hermitian $K$, $\|U^*KU\|_{\text{op}}=\|K\|_{\text{op}}$ because $U$ is unitary.
5. **H12 trace invariance** (A2). Cyclic permutation gives $\operatorname{Tr}((U^*KU)^m)=\operatorname{Tr}(K^m)$.
6. **C3 translation collapse** (A2). For integer $q$, $\sigma(\xi)\sigma(\xi+q)=\tfrac14(-1)^{2q}= \tfrac14$, independent of $\xi$.
7. **H13 Gauss factor** (A2). $e(n/4)-e(3n/4)=2i\chi_4(n)$ is exact.
8. **H13 Hessian degeneracy** (A2). $\det\nabla^2\Phi=0$ for $\Phi=\sqrt{Xhm}$; this blocks generic full-rank stationary phase or decoupling on that phase.

## Claims that need proof

- **A1 -- H4 final printed-source normalisation.** The translation from Vaaler's $N,j_N,k_N,\widehat J_{N+1}$ to the repo's $H,\alpha_{h,H},K_H$ is plausible, but the proof draft still lacks an exact page/equation citation that confirms the coefficient formula, the sign, and the residual constant. Without that, H4 remains an external theorem dependency awaiting a page-level check.
- **A1 -- M9b theorem-extensibility.** The shifted-phase formulation is algebraically correct, but it has not been shown that Li--Yang (or any Bombieri-Iwaniec variant) accepts $F_{\rho,D}(z)$ with a $D,X$-dependent constant term, nor that the $h$-weights derived from the Vaaler coefficients $\alpha_{h,H_D}$ satisfy the required bounded-variation hypotheses. This is an open theorem-extension gap.
- **A2 -- Post-transform H13 collapse is not a theorem.** A2 argues that applying Cauchy--Schwarz after the H13 transform again produces a diagonal unitary conjugation, hence repeats the Q1-Spectral blindness. This is a plausible *diagnostic*, but it assumes a specific reduction order. A different bilinear or signed-spacing step might avoid the trap; no exhaustive proof is given.
- **A2 -- BSOS and CRI as effective statistics.** Both are proposed objects, but neither is shown to imply a bound on M9. The CRI statistic, while algebraically derived from the expansion of $|S_1-S_3|^2$, is only partially defined (the cross-term weight is $|\alpha_h|$ while the full squared expansion contains $\alpha_h\overline{\alpha_{h'}}$, etc.). No estimate linking CRI to $X^{1/4+\epsilon}$ is offered.
- **A2 -- Global obstruction language.** Several phrasings (e.g. "axiomatic collapse", "permanently erased") slightly overstate the diagnostic's scope. The reviews correctly ask for downgrading these to conditional or restrictive statements; this has not been fully executed in the present A2 output.

## Possible errors or hidden assumptions

- **A1 -- Integer tie-breaking in R5.** The proof chooses the nearest integer to $X/d$ arbitrarily when two are equally close; the estimate $\|X/d\|\asymp|X-md|/D$ uses the fact that $|X-md|\le d/2$, so the implied constant remains unchanged regardless of tie rule. This is harmless.
- **A1 -- Dyadic weights signed vs. positive.** The residual proof uses $|w_D|$ and bounded overlap, so no cancellation among dyadic blocks is needed. The assumption that $w_D$ is a partition of unity with bounded overlap is standard.
- **A1 -- H4 constant $C$ vs. $(2H+2)^{-1}$.** A1 writes $|R_H(t)|\le (2H+2)^{-1}K_H(t)$. If the printed Vaaler constant is, say, $(2N)^{-1}$, the $O(1/H)$ scaling is still sufficient for R5, so this is not critical for the exponent-level bound. Nevertheless, the exact constant should be matched for a rigorous proof draft.
- **A2 -- Over-strong conclusion from C3.** The claim that odd dilations necessarily destroy parity oscillation is correct in the model, but the mapping to actual spacing matrices (where the dual variable may not be dilated by an odd integer in a simple way) is not made. The diagnostic should be kept conditional.
- **A2 -- Q1-Spectral only covers a specific reduction.** The proof that $\|U^*KU\|_{\text{op}}=\|K\|_{\text{op}}$ is valid, but the statement "any method that first places $\chi_4(d)$ into a diagonal conjugation and then estimates by operator norm" is not a theorem that this is the *only* way standard methods can be arranged. Some methods (e.g. double large sieve) may use other bilinear forms that do not factor through a single diagonal unitary; the diagnostic's scope must be carefully delineated -- as A2 partly does in the "Signed-Form Methods Not Blocked" list, but the earlier summary language is stronger.
- **A2 -- H13 transform: sign of $\alpha$ in the dual sum.** In the Poisson derivation, A2 obtains $\frac{i}{2}\sum_m \chi_4(m) \int ...$. The constant $i/2$ depends on the numbering of the Fourier transform conventions; this does not affect the asymptotic scaling, but one must be consistent when later combining with Vaaler coefficients.

## Suggested synthesis

- Retain A1's proof-infrastructure as the canonical reduction. Upgrade R5-Full from "provisionally proved" to "proved conditional on H4" after the H4 source citation is supplied.
- Insert A2's Q1-Spectral and C3 as **exact, bounded-scope guardrail lemmas** rather than global impossibility theorems. Every proposed estimate for M9 must be checked against the Q1-Spectral filter: if the argument reduces to an operator-norm bound on a matrix where $\chi_4$ enters only as $U^*KU$, the estimate is character-blind and cannot beat known divisor-problem exponents.
- Keep A2's H13 transform as an exploratory device, but accept that, under the most natural post-transform Cauchy--Schwarz, it recovers the same blindness. Future work on H13 should attempt a signed-spacing or cross-residue statistic that avoids the $U^*KU$ pattern.
