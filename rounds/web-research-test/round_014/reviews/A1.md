## Possible errors or hidden assumptions

A2 overstates the scope of Q1-Spectral in several places. The proved statement is about norm extraction after a specific conjugacy structure appears. It does not rule out direct estimates of

$$
\sum_{d_1,d_2}\chi_4(d_1)\chi_4(d_2)w(d_1)w(d_2)K_{d_1,d_2}
$$

before spectral majorization, nor does it rule out a Bombieri--Iwaniec dissection that tracks signed residue classes before Cauchy--Schwarz.

A2's use of "standard decoupling" is under-specified. Decoupling estimates do not always present themselves as a simple operator norm on the same $d$-space. The correct phrasing is: any decoupling or spacing argument that reduces the signed form to a unitarily invariant norm of $U^*KU$ is character-blind.

A2's C3 even-dilation case is algebraically interesting but not yet connected to an actual M9 or H13 variable map. It should be labeled a parity toy model unless the next round identifies a concrete spacing transformation where the even-dilation branch occurs naturally.

A2's H13 "recursive Q1" conclusion should not be read as a failure of the transform itself. It only falsifies the immediate route "H13 then Cauchy/operator norm." A signed dual spacing statistic could still be non-conjugacy and should be tested.

A3's R5 numerical work is too small to support any asymptotic inference. The shifted-leg example explicitly exceeds $X^{1/4}$ at $X=16$ and is saved only by constants. That is not a defect, but the write-up should call it a toy arithmetic check, not a validation of R5.

A3's stationary-phase nonstationary bound

$$
|I(\xi)|\ll_A D(1+|\xi|)^{-A}
$$

is not dimensionally satisfactory as a uniform statement in the scaled variables. The decay should depend on the scaled derivative size after $u=Dv$, not only on $|\xi|$.

A3's short-dual-length comment "$k\ll D^2/X\ll 1$" is not uniformly correct in the allowed range. If $D\asymp X^{1/2}$, then $D^2/X\asymp1$, and the small-$k$ boundary is exactly where separate treatment is needed.

A3's assertion that Li--Yang's printed condition $M<T^{-7/16}$ is a typo may be right, but it should be presented as a source-audit issue unless the TeX and subsequent derivation unambiguously confirm the intended positive exponent.

Both A2 and A3 rely on small toy computations for CRI, Q1, R5, or M9. These computations are useful for debugging formulas but do not yet provide evidence about the endpoint range $D\asymp X^{1/2}$, $H_D\asymp X^{1/4}$.
