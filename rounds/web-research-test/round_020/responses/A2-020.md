## Summary

This report executes the Round 20 reasoning tasks assigned to A2, focusing on the exact finite-statistic framework for the fixed-coefficient Vaaler main sums $\mathcal{M}_1$ and $\mathcal{M}_2$. The primary contribution is the derivation of the `BSOS-M9a` target under a frozen $|\alpha_h|$-weighted Cauchy-Schwarz normalization, showing that an off-diagonal bound of $X^{1/2+\epsilon}$ is sufficient for $\mathcal{M}_1$. Concurrently, a structural obstruction is identified for $\mathcal{M}_2$ (`M9b`): the phase shift $\rho/4$ cancels exactly in the off-diagonal Cauchy-Schwarz kernel over $h$, reducing the analyzed route to an unsigned reciprocal sum with the same character-blind structure as the Dirichlet divisor problem. The known divisor exponent $\theta^* \approx 0.314$ is therefore a warning sign unless an applicable theorem beats the corresponding divisor-type barrier. Furthermore, the diagonal-sizing analysis shows that the two analyzed character-preserving Cauchy-Schwarz alternatives (over $d$, or over the dual variable $k$ after a B-process) yield diagonal contributions of $X$ and $X^{3/4}$ respectively, both above the required $X^{1/2}$ threshold. This gives a serious double-bind for the current $\mathcal{M}_2$ route. The `Q1-Spectral` and `H12-Trace` diagnostics are formalized narrowly to specify exactly which operator-norm and cyclic-trace methods are character-blind. Finally, the `H13` dual transform is analyzed to show that it maps the spatial character into a dual diagonal unitary conjugation, recreating the `Q1-Spectral` trap on the dual side if standard norm estimates are applied.

## Main claim or direction

The fixed-coefficient main sum $\mathcal{M}_1(D;X)$ admits a sufficient finite signed-statistic target (`BSOS-M9a`) because Cauchy-Schwarz over $h$ yields an admissible diagonal of size $D \log H_D \ll X^{1/2+\epsilon}$ while preserving the spatial character $\chi_4(d_1)\chi_4(d_2)$. Conversely, the frequency-character sum $\mathcal{M}_2(D;X)$ is subject to a double-bind (`M9b-Diagonal-Obstruction`) for the standard spacing routes analyzed here: Cauchy-Schwarz over $h$ yields an admissible diagonal but erases $\chi_4(h)$ and cancels the $\rho$ shift, reducing the off-diagonal to a character-blind divisor-type problem; Cauchy-Schwarz over $d$ preserves $\chi_4(h)$ but yields a diagonal of size $D^2 \asymp X$; and Cauchy-Schwarz over the dual variable $k$ after a B-process yields a diagonal of size $H_D X / D \asymp X^{3/4}$. Thus the present analysis does not supply a viable $X^{1/4+\epsilon}$ bound for $\mathcal{M}_2$; it points instead toward a genuinely 2D cancellation mechanism or a non-conjugacy trace statistic.

## Detailed reasoning

### 1. Derivation of the BSOS-M9a Target

We freeze the $|\alpha_h|$-weighted Cauchy-Schwarz normalization for $\mathcal{M}_1(D;X)$. The target is to prove $\mathcal{M}_1(D;X) \ll_\epsilon X^{1/4+\epsilon}$ for $X^{1/4} \le D \le X^{1/2}$ and $H_D \asymp D X^{-1/4}$.
The sum is defined as:
$$
\mathcal{M}_1(D;X) = -4 \sum_{1 \le |h| \le H_D} \alpha_{h,H_D} \sum_{d \asymp D} \chi_4(d) w_D(d) e(hX/d)
$$
Let $S(h) = \sum_{d \asymp D} \chi_4(d) w_D(d) e(hX/d)$. Applying Cauchy-Schwarz over $h$ with weights $|\alpha_{h,H_D}|$:
$$
|\mathcal{M}_1(D;X)|^2 \le 16 \left( \sum_{1 \le |h| \le H_D} |\alpha_{h,H_D}| \right) \sum_{1 \le |h| \le H_D} |\alpha_{h,H_D}| |S(h)|^2
$$
Define the logarithmic factor $L = \sum_{1 \le |h| \le H_D} |\alpha_{h,H_D}|$. Since $\alpha_{h,H_D} = -\frac{\Phi(|h|/(H_D+1))}{2\pi i h}$ and $\Phi(u)$ is bounded and bounded away from zero for small $u$, we have $|\alpha_{h,H_D}| \asymp 1/|h|$, yielding $L \asymp \log H_D \ll \log X$.
Expanding $|S(h)|^2$:
$$
|S(h)|^2 = \sum_{d_1, d_2 \asymp D} \chi_4(d_1)\chi_4(d_2) w_D(d_1)w_D(d_2) e\left(hX\left(\frac{1}{d_1} - \frac{1}{d_2}\right)\right)
$$
Substituting this into the outer sum and exchanging the order of summation:
$$
\sum_{1 \le |h| \le H_D} |\alpha_{h,H_D}| |S(h)|^2 = \sum_{d_1, d_2 \asymp D} \chi_4(d_1)\chi_4(d_2) K_{d_1, d_2}^{(|\alpha|)}
$$
where the frozen Cauchy-Schwarz kernel is defined as:
$$
K_{d_1, d_2}^{(|\alpha|)} = w_D(d_1)w_D(d_2) \sum_{1 \le |h| \le H_D} |\alpha_{h,H_D}| e\left(hX\left(\frac{1}{d_1} - \frac{1}{d_2}\right)\right)
$$
We partition this quadratic form into diagonal ($d_1 = d_2$) and off-diagonal ($d_1 \ne d_2$) components.
For the diagonal, $d_1 = d_2 \implies e(0) = 1$, so $K_{d, d}^{(|\alpha|)} = w_D(d)^2 L$. The diagonal contribution is:
$$
\mathcal{S}_{\rm diag} = \sum_{d \asymp D} \chi_4(d)^2 w_D(d)^2 L
$$
Since $\chi_4(d)^2 = 1$ for odd $d$ and $0$ for even $d$, and $w_D(d)$ is a smooth dyadic weight, $\mathcal{S}_{\rm diag} \asymp D L \asymp D \log H_D$.
The off-diagonal signed statistic is defined as:
$$
\mathcal{S}_{\rm signed} = \sum_{d_1 \ne d_2} \chi_4(d_1)\chi_4(d_2) K_{d_1, d_2}^{(|\alpha|)}
$$
Thus, the Cauchy-Schwarz inequality yields:
$$
|\mathcal{M}_1(D;X)|^2 \le 16 L (\mathcal{S}_{\rm diag} + \mathcal{S}_{\rm signed}) \ll L (D L + |\mathcal{S}_{\rm signed}|) \ll D L^2 + L |\mathcal{S}_{\rm signed}|
$$
Since $D \le X^{1/2}$ and $L \asymp \log X$, the diagonal term is bounded by $D L^2 \ll X^{1/2} (\log X)^2 \ll X^{1/2+\epsilon}$.
To achieve the target $|\mathcal{M}_1(D;X)| \ll_\epsilon X^{1/4+\epsilon}$, we require $|\mathcal{M}_1(D;X)|^2 \ll_\epsilon X^{1/2+2\epsilon}$. This holds if and only if the off-diagonal term satisfies $L |\mathcal{S}_{\rm signed}| \ll_\epsilon X^{1/2+2\epsilon}$, which reduces to:
$$
|\mathcal{S}_{\rm signed}| \ll_\epsilon X^{1/2+\epsilon}
$$
This proves that the `BSOS-M9a` target is rigorously sufficient under the frozen $|\alpha_h|$-weighted normalization.

### 2. The M9b Shifted-F Cancellation

We now construct the analogous framework for $\mathcal{M}_2(D;X)$. Using the shifted-$F$ formulation, the sum is a linear combination of terms of the form:
$$
\mathcal{M}_{2,\rho}(D;X) = \sum_{1 \le |h| \le H_D} \alpha_{h,H_D} \sum_{d \asymp D} w_D(d) e\left(h\left(\frac{X}{4d} + \frac{\rho}{4}\right)\right)
$$
for $\rho \in \{1, 3\}$. Let $B_\rho(h) = \sum_{d \asymp D} w_D(d) e(h(X/(4d) + \rho/4))$. Applying Cauchy-Schwarz over $h$ with weights $|\alpha_{h,H_D}|$:
$$
|\mathcal{M}_{2,\rho}(D;X)|^2 \le L \sum_{1 \le |h| \le H_D} |\alpha_{h,H_D}| |B_\rho(h)|^2
$$
Expanding $|B_\rho(h)|^2$:
$$
|B_\rho(h)|^2 = \sum_{d_1, d_2 \asymp D} w_D(d_1)w_D(d_2) e\left(h\left(\frac{X}{4d_1} + \frac{\rho}{4}\right) - h\left(\frac{X}{4d_2} + \frac{\rho}{4}\right)\right)
$$
The phase difference simplifies exactly:
$$
h\left(\frac{X}{4d_1} + \frac{\rho}{4}\right) - h\left(\frac{X}{4d_2} + \frac{\rho}{4}\right) = \frac{hX}{4d_1} - \frac{hX}{4d_2}
$$
The parameter $\rho$ cancels completely. The resulting off-diagonal kernel is:
$$
K_{d_1, d_2}^{(|\alpha|, \text{unshifted})} = w_D(d_1)w_D(d_2) \sum_{1 \le |h| \le H_D} |\alpha_{h,H_D}| e\left(\frac{hX}{4}\left(\frac{1}{d_1} - \frac{1}{d_2}\right)\right)
$$
The off-diagonal statistic is purely unsigned:
$$
\mathcal{S}_{\rm unsigned} = \sum_{d_1 \ne d_2} K_{d_1, d_2}^{(|\alpha|, \text{unshifted})}
$$
Because the spatial character $\chi_4(d)$ is absent, this has the same character-blind reciprocal structure as the off-diagonal sum for the Dirichlet divisor problem (evaluated at $X/4$). The relevant theorem-hypothesis check is whether a Li-Yang type divisor estimate, with exponent $\theta^* \approx 0.314$, applies to this exact weighted reciprocal form. If the best available input only gives the divisor-type strength suggested by $\theta^*$, the resulting squared-statistic scale is above the required $X^{1/2+\epsilon}$ target. Therefore, Cauchy-Schwarz over $h$ for $\mathcal{M}_2$ does not by itself overcome the divisor barrier.

### 3. The M9b Diagonal Obstruction

To bypass the divisor barrier for $\mathcal{M}_2$, one must preserve the frequency character $\chi_4(h)$. This requires applying Cauchy-Schwarz over a variable other than $h$. We analyze the two available alternatives:

**Alternative A: Cauchy-Schwarz over $d$.**
Rewrite the sum as $\mathcal{M}_2 = 8i \sum_{d \asymp D} w_D(d) \sum_{1 \le |h| \le H_D} \alpha_{h,H_D} \chi_4(h) e(hX/(4d))$.
Applying Cauchy-Schwarz over $d$:
$$
|\mathcal{M}_2|^2 \le \left( \sum_{d \asymp D} w_D(d) \right) \sum_{d \asymp D} w_D(d) \left| \sum_{1 \le |h| \le H_D} \alpha_{h,H_D} \chi_4(h) e\left(\frac{hX}{4d}\right) \right|^2
$$
The outer multiplier is $\sum_d w_D(d) \asymp D$. The diagonal of the inner squared sum occurs when $h_1 = h_2$, yielding:
$$
\sum_{1 \le |h| \le H_D} |\alpha_{h,H_D}|^2 \chi_4(h)^2 \sum_{d \asymp D} w_D(d) \asymp D \sum_{h} \frac{1}{h^2} \asymp D
$$
The total diagonal contribution to $|\mathcal{M}_2|^2$ is $D \times D = D^2$. At the endpoint $D \asymp X^{1/2}$, the diagonal is $D^2 \asymp X$. This strictly exceeds the target $X^{1/2+\epsilon}$.

**Alternative B: B-process to dual variable $k$, then Cauchy-Schwarz over $k$.**
Apply Poisson summation over $d$ to the inner sum $B(h) = \sum_d w_D(d) e(hX/(4d))$. The stationary phase approximation yields a dual sum over $k$:
$$
B(h) \approx \sum_{k \asymp K_{\rm dual}} c_{h,k} e(\sqrt{hXk})
$$
where the stationary point is $u_0 = \frac{1}{2}\sqrt{hX/k}$, the dual length is $K_{\rm dual} \asymp hX/D^2$, and the amplitude satisfies $|c_{h,k}|^2 \asymp (hX)^{1/2} k^{-3/2}$.
The main sum becomes $\mathcal{M}_2 \approx \sum_h \alpha_h \chi_4(h) \sum_k c_{h,k} e(\sqrt{hXk})$. Exchanging sums and applying Cauchy-Schwarz over $k$:
$$
|\mathcal{M}_2|^2 \le \left( \sum_{k \asymp K_{\rm max}} 1 \right) \sum_k \left| \sum_h \alpha_h \chi_4(h) c_{h,k} e(\sqrt{hXk}) \right|^2
$$
The outer multiplier is the maximum dual length $K_{\rm max} \asymp H_D X / D^2$. The diagonal of the inner squared sum ($h_1 = h_2$) is:
$$
\sum_h |\alpha_h|^2 \chi_4(h)^2 \sum_k |c_{h,k}|^2
$$
By Parseval's identity, the $L^2$ norm of the dual amplitude matches the primal weight: $\sum_k |c_{h,k}|^2 \asymp \sum_d w_D(d)^2 \asymp D$. Thus, the inner diagonal sum is $\sum_h |\alpha_h|^2 D \asymp D$.
The total diagonal contribution to $|\mathcal{M}_2|^2$ is $K_{\rm max} \times D \asymp (H_D X / D^2) D = H_D X / D$.
At the endpoint $D \asymp X^{1/2}$ and $H_D \asymp X^{1/4}$, the diagonal is $X^{1/4} X / X^{1/2} = X^{3/4}$. This strictly exceeds the target $X^{1/2+\epsilon}$.

Conclusion: In the two character-preserving Cauchy-Schwarz variants analyzed here, the diagonal contribution is at least $X^{3/4}$ at the upper endpoint. This establishes a concrete double-bind for the present $\mathcal{M}_2$ strategy.

### 4. Q1-Spectral and H12-Trace on M9a

We formalize the operator-norm diagnostic for the exact M9a kernel. Let $\mathcal{D}_{\rm odd} = \{d : D \le d < 2D, 2 \nmid d\}$ and define the Hilbert space $\mathcal{V}_D = \ell^2(\mathcal{D}_{\rm odd})$. Define the unitary operator $U : \mathcal{V}_D \to \mathcal{V}_D$ by $(Uv)_d = \chi_4(d) v_d$. Since $\chi_4(d) \in \{\pm 1\}$ for odd $d$, $U^* = U$ and $U^* U = I$.
The signed off-diagonal statistic is $\mathcal{S}_{\rm signed} = \sum_{d_1 \ne d_2} \chi_4(d_1)\chi_4(d_2) K_{d_1, d_2}^{(|\alpha|)}$. Let $v \in \mathcal{V}_D$ be the vector with $v_d = 1$ for all $d$. Then:
$$
\mathcal{S}_{\rm signed} = v^T U^* K_{\rm off}^{(|\alpha|)} U v
$$
If a method bounds this bilinear form by passing to the operator norm, we have:
$$
|v^T U^* K_{\rm off}^{(|\alpha|)} U v| \le \|v\|^2 \|U^* K_{\rm off}^{(|\alpha|)} U\|_{\rm op} = D \|K_{\rm off}^{(|\alpha|)}\|_{\rm op}
$$
Because the operator norm is unitarily invariant, the character $U$ is erased from any estimate that factors only through $\|U^* K U\|_{\rm op}$. The same issue applies to spectral-radius, Frobenius-norm, and entrywise absolute-value matrix bounds when they discard the signed bilinear form and retain only conjugacy-invariant or absolute data.
Similarly, for pure cyclic traces:
$$
\operatorname{Tr}((U^* K_{\rm off}^{(|\alpha|)} U)^m) = \operatorname{Tr}(U^* (K_{\rm off}^{(|\alpha|)})^m U) = \operatorname{Tr}((K_{\rm off}^{(|\alpha|)})^m U U^*) = \operatorname{Tr}((K_{\rm off}^{(|\alpha|)})^m)
$$
This proves that pure conjugacy-invariant trace statistics are character-blind. However, this diagnostic does not block open-path moments (e.g., $v^T (U^* K U)^m v$) or non-conjugacy signed forms.

### 5. H13-Dual and the Q1 Trap

The H13 transform applies Poisson summation modulo 4 to the spatial-character sum $\mathcal{M}_1$. The formal transform yields a dual sum over $m$ with a dual character $\chi_4(m)$ and a stationary phase $\sqrt{Xhm}$.
If the first post-transform spacing step applies Cauchy-Schwarz over $h$, the resulting dual kernel is:
$$
\widetilde{K}_{m_1, m_2} = \sum_h |\alpha_h| c_{h,m_1} \overline{c_{h,m_2}} e\left(\sqrt{hX}(\sqrt{m_1} - \sqrt{m_2})\right)
$$
The signed dual statistic is $\sum_{m_1 \ne m_2} \chi_4(m_1)\chi_4(m_2) \widetilde{K}_{m_1, m_2}$. This has the exact same structure as the primal M9a statistic: it can be written as $\widetilde{v}^T \widetilde{U}^* \widetilde{K}_{\rm off} \widetilde{U} \widetilde{v}$ where $\widetilde{U} = \operatorname{diag}(\chi_4(m))$.
Therefore, if the subsequent estimate relies on the operator norm $\|\widetilde{U}^* \widetilde{K}_{\rm off} \widetilde{U}\|_{\rm op}$, the `Q1-Spectral` diagnostic applies identically to the dual side, and the dual character is erased. H13 appears viable only if the post-transform step utilizes a non-conjugacy signed statistic.

## Hidden assumptions and potential gaps

1.  **Diagonal Sum Exactness:** The `BSOS-M9a` implication assumes the diagonal sum $\sum_{d \text{ odd}} w_D(d)^2$ is exactly $\asymp D$. If the dyadic weights $w_D(d)$ have sharp boundary transitions, the implied constants could inflate.
2.  **Divisor Barrier Universality:** The `M9b-CS-Blindness` obstruction assumes no special cancellation exists for the specific fractional shifts $\rho/4$ in the unsigned reciprocal sum. If the shift interacts favorably with the $h$ summation, the $\theta^*$ barrier might be bypassed, though this is highly unlikely for character-blind sums.
3.  **H13 Dual Length Dominance:** The dual length $m \asymp hX/D^2$ assumes the stationary point dominates the integral completely. Nonstationary integration-by-parts tails could introduce boundary terms that alter the Cauchy-Schwarz diagonal sizing.
4.  **Q1-Spectral Scope:** The diagnostic assumes the spacing method factors strictly through the operator norm or cyclic trace. Methods that evaluate the bilinear form directly against the specific vector $v$ are not blocked.
5.  **Parseval Amplitude Uniformity:** The application of Parseval's identity to the B-process amplitude $c_{h,k}$ assumes the stationary phase approximation is uniformly valid across the entire support of $w_D(u)$, which may fail near the endpoints of the dyadic interval.

The primary obstruction identified in this round is the `M9b-Diagonal-Obstruction`. It is a strong obstruction to the analyzed proof strategies that attempt to bound $\mathcal{M}_2(D;X)$ by applying Cauchy-Schwarz in a way that preserves the frequency character $\chi_4(h)$. The critical question is whether the resulting dual kernel $\widetilde{K}_{m_1, m_2}$ admits a signed bilinear estimate that avoids the `Q1-Spectral` trap.
To explore this, we propose evaluating the open-path moment $v^T \widetilde{U}^* \widetilde{K} \widetilde{U} v$ directly, rather than passing to the operator norm. This requires a discrete spacing theorem tailored to square-root phases, which is a known but difficult area of exponential sum theory.
