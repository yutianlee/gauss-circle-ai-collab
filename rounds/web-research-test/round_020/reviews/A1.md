A2's M9b obstruction needs a more exact kernel-level proof. In the shifted-$F$ decomposition, applying Cauchy--Schwarz separately to the two shifted pieces indeed cancels $\rho/4$ inside $|B_\rho(h)|^2$. But the actual $\mathcal M_2$ is a difference of two shifted pieces with the same Vaaler coefficient. A cleaner first-step computation is

$$
\mathcal M_2
=

\left(e(h/4)-e(3h/4)\right)

$$

so after $|\alpha_h|$-weighted Cauchy--Schwarz one obtains a factor

$$

$$

which is $4$ for odd $h$ and $0$ for even $h$. Thus the shift is indeed lost, but the exact resulting kernel has an odd-$h$ restriction and a factor $4$. This should be written explicitly before comparing the problem to divisor-type reciprocal sums.

## Possible errors or hidden assumptions

A2's phrasing around M9b sometimes sounds stronger than the proof supports. The current output shows a strong obstruction to several standard Cauchy--Schwarz routes. It does not prove that all routes to $\mathcal M_2$ fail.

A2's BSOS-M9a implication assumes the dyadic weights satisfy

$$
\sum_{d\in \mathcal D_{\rm odd}} w_D(d)^2\asymp D
$$

with uniform constants. This is standard for a well-chosen smooth dyadic partition, but it should be stated as a hypothesis.

A2's divisor-barrier language assumes the character-blind kernel after $\rho/4$ cancellation has no special cancellation from the odd-$h$ restriction, fractional shifts, or fixed Vaaler weights. This is plausible, but it must be checked against actual divisor-type theorems rather than inferred from the record exponent alone.

A2's H13 analysis assumes the stationary phase approximation dominates uniformly. Endpoint support, transition ranges, and nonstationary integration-by-parts tails may change diagonal sizes. The output itself lists this as a hidden assumption, and that caution should be preserved.

A3's H4 source table should avoid over-labeling $j_N$ as a de la Vallee Poussin kernel unless this terminology is explicitly in the source or proved equivalent. It is safer to keep Vaaler's notation: $j_N$ is the periodic kernel built from $J_{N+1}$, and $k_N$ is the Fejer kernel.

A3's exact-resonance handling in R5 should be written with constants. At an exact resonance, $K_H(0)=H+1$, so

$$
\frac{1}{H}K_H(0)=1+\frac1H,
$$

which is $O(1)$, not literally bounded by $W_\Delta(0)=1$ unless the implicit constant is retained.
