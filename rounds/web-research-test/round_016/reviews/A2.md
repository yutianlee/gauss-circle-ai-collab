This algebraic manipulation perfectly matches the complex conjugate $\overline{\alpha_{h,H}} = \overline{\frac{i \Phi(h/(H+1))}{2\pi h}} = -\frac{i \Phi(h/(H+1))}{2\pi h}$. The equality holds identically.

$$
-4 \sum_{1 \le |h| \le H} \alpha_{h,H} A_h = -4 \sum_{h=1}^H (\alpha_{h,H} A_h + \alpha_{-h,H} A_{-h})
$$
Substituting the established conjugacy relations yields $\alpha_{h,H} A_h + \overline{\alpha_{h,H}} \overline{A_h} = 2 \Re(\alpha_{h,H} A_h)$.
Evaluating the real part:
$$
2 \Re\left(\frac{i \Phi}{2\pi h} (\Re A_h + i \Im A_h)\right) = 2 \Re\left(\frac{i \Phi \Re A_h - \Phi \Im A_h}{2\pi h}\right) = 2 \left( -\frac{\Phi}{2\pi h} \Im A_h \right) = -\frac{\Phi}{\pi h} \Im A_h
