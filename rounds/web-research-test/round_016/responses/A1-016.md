## Detailed reasoning

### 1. H4 source-normalization

Use Vaaler's periodic majorant theorem as the H4 source.

Vaaler's paper is Jeffrey D. Vaaler, "Some extremal functions in Fourier analysis," *Bulletin of the American Mathematical Society* 12(2), 183--216, 1985; the uploaded source shows the publication metadata on printed page 183.

The source anchors are:

- Vaaler Theorem 6, printed page 192, equation (2.28): the Fourier transform of $J$ is

$$
\widehat J(t)
=
\pi t(1-|t|)\cot(\pi t)+|t|,
\qquad 0<|t|<1,
$$

and $\widehat J(t)=0$ for $|t|\ge 1$. The snippet also records that $J$ is even, nonnegative, continuously differentiable, and strictly decreasing on $[0,1]$.

- Vaaler Section 7, printed page 207, equations (7.1)--(7.3): periodic trigonometric polynomials $i_N,j_N,k_N$ are defined using Poisson summation, and $k_N$ is identified as the periodic Fejer kernel.

- Vaaler Theorem 18, printed page 210, equations (7.13)--(7.17), especially (7.14): the polynomial $\psi*j_N$ satisfies the residual inequality

$$
|\psi*j_N(x)-\psi(x)|\le (2N+2)^{-1}k_N(x).
$$

This is the exact majorant form needed for the repo.

Translate Vaaler's notation into the repo notation as follows.

Set

$$
H=N.
$$

The periodic Fejer kernel is

$$
K_H(t)=k_H(t)
=
\sum_{|k|\le H}
\left(1-\frac{|k|}{H+1}\right)e(kt)
=
\frac{1}{H+1}
\left(
\frac{\sin \pi(H+1)t}{\sin \pi t}
\right)^2.
$$

For $0<u<1$, define

$$
\Phi(u)=\pi u(1-u)\cot(\pi u)+u.
$$

Since $\widehat J(t)$ is even, the $h$th coefficient in $\psi*j_H$ is

$$
-\frac{\Phi(|h|/(H+1))}{2\pi i h},
\qquad 1\le |h|\le H.
$$

Thus the repo H4 statement should be:

$$
\psi_C(t)
=
\sum_{1\le |h|\le H}
\alpha_{h,H}e(ht)+R_H(t),
$$

where $\psi_C$ is the centered normalized sawtooth,

$$
\alpha_{h,H}
=
-\frac{\Phi(|h|/(H+1))}{2\pi i h},
$$

and

$$
|R_H(t)|\le \frac{1}{2H+2}K_H(t).
$$

The coefficient conjugacy is

$$
\alpha_{-h,H}=\overline{\alpha_{h,H}},
$$

not $-\overline{\alpha_{h,H}}$.

For $h>0$,

$$
\alpha_{h,H}
=
\frac{i}{2\pi h}\Phi\left(\frac{h}{H+1}\right).
$$

The floor-compatible sawtooth used in the arithmetic identity is

$$
\psi_F(t)=t-\lfloor t\rfloor-\frac12,
$$

so at integers

$$
\psi_F(n)=-\frac12.
$$

Vaaler's centered periodic sawtooth has midpoint value $0$ at discontinuities. This does not invalidate H4 for $\psi_F$, because at an integer $n$ the Vaaler polynomial has value $0$, while

$$
|\psi_F(n)-0|=\frac12.
$$

Also

$$
K_H(0)=H+1,
$$

so

$$
\frac{K_H(0)}{2H+2}
=
\frac12.
$$

Thus the residual majorant exactly covers the half-jump between the centered convention and the floor-compatible convention.

### 2. R5-Full proof-draft lemma

State R5-Full as follows.

**Lemma R5-Full.** Let $X\ge 2$, let

$$
X^{1/4}\le D\le X^{1/2},
$$

and let $H$ satisfy

$$
H\asymp D X^{-1/4}.
$$

Let $w_D$ be a dyadic weight supported on $d\asymp D$, with $|w_D(d)|\le 1$. Then

$$
\frac1H
\sum_{d\asymp D}
|w_D(d)|K_H(X/d)
\ll_\epsilon X^{1/4+\epsilon},
$$

and for $\rho\in\{1,3\}$,

$$
\frac1H
\sum_{d\asymp D}
|w_D(d)|
K_H\left(\frac{X/d+\rho}{4}\right)
\ll_\epsilon X^{1/4+\epsilon}.
$$

Consequently, assuming H4, the full Vaaler residual arising from both sawtooth legs contributes

$$
\ll_\epsilon X^{1/4+\epsilon}
$$

after summing over all dyadic $D$.

**Proof.**

The standard Fejer kernel bound is

$$
K_H(t)\ll \min\left(H,\frac1{H\|t\|^2}\right).
$$

Therefore

$$
\frac1H K_H(t)
\ll
\min\left(1,\frac1{H^2\|t\|^2}\right).
$$

For the first leg, choose an integer $m=m(d)$ nearest to $X/d$. If there is a tie, choose either nearest integer; the bound is unchanged. Since $d\asymp D$,

$$
\left\|\frac Xd\right\|
=
\frac{|X-md|}{d}
\asymp
\frac{|X-md|}{D}.
$$

Let

$$
\Delta=\frac{D}{H}.
$$

With the local height $H\asymp D X^{-1/4}$,

$$
\Delta\asymp X^{1/4}.
$$

Then

$$
\frac1H K_H(X/d)
\ll
\min\left(1,\frac{\Delta^2}{|X-md|^2}\right).
$$

For fixed $n=md$, the number of pairs $(m,d)$ with $d\asymp D$ is at most $\tau(n)$. Also $n=X+O(D)$, hence $n\asymp X$ for $X$ large; finite small $X$ is absorbed into the implicit constant. Therefore

$$
\frac1H
\sum_{d\asymp D}
|w_D(d)|K_H(X/d)
\ll
\sum_{n\in\mathbb Z}
\tau(|n|)
\min\left(1,\frac{\Delta^2}{|X-n|^2}\right),
$$

where the sum may be restricted to $n\asymp X$ except for harmless finite endpoint terms.

Using

$$
\tau(n)\ll_\epsilon n^\epsilon
$$

and $n\asymp X$ gives

$$
\frac1H
\sum_{d\asymp D}
|w_D(d)|K_H(X/d)
\ll_\epsilon
X^\epsilon
\sum_{n\in\mathbb Z}
\min\left(1,\frac{\Delta^2}{|X-n|^2}\right).
$$

The last one-dimensional kernel sum satisfies

$$
\sum_{n\in\mathbb Z}
\min\left(1,\frac{\Delta^2}{|X-n|^2}\right)
\ll \Delta+1.
$$

Indeed, the terms with $|X-n|\le \Delta$ contribute $O(\Delta+1)$, and the annulus

$$
2^j\Delta<|X-n|\le 2^{j+1}\Delta
$$

contributes $O(2^{-j}\Delta)$. Since $\Delta\asymp X^{1/4}$,

$$
\frac1H
\sum_{d\asymp D}
|w_D(d)|K_H(X/d)
\ll_\epsilon X^{1/4+\epsilon}.
$$

For the shifted second leg, fix $\rho\in\{1,3\}$ and choose $m$ nearest to

$$
\frac{X/d+\rho}{4}.
$$

Then

$$
\left\|
\frac{X/d+\rho}{4}
\right\|
=
\frac{|X/d+\rho-4m|}{4}
=
\frac{|X-d(4m-\rho)|}{4d}.
$$

Set

$$
\ell=4m-\rho.
$$

Then

$$
\ell\equiv-\rho\pmod 4.
$$

For $X$ sufficiently large, $\ell>0$, because $\ell\asymp X/d$. Any finite exceptional range is absorbed. The same Fejer bound yields

$$
\frac1H
K_H\left(\frac{X/d+\rho}{4}\right)
\ll
\min\left(1,\frac{\Delta^2}{|X-d\ell|^2}\right).
$$

Grouping by

$$
n=d\ell
$$

again gives at most $\tau(n)$ representations, now with a congruence restriction on $\ell$, which can only reduce multiplicity. Hence

$$
\frac1H
\sum_{d\asymp D}
|w_D(d)|
K_H\left(\frac{X/d+\rho}{4}\right)
\ll_\epsilon X^{1/4+\epsilon}.
$$

The zero Fejer mode is included in the positive kernel argument. If treated separately, it contributes

$$
\frac1H\sum_{d\asymp D}|w_D(d)|
\ll \frac{D}{H}
\asymp X^{1/4}.
$$

The nonzero positive and negative Fejer frequencies are also included because $K_H$ is real, nonnegative, and symmetric.

Dyadic weights cause no issue: use $|w_D|$ in the majorant. Bounded overlap in the dyadic partition gives at most $O(\log X)$ blocks, absorbed into $X^\epsilon$.

For $D<X^{1/4}$, do not apply Vaaler. Since $|\psi_F|\le 1/2$, the total contribution from such short denominator blocks is

$$
O(X^{1/4})
$$

up to logarithmic dyadic bookkeeping.

This proves R5-Full conditional on H4.

### 3. Bridge-R16

State the bridge as a conditional theorem.

**Bridge-R16.** Assume:

1. H1--H3: the balanced hyperbola and floor-compatible sawtooth reduction,

$$
P(X)
=
-4\sum_{d\le y}\chi_4(d)\psi_F(X/d)
+
4\sum_{d\le y}
\left[
\psi_F\left(\frac{X/d+1}{4}\right)
-
\psi_F\left(\frac{X/d+3}{4}\right)
\right]
+
O(1).
$$

2. H4: Vaaler's finite approximation with coefficients

$$
\alpha_{h,H}
=
-\frac{\Phi(|h|/(H+1))}{2\pi i h}
$$

and residual majorant

$$
|R_H(t)|\le \frac1{2H+2}K_H(t).
$$

3. R5-Full: all Fejer residuals from the dyadic Vaaler decomposition contribute

$$
\ll_\epsilon X^{1/4+\epsilon}.
$$

4. M9: for every dyadic $D$ with $X^{1/4}\le D\le X^{1/2}$,

$$
\mathcal M_1(D;X),\mathcal M_2(D;X)
\ll_\epsilon X^{1/4+\epsilon}.
$$

Then

$$
P(X)\ll_\epsilon X^{1/4+\epsilon}.
$$

**Proof.**

Split $1\le d\le y$ into short blocks $D<X^{1/4}$ and Vaaler blocks $X^{1/4}\le D\le X^{1/2}$.

Short blocks contribute

$$
\ll X^{1/4}
$$

because $|\psi_F|\le 1/2$ and the number of denominators is $O(X^{1/4})$.

On each remaining dyadic block, apply H4 with

$$
H_D\asymp D X^{-1/4}.
$$

The main terms are exactly $\mathcal M_1(D;X)$ and $\mathcal M_2(D;X)$, with smooth dyadic weights. By M9 each contributes

$$
\ll_\epsilon X^{1/4+\epsilon}
$$

per dyadic block. There are $O(\log X)$ dyadic blocks, absorbed into $X^\epsilon$.

The residual terms are bounded by R5-Full. The $O(1)$ from H1--H3 is harmless.

Therefore

$$
P(X)\ll_\epsilon X^{1/4+\epsilon}.
$$

This proves the conditional bridge, not the conjectural estimate itself.

### 4. Correct paired-frequency form of M9

Assume $w_D$ is real-valued. Define

$$
A_h(D;X)
=
\sum_{d\asymp D}
\chi_4(d)w_D(d)e(hX/d),
$$

and

$$
B_h(D;X)
=
\sum_{d\asymp D}
w_D(d)e(hX/(4d)).
$$

Then

$$
A_{-h}=\overline{A_h},
\qquad
B_{-h}=\overline{B_h}.
$$

Also

$$
\alpha_{-h,H}=\overline{\alpha_{h,H}}.
$$

For $h>0$,

$$
\alpha_{h,H}
=
\frac{i}{2\pi h}\Phi\left(\frac{h}{H+1}\right).
$$

Thus the first main term pairs as

$$
\mathcal M_1(D;X)
=
-8\sum_{1\le h\le H_D}
\Re\left(\alpha_{h,H_D}A_h(D;X)\right).
$$

Equivalently,

$$
\mathcal M_1(D;X)
=
\frac{4}{\pi}
\sum_{1\le h\le H_D}
\frac{\Phi(h/(H_D+1))}{h}
\Im A_h(D;X).
$$

For the second main term, set

$$
\gamma_h=e(h/4)-e(3h/4).
$$

Then

$$
\gamma_h=2i\chi_4(h),
$$

with $\gamma_h=0$ for even $h$, and

$$
\gamma_{-h}=\overline{\gamma_h}.
$$

Hence

$$
\mathcal M_2(D;X)
=
8\sum_{1\le h\le H_D}
\Re\left(\alpha_{h,H_D}\gamma_h B_h(D;X)\right).
$$

For odd $h$,

$$
\alpha_{h,H_D}\gamma_h
=
-\frac{\Phi(h/(H_D+1))}{\pi h}\chi_4(h),
$$

so

$$
\mathcal M_2(D;X)
=
-\frac{8}{\pi}
\sum_{\substack{1\le h\le H_D\\2\nmid h}}
\frac{\Phi(h/(H_D+1))}{h}
\chi_4(h)
\Re B_h(D;X).
$$

These paired forms should replace any formula using

$$
\alpha_{-h,H}=-\overline{\alpha_{h,H}}.
$$

That sign is wrong.

### 5. M9b shifted-$F$ theorem-extension statement

Do not absorb $\chi_4(h)$ into a bounded-variation $h$-weight unless the theorem explicitly permits periodic weights with variation $\asymp H$. The safer representation is:

$$
e(hX/(4d))(e(h/4)-e(3h/4))
=
e\left(h\left(\frac{X}{4d}+\frac14\right)\right)
-
e\left(h\left(\frac{X}{4d}+\frac34\right)\right).
$$

After scaling

$$
d=Dz,
$$

write

$$
h\left(\frac{X}{4d}+\frac{\rho}{4}\right)
=
\frac{hX}{D}F_{\rho,D}(z),
$$

where

$$
F_{\rho,D}(z)
=
\frac1{4z}+\frac{\rho D}{4X},
\qquad
\rho\in\{1,3\}.
$$

The derivatives are independent of the constant shift:

$$
F_{\rho,D}'(z)=-\frac1{4z^2},
$$

$$
F_{\rho,D}''(z)=\frac1{2z^3},
$$

$$
F_{\rho,D}'''(z)=-\frac{3}{2z^4}.
$$

Thus

$$
F_{\rho,D}'F_{\rho,D}'''-3(F_{\rho,D}'')^2
=
-\frac{3}{8z^6}.
$$

On $z\in[1,2]$, this has uniform upper and lower bounds away from $0$. Therefore the shifted-$F$ phase satisfies the same derivative nondegeneracy pattern as the unshifted reciprocal phase.

The theorem-extension target should be stated as:

**M9b-ShiftedF theorem requirement.** A usable Bombieri--Iwaniec/Li--Yang-type theorem for M9b must allow phases

$$
\frac{hX}{D}
\left(
\frac1{4z}+\frac{\rho D}{4X}
\right)
$$

uniformly for

$$
X^{1/4}\le D\le X^{1/2},
\qquad
\rho\in\{1,3\},
$$

with dyadic $h$-weights arising from

$$
\alpha_{h,H_D}
=
-\frac{\Phi(|h|/(H_D+1))}{2\pi i h}.
$$

After splitting into $h\sim H_0$, the factor $1/h$ may be written as $H_0^{-1}$ times a smooth bounded-variation weight on $[1,2]$. The theorem must therefore handle smooth dyadic $h$ weights and smooth dyadic $d$ weights, not arbitrary complex coefficients.

If the theorem only accepts phases $F(z)=1/z$ without additive constants inside $F$, or only accepts unshifted integral frequencies, then M9b is not covered without an extra argument.

## Hidden assumptions and potential gaps

1. **H4 centered-to-floor conversion.**
The proof must explicitly distinguish Vaaler's centered sawtooth from $\psi_F(n)=-1/2$. The Fejer majorant covers the half-jump, but the proof draft must say this.

2. **Choice of Vaaler height.**
If

$$
H_D=\lfloor cDX^{-1/4}\rfloor
$$

is $0$ near the lower edge, the block must be treated as short or one must define $H_D=\max(1,\lfloor cDX^{-1/4}\rfloor)$. This is a bookkeeping issue but should not be left implicit.

3. **Dyadic weights.**
R5 uses $|w_D|$. If the dyadic partition has signed weights, the residual proof remains valid only after taking absolute values. Main terms retain $w_D$ and require real-valued weights for the paired-frequency formulas.

4. **Second-leg positivity of $\ell=4m-\rho$.**
For large $X$, $\ell\asymp X/d>0$. Small $X$ must be absorbed separately. The proof draft should include the finite-exception sentence.

5. **M9b theorem extension.**
The shifted-$F$ formulation avoids nonsmooth $\chi_4(h)$ weights, but an actual theorem must allow $F_{\rho,D}$ depending mildly on $D/X$ through a constant term.

6. **Operator-norm blindness.**
Q1-Spectral only blocks methods that pass through diagonal-unitary conjugation and then use unitarily invariant norms or absolute-value matrices. It does not block signed estimates before norm extraction.

7. **Stationary-phase transforms.**
H13/B-process-first may preserve a dual character, but the dual phase is of square-root type and Hessian-degenerate. Single-integral stationary phase does not imply double-sum cancellation.

## Counterexample or obstruction search

### Obstruction 1: exact resonances in R5

If $X/d$ is an integer, then $K_H(X/d)=H+1$, and the residual contribution per such $d$ is

$$
\frac{H+1}{H}\asymp 1.
$$

The number of such $d$ in a dyadic block is at most

$$
\tau(X)
$$

when $X$ is an integer, and is zero if $X$ is not integer and exact divisibility is unavailable. The R5 product-count proof covers this by the same divisor bound. Thus exact Fejer spikes do not falsify R5.

### Obstruction 2: many shifted second-leg resonances

A shifted resonance satisfies

$$
\frac{X/d+\rho}{4}\in\mathbb Z,
$$

equivalently

$$
X=d(4m-\rho).
$$

For integer $X$, the number of such resonances is at most a congruence-restricted divisor count. This again gives

$$
\ll_\epsilon X^\epsilon
$$
