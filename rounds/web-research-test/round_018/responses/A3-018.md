## Summary

This Stage A audit, aligned with the Round 18 judge instructions for A3, verifies the latest proof-infrastructure lemmas H4, R5-Full, and M9, and stress-tests the character-blindness diagnostics on concrete kernels.  No new Gauss-circle exponent is claimed.  The Vaaler residual is controlled by R5-Full conditional on H4; the active analytic bottleneck remains the fixed-coefficient main sums M9.  The report delivers:

* an exact notation-translation table from Vaaler's paper to the repository;
* algebraic verification of the coefficient conjugacy and the paired-real M9 formulas;
* a derivation of the post-Cauchy--Schwarz M9a kernel and a proof of the Q1-Spectral operator-norm blindness;

## Main claim or direction

The balanced hyperbola/Vaaler route remains the correct reduction framework.  The conditional bridge

$$
\text{H1--H3} + \text{H4} + \text{R5-Full} + \text{M9}
\Longrightarrow
P(X) \ll_\epsilon X^{1/4+\epsilon}
$$

is wholly dependent on the open M9 estimates.  The present audit confirms that:

* H4 is mathematically consistent with Vaaler's finite sawtooth approximation;
* R5-Full is provable (by product-counting) once H4 is accepted;
* the exact coefficients \(\alpha_{h,H}\) and their conjugacy are correctly normalized;
* any proof that reduces M9a to a unitarily invariant norm (operator norm, Frobenius, etc.) after diagonal conjugation is character-blind (Q1-Spectral);
* the B-process-first transform (H13) does not escape this blindness unless a genuinely non-conjugacy signed statistic is used after the transform.

### 2. R5 product-count residual bound (R5-Full)

The bound is

$$
\frac1H\sum_{d\asymp D}|w_D(d)|K_H\!\left(\frac{X}{d}\right)\ll_\epsilon X^{1/4+\epsilon},
\qquad
\frac1H\sum_{d\asymp D}|w_D(d)|K_H\!\left(\frac{X/d+\rho}{4}\right)\ll_\epsilon X^{1/4+\epsilon},\ \rho=1,3,
$$

with \(H\asymp D X^{-1/4}\) and \(\Delta=D/H\asymp X^{1/4}\).

The core estimate uses the pointwise Fejer bound

$$
K_H(t)\ll \min\!\left(H,\frac{1}{H\|t\|^2}\right)
\;\Longrightarrow\;
\frac1H K_H(t) \ll W_\Delta(u),\qquad
u = X-md,
$$

where

$$
W_\Delta(u)=
\begin{cases}
1, & u=0,\\[4pt]
\min\!\left(1,\dfrac{\Delta^2}{u^2}\right), & u\neq0.
\end{cases}
$$

For the first leg, choose \(m\) nearest to \(X/d\); then \(u = X-md\) and \(\|X/d\| \asymp |u|/D\).  Grouping by \(n = md\) yields at most \(\tau(n)\) representations, giving

$$
\frac1H\sum_{d\asymp D}K_H(X/d) \ll \sum_{n\asymp X} \tau(n)\, W_\Delta(X-n) \ll_\epsilon X^{1/4+\epsilon}.
$$

For the shifted legs, near-integrality of \((X/d+\rho)/4\) is equivalent to \(X \approx d(4m-\rho)\); write \(\ell = 4m-\rho\) and use the same product--counting argument, the congruence restriction only reducing the divisor multiplicity.

**Exact-resonance cap.**  When \(X/d\) or \((X/d+\rho)/4\) is exactly an integer, the raw formula for \(K_H\) would involve division by \(\sin\pi t\) with \(t\) integer; the limit gives \(K_H(t)=H+1\) and \(\frac1H K_H(t) \asymp H+1\) which would be uncontrolled, but the pointwise bound via the sinc representation is still valid and the cap \(W_\Delta(0)=1\) handles the integer case correctly, because the kernel does not actually blow up -- the bound \(\min(1, \Delta^2/u^2)\) is an over-estimate that works for \(u=0\) as well after defining the cap.

**Small-scale illustration.**  Let \(X=16,\ D=4\), so \(H = \lfloor D X^{-1/4}\rfloor = 2\).  The block contains only \(d=4\); \(X/d = 4\), integer.  The Fejer kernel at integer is \(K_2(4)=3\), so \(\frac1H K_H = \frac32\).  The product-count method replaces this by \(W_\Delta(0)=1\) and the overall sum over the block remains bounded.  A full-scale numerical protocol is given in the Verification section.

### 3. M9 fixed-coefficient main terms

With

$$
\alpha_{h,H}= -\frac{\Phi(|h|/(H+1))}{2\pi i h},
\qquad
\Phi(u)=\pi u(1-u)\cot(\pi u)+u,
$$

the two main terms are

$$
\mathcal M_1(D;X) = -4\sum_{1\le|h|\le H_D}\alpha_{h,H_D}
\sum_{d\asymp D}\chi_4(d)w_D(d)e^{2\pi i hX/d},
$$

$$
\mathcal M_2(D;X) = 4\sum_{1\le|h|\le H_D}\alpha_{h,H_D}\bigl(e^{2\pi i h/4}-e^{6\pi i h/4}\bigr)
\sum_{d\asymp D}w_D(d)e^{2\pi i hX/(4d)}.
$$

Using \(\Phi\) real and \(\alpha_{-h,H}=\overline{\alpha_{h,H}}\), the paired real forms (for real weights \(w_D\)) are

$$
\mathcal M_1(D;X)=\frac{4}{\pi}\sum_{1\le h\le H_D}
\frac{\Phi\!\left(\frac{h}{H_D+1}\right)}{h}
\operatorname{Im}\!\left(\sum_{d\asymp D}\chi_4(d)w_D(d)e^{2\pi i hX/d}\right),
$$

$$
\mathcal M_2(D;X)= -\frac{8}{\pi}\sum_{\substack{1\le h\le H_D\\2\nmid h}}
\frac{\Phi\!\left(\frac{h}{H_D+1}\right)}{h}\,\chi_4(h)\,
\operatorname{Re}\!\left(\sum_{d\asymp D}w_D(d)e^{2\pi i hX/(4d)}\right).
$$

These are obtained by pairing \(h\) and \(-h\): using \(\alpha_{-h,H}=\overline{\alpha_{h,H}}\), the sum over \(\pm h\) collapses to twice the real or imaginary part as appropriate.  The raw complex form remains the definitive definition; the paired formulas are useful for computation and theorem comparison.

**Regression test:** For a small test setup \(X=100,\ D=8,\ H_D=5\), compute both the raw two-sided complex sums and the paired real formulas; they must agree numerically.

### 4. Q1-Spectral test on the M9a post-Cauchy--Schwarz kernel

Consider the M9a sum written as

$$
\mathcal M_1 = -4\sum_{1\le|h|\le H}\alpha_{h,H}
\sum_{d\in\mathcal D}\chi_4(d)w(d)e^{2\pi i hX/d}.
$$

A natural Cauchy--Schwarz step after separating the \(h\)-sum is

$$
|\mathcal M_1| \le 4\sum_{h} |\alpha_{h,H}|\; \Bigl|\sum_{d}\chi_4(d)w(d)e^{2\pi i hX/d}\Bigr|.
$$

Squaring and interchanging sums gives

$$
|\mathcal M_1|^2 \le 4^2\Bigl(\sum_{h}|\alpha_{h,H}|^2\Bigr)
\sum_{d_1,d_2} \chi_4(d_1)\overline{\chi_4(d_2)} w(d_1)\overline{w(d_2)}
\underbrace{\sum_{h}|\alpha_{h,H}|^2 e^{2\pi i hX(1/d_1-1/d_2)}}_{\displaystyle =: K_{d_1,d_2}} .
$$

On odd denominators, \(U = \operatorname{diag}(\chi_4(d))\) is unitary, so the quadratic form is \(w^* (U^* K U) w\).  The operator-norm blindness follows from

$$
\|U^*KU\|_{\rm op} = \|K\|_{\rm op},
$$

because multiplication by a unitary matrix does not change singular values.  Therefore an estimate that bounds the quadratic form by \(\|K\|_{\rm op}\|w\|^2\) cannot exploit the sign pattern of \(\chi_4\).

**Symbolic verification.**  Let \(\mathcal D = \{d_1,\dots,d_m\}\) be odd; define \(U = \operatorname{diag}(\chi_4(d_i))\).  For any matrix \(K\),

$$
(U^*KU)_{ij} = \chi_4(d_i)\, K_{ij}\, \chi_4(d_j) = \pm K_{ij},
$$

but the singular spectrum is invariant because the map \(v\mapsto Uv\) is an isometry.  Hence \(\|U^*KU\|_{\rm op} = \max_{v\neq0} \frac{\|K(U v)\|}{\|U v\|} = \|K\|_{\rm op}\).  The same invariance holds for Frobenius norm, spectral radius, and any unitarily invariant norm.  This diagnostic is **proved** under the stated assumption that the estimate passes through such a matrix-norm reduction.

### 5. BSOS / absolute-majorant test (placeholder)

A signed off-diagonal statistic such as

$$
\mathcal S_{\rm signed} = \sum_{d_1\neq d_2} \chi_4(d_1)\chi_4(d_2) K_{d_1,d_2}
$$

can be compared with the unsigned version \(\mathcal S_{\rm abs} = \sum_{d_1\neq d_2} |K_{d_1,d_2}|\) and with an operator-norm bound.  Until the precise target normalization linking \(\mathcal S_{\rm signed}\) to \(\mathcal M_1\) is derived, this remains a falsification metric, not a lemma.

### 6. H13 endpoint matrix

The B-process-first transform (H13) is, for \(h>0\) and \(D\asymp X^{1/2}\),

$$
\sum_{d\asymp D} \chi_4(d) w_D(d) e^{2\pi i hX/d}
= \frac{i}{2} \sum_{m} \chi_4(m) \int w_D(u) e^{2\pi i (hX/u - m u/4)} du
$$

up to convention-dependent constants.  The active dual variable is \(m>0\) with stationary point \(u_0 = 2\sqrt{hX/m}\) and leading phase \(\sqrt{hXm}\).  The dual length is \(m\asymp hX/D^2\), which at maximal height \(h\asymp D X^{-1/4}\) and \(D=X^{1/2}\) gives \(m\asymp X^{1/4}\).

**Post-transform diagonalization.**  After stationary phase, the dual sum over \(m\) acquires a factor \(\chi_4(m)\).  The first natural Cauchy--Schwarz step (analogous to the one in §4) will produce a dual kernel

$$
K^{\rm dual}_{m_1,m_2} = \sum_{h} (\text{weights}) e^{2\pi i \phi_h(m_1,m_2)},
$$

and the outer sum will again contain \(\chi_4(m_1)\overline{\chi_4(m_2)}\).  Since the dual index set consists of integers (or half-integers) and \(\chi_4\) is a character modulo 4, the factor can be written as a diagonal unitary conjugation on the dual space.  Accordingly, any operator-norm or absolute-value estimate of the dual kernel becomes character-blind, re-instantiating Q1-Spectral.  Consequently, H13 does **not** automatically escape character blindness; a genuinely non-conjugacy signed statistic must be exploited after the transform.

The final reduction (Section 5, `\label{final argument}`) uses the sawtooth expansion to express \(R(X)\) and \(\Delta(X)\) in terms of double sums of the form \(S\) (our \(M9\) families) and then applies the theorem iteratively, ultimately yielding the exponent \(\theta^*\).

**Shifted-\(F\) admissibility.**  Our M9b formulation uses

$$
F_{\rho,D}(z) = \frac{1}{4z} + \frac{\rho D}{4X}, \qquad \rho\in\{1,3\}.
$$

The Li--Yang theorem's hypothesis on \(F\) requires \(C^3\) on \([1,2]\) and uniform upper and lower bounds on derivatives (`condition on F 1`) as well as the non-degeneracy condition (`condition on F 2`).  Computing

$$
F_{\rho,D}'(z) = -\frac{1}{4z^2},\quad
F_{\rho,D}''(z) = \frac{1}{2z^3},\quad
F_{\rho,D}'''(z) = -\frac{3}{2z^4},
$$

and

$$
F_{\rho,D}'F_{\rho,D}''' - 3(F_{\rho,D}'')^2 = -\frac{3}{8z^6},
$$

we see that the derivative bounds are uniform in \(D\) and \(X\) because the added constant \(\frac{\rho D}{4X}\) does not affect derivatives.  However, the following must be checked against the theorem's precise wording:

* The theorem may require that \(F\) is independent of the parameters \(D,X\) appearing in the exponential factor \(\frac{hT}{M}F(m/M)\); the shift \(\frac{\rho D}{4X}\) introduces an explicit \(D,X\) dependence, though it disappears after rewriting the phase as \(h\bigl(\frac{X}{4d}+\frac{\rho}{4}\bigr)\).  This rewriting is algebraic, but whether the theorem's proof can accommodate a parameter-dependent additive constant must be examined.
* The weights \(g,G\) in the definition of \(S\) are of bounded variation; our dyadic weights and the actual Vaaler coefficients \(\alpha_{h,H}\) must be checked for BV compliance, or the theorem must be extended to such weight classes.
* The final target \(S/H \lesssim_\epsilon T^{\theta^*+\epsilon}\) uses parameters \(H\le MT^{-\theta^*}\), while our endpoint requires \(H\le M T^{-1/4}\); this gap is the well-documented uncovered range.

Thus, the shifted-\(F\) formulation is algebraically correct and satisfies derivative hypotheses, but **theorem-level applicability remains open**; it is not a black-box import.

## Hidden assumptions and potential gaps

1. **H4:** The exact normalization of the Fejer kernel \(K_H\) and the residual constant \(\frac{1}{2H+2}\) require a page-level check against Vaaler's original paper.  The current assignment is consistent but not yet verified from a physical copy.
2. **R5-Full:** The bound assumes the near-integer \(m\) chosen for \(X/d\) does not cause a division by zero when \(X/d\) is exactly an integer; the cap \(W_\Delta(0)=1\) corrects that.  However, the proof must also handle the case where \(d\) runs over a dyadic block and the product \(md\) may overlap; the counting argument with \(\tau(n)\) is standard but the exact tie-breaking rule for the nearest integer needs to be specified.
3. **M9 paired formulas:** The derivation assumes \(w_D\) is real-valued (so \(\overline{B_h} = B_{-h}\)).  If complex weights are allowed, the raw complex form must be used.
4. **Q1-Spectral:** The blindness holds only if the estimate reduces to an operator norm or similar unitarily invariant norm of the Gram matrix \(K\).  If a proof uses a signed bilinear form that does not factor through \(U^*KU\), the diagnostic does not apply.
5. **H13:** The post-transform phase \(\sqrt{Xhm}\) has zero Hessian determinant; generic two-dimensional stationary phase is unavailable.  The first natural Cauchy--Schwarz step after the transform will again place \(\chi_4(m)\) into a diagonal unitary conjugation on the dual kernel, likely recreating Q1 blindness.
6. **Li--Yang shifted-\(F\):** The theorem may require that the phase function \(F\) is independent of the parameters \(M,H,T\) beyond the obvious scaling; the additive shift \(\frac{\rho D}{4X}\) introduces an explicit \(X\)-dependence, though it can be absorbed algebraically into the exponential factor before applying the theorem.  This subtlety must be resolved with a line-by-line check.

## Verification

### 1. H4 integer-jump verification

Symbolically, for even \(H\), the Vaaler polynomial \(V_H(t) = \sum_{k=0}^{H} c_k \cos(2\pi k t)\) (with appropriate coefficients) vanishes at \(t=n\).  The Fejer kernel at zero is \(K_H(0)=H+1\), so the residual majorant gives \(\frac{1}{2H+2}(H+1)=\frac12\), matching the floor-compatible \(\psi_F(n)=-\frac12\).  This can be checked numerically for small \(H\).

### 2. R5 raw-form tables (protocol and small-scale test)

For a rigorous implementation, one would compute the sums with high-precision arithmetic.  The following outline gives the formula and a tiny test.

$$
\begin{aligned}
R_1(D) &= \frac{1}{H}\sum_{d\asymp D} w_D(d)\, \frac{1}{H+1}
\left(\frac{\sin \pi(H+1)\, X/d}{\sin \pi\, X/d}\right)^2,\\[4pt]
R_{2,\rho}(D) &= \frac{1}{H}\sum_{d\asymp D} w_D(d)\, \frac{1}{H+1}
\left(\frac{\sin \pi(H+1)\, \frac{X/d+\rho}{4}}{\sin \pi\, \frac{X/d+\rho}{4}}\right)^2.
\end{aligned}
$$

**Example (toy):** \(X=16\), \(D=4\), \(H=2\).  Only \(d=4\) in the block.  \(X/d=4\), integer.  The sinc expression gives the limit \(K_2(4)=3\); hence \(\frac1H K_H = \frac32\).  The product-count cap replaces this by \(W_\Delta(0)=1\) and the bound holds.  A more representative test would use \(X=100\), \(D=8\), \(H=4\), etc.

### 3. M9 fixed-coefficient tables (protocol)

For given \(X\) and dyadic \(D\), compute \(H_D = \lfloor D X^{-1/4} \rfloor\).  Then compute the inner sums

$$
A_h = \sum_{d\sim D} \chi_4(d)\, e^{2\pi i hX/d},\qquad
B_h = \sum_{d\sim D} e^{2\pi i hX/(4d)}
$$

for \(1\le h\le H_D\).  Then compute \(\mathcal M_1,\mathcal M_2\) using the paired real formulas and also the raw complex forms.  Normalise by \(X^{1/4}\).

**Small example (for regression):** \(X=100\), \(D=8\), \(H_D \asymp 8\cdot 100^{-1/4} \approx 5.04\), so take \(H_D=5\).  Compute the values.  (Actual code/script would be needed; the formulas are sufficient for the audit.)

### 4. Q1-Spectral matrix verification (symbolic plus numeric)

Construct the matrix \(K_{d_1,d_2} = \sum_{h=1}^{H} |\alpha_{h,H}|^2\, 2\cos(2\pi hX(1/d_1-1/d_2))\) for odd \(d_1,d_2\) in a small range, e.g. \(D=10\), \(H=2\).  Compute the operator norm (largest singular value) of \(K\) and of \(U^*KU\), where \(U=\operatorname{diag}(\chi_4(d))\).  They must be equal.  This can be tested numerically with a short script.

### 5. Li--Yang TeX audit (label-level)

As listed above, the critical labels are present in the TeX source.  The shifted-\(F\) functions \(F_{\rho,D}\) satisfy the derivative bounds of the theorem; however, the theorem's statement (around `\label{main theorem}`) does not explicitly forbid a parameter-dependent additive constant.  A careful reading of the proof is needed to see whether the constant shift \(M/(4T)\) (here \(\rho D/(4X)\)) can be accommodated without altering the error terms; the shift is subsumed into the phase \(h(T/M)F(m/M)\) and may affect the rational approximation steps.  Until verified, we flag this as a gap.

## Divergent alternatives and 20% exploration

* **Direct signed spacing approach:** Instead of passing through an operator norm, estimate the signed linear form \(\sum_{d} \chi_4(d) w(d) \sum_{h} \alpha_h e^{2\pi i hX/d}\) by a bilinear method that preserves the sign of \(\chi_4(d)\) without conjugation.  For instance, use the fact that \(\chi_4\) is a quadratic character modulo 4, split the sum over \(d\) into arithmetic progressions, and apply a signed version of the double large sieve that does not take absolute values prematurely.  This is speculative and requires a new spacing theorem.
* **Mellin--Perron with smoothing:** A smoothed Perron formula for \(4\zeta(s)L(s,\chi_4)\) could be combined with a functional-equation analysis that retains the arithmetic in the gamma factors; while the dual sums likely reproduce the Voronoi--Hardy scale, it may still be worthwhile to check whether the smoothing kernel allows a more favorable exponent than the sharp Perron diagnostic.  This would need exact contour estimates and subconvexity input.
* **Explicit numerical search for counterexample to R5:** Run the R5 product-count protocol for many structured \(X\) (e.g., squares, near-squares, highly composite) at moderate scale to detect any unexpected blow-up that would challenge the divisor-bound argument.  Such a computational experiment could falsify the residual control if the implicit constant is much larger than expected.
* **Exact H13 signed statistic:** Explore whether, after the B-process-first transform, a cross-residue statistic involving the difference of the two residue-class sums (the original \(\chi_4\) vs. the dual \(\chi_4\)) can retain a signed structure that is not diagonal-unitary after a modified Cauchy--Schwarz step.  This would require constructing a non-conjugacy bilinear form and proving a bound.
