## Detailed reasoning

### 1. H4-R18: finite Vaaler approximation in proof-draft notation

Use the convention

$$
e(t)=e^{2\pi i t}.
$$

Vaaler's source is Jeffrey D. Vaaler, "Some extremal functions in Fourier analysis," *Bulletin of the American Mathematical Society* 12(2), 183--216, 1985. The prior source audit identifies the relevant anchors as Theorem 6, printed page 192, equation (2.28), for the Fourier transform of $J$, and Theorem 18, printed page 210, equations (7.13)--(7.16), especially (7.14), for the periodic sawtooth approximation and Fejer residual majorant.

Vaaler defines periodic trigonometric polynomials $i_N,j_N,k_N$ in Section 7, equations (7.1)--(7.3), and $k_N$ is the periodic Fejer kernel. Theorem 18 gives the approximation $\psi*j_N$ and the residual bound

$$
|\psi*j_N(x)-\psi(x)|\le (2N+2)^{-1}k_N(x).
$$

The coefficient function comes from Theorem 6, equation (2.28):

$$
\widehat J(t)
=
\pi t(1-|t|)\cot(\pi t)+|t|,
\qquad
0<|t|<1.
$$

This source mapping is also recorded in prior A1/A3 audits.

Set

$$
H=N.
$$

Define the Fejer kernel in repo notation by

$$
K_H(t)=k_N(t)
=
\sum_{|k|\le H}
\left(1-\frac{|k|}{H+1}\right)e(kt)
=
\frac1{H+1}
\left(\frac{\sin \pi(H+1)t}{\sin \pi t}\right)^2.
$$

Define

$$
\Phi(u)=\pi u(1-u)\cot(\pi u)+u,
\qquad 0<u<1.
$$

Since Vaaler's coefficient is obtained from

$$
\widehat J_{N+1}(h)
=
\widehat J\left(\frac{h}{N+1}\right),
$$

and $\widehat J$ is even in the relevant formula, the repo coefficient is

$$
\alpha_{h,H}
=
-\frac{\Phi(|h|/(H+1))}{2\pi i h},
\qquad 1\le |h|\le H.
$$

For $h>0$ this is

$$
\alpha_{h,H}
=
\frac{i}{2\pi h}\Phi\left(\frac{h}{H+1}\right).
$$

The conjugacy relation is therefore

$$
\alpha_{-h,H}=\overline{\alpha_{h,H}},
$$

not $-\overline{\alpha_{h,H}}$.

Define the Vaaler polynomial

$$
V_H(t)=\sum_{1\le |h|\le H}\alpha_{h,H}e(ht).
$$

Let $\psi_C$ be the centered periodic sawtooth,

$$
\psi_C(t)=
\begin{cases}
t-\lfloor t\rfloor-\frac12,& t\notin\mathbb Z,\\
0,& t\in\mathbb Z.
\end{cases}
$$

Vaaler's theorem gives

$$
|\psi_C(t)-V_H(t)|
\le
\frac{1}{2H+2}K_H(t)
$$

for all real $t$.

The arithmetic identity uses the floor-compatible sawtooth

$$
\psi_F(t)=t-\lfloor t\rfloor-\frac12
$$

for all real $t$, so

$$
\psi_F(n)=-\frac12
\qquad(n\in\mathbb Z).
$$

At nonintegers, $\psi_F=\psi_C$. At integers, $V_H(n)=0$ because the $h$ and $-h$ terms cancel, while

$$
K_H(0)=H+1.
$$

Therefore

$$
\frac{K_H(0)}{2H+2}=\frac12
=
|\psi_F(n)-V_H(n)|.
$$

Thus the same residual majorant covers the floor-compatible convention. The proof-draft H4 statement is:

**H4-R18. Vaaler finite approximation with floor-compatible residual.** For every integer $H\ge1$ and every real $t$,

$$
\psi_F(t)
=
\sum_{1\le |h|\le H}\alpha_{h,H}e(ht)
+
R_H^F(t),
$$

where

$$
\alpha_{h,H}
=
-\frac{\Phi(|h|/(H+1))}{2\pi i h},
$$

and

$$
|R_H^F(t)|
\le
\frac1{2H+2}K_H(t).
$$

This is an imported theorem dependency from Vaaler, with the integer convention handled by the Fejer majorant.

### 2. R5-Full-R18: completed Fejer residual lemma

**Lemma R5-Full-R18.** Let $X\ge2$ be real. Let

$$
X^{1/4}\le D\le X^{1/2},
$$

and let $H$ be an integer satisfying

$$
c_0D X^{-1/4}\le H\le C_0D X^{-1/4}
$$

for fixed positive constants $c_0,C_0$. Let $w_D$ be a dyadic weight supported on $d\asymp D$, with $|w_D(d)|\le C_w$. Then, for every $\epsilon>0$,

$$
\frac1H
\sum_{d\asymp D}
|w_D(d)|K_H(X/d)
\ll_\epsilon
X^{1/4+\epsilon},
$$

and, for $\rho\in\{1,3\}$,

$$
\frac1H
\sum_{d\asymp D}
|w_D(d)|
K_H\left(\frac{X/d+\rho}{4}\right)
\ll_\epsilon
X^{1/4+\epsilon}.
$$

Consequently, assuming H4-R18, the full Vaaler residual from both sawtooth legs in H3 contributes

$$
\ll_\epsilon X^{1/4+\epsilon}
$$

after summing over all dyadic blocks, with logarithmic losses absorbed into $X^\epsilon$.

#### Proof: Fejer kernel bound

The Fejer kernel satisfies the standard pointwise estimate

$$
K_H(t)\ll \min\left(H,\frac1{H\|t\|^2}\right),
$$

where

$$
\|t\|=\min_{m\in\mathbb Z}|t-m|.
$$

Hence

$$
\frac1H K_H(t)
\ll
\min\left(1,\frac1{H^2\|t\|^2}\right).
$$

Set

$$
\Delta=\frac{D}{H}.
$$

Since $H\asymp D X^{-1/4}$, one has

$$
\Delta\asymp X^{1/4}.
$$

Define the exact-resonance kernel

$$
W_\Delta(u)=
\begin{cases}
1,& u=0,\\
\min\left(1,\frac{\Delta^2}{u^2}\right),& u\ne0.
\end{cases}
$$

This is the corrected kernel. It includes exact resonances by setting $W_\Delta(0)=1$.

#### First residual leg

Consider

$$
R_1(D;X)
=
\frac1H
\sum_{d\asymp D}
|w_D(d)|K_H(X/d).
$$

For each $d$, choose $m=m(d)$ to be a nearest integer to $X/d$. If there is a tie, choose either nearest integer; no estimate depends on the tie choice. If $X/d$ is an integer, choose $m=X/d$.

For $d\asymp D$,

$$
\left\|\frac Xd\right\|
=
\left|\frac Xd-m\right|
=
\frac{|X-md|}{d}
\asymp
\frac{|X-md|}{D}.
$$

Therefore

$$
\frac1H K_H(X/d)
\ll
W_\Delta(X-md).
$$

Let

$$
n=md.
$$

For large $X$, since $d\le 2D\le2X^{1/2}$, the nearest integer $m$ is positive. Any finite small-$X$ exceptions are absorbed into the implicit constant. For fixed integer $n$, the number of representations $n=md$ with $d\asymp D$ is at most $\tau(n)$. Since nearest-integer choice gives $|X-n|\ll D$, all contributing $n$ lie in $n\asymp X$ for large $X$.

Thus

$$
R_1(D;X)
\ll
\sum_{n\asymp X}
\tau(n)W_\Delta(X-n).
$$

Using

$$
\tau(n)\ll_\epsilon n^\epsilon
$$

and the elementary bound

$$
\sum_{n\in\mathbb Z}W_\Delta(X-n)
\ll \Delta+1,
$$

we get

$$
R_1(D;X)
\ll_\epsilon
X^\epsilon(\Delta+1)
\ll_\epsilon
X^{1/4+\epsilon}.
$$

This argument covers real and integer $X$. If $X$ is an integer and $n=X$, the exact resonance contributes $W_\Delta(0)=1$, with multiplicity bounded by $\tau(X)$.

#### Shifted residual legs

For $\rho\in\{1,3\}$, consider

$$
R_{2,\rho}(D;X)
=
\frac1H
\sum_{d\asymp D}
|w_D(d)|
K_H\left(\frac{X/d+\rho}{4}\right).
$$

For each $d$, choose $m=m(d)$ nearest to

$$
\frac{X/d+\rho}{4}.
$$

Then

$$
\left\|\frac{X/d+\rho}{4}\right\|
=
\frac{|X/d+\rho-4m|}{4}
=
\frac{|X-d(4m-\rho)|}{4d}.
$$

Set

$$
\ell=4m-\rho.
$$

Then

$$
\ell\equiv-\rho\pmod4.
$$

For large $X$, $\ell>0$, because $\ell\asymp X/d\gg1$. Small exceptions are finite and harmless. As before,

$$
\frac1H
K_H\left(\frac{X/d+\rho}{4}\right)
\ll
W_\Delta(X-d\ell).
$$

Let

$$
n=d\ell.
$$

For fixed $n$, the number of representations $n=d\ell$ with $d\asymp D$ and $\ell\equiv-\rho\pmod4$ is at most $\tau(n)$. The congruence restriction only reduces multiplicity. Therefore

$$
R_{2,\rho}(D;X)
\ll
\sum_{n\asymp X}
\tau(n)W_\Delta(X-n)
\ll_\epsilon
X^{1/4+\epsilon}.
$$

This proves the shifted-leg estimate.

#### Zero Fejer mode

The constant term of $K_H$ contributes

$$
\frac1H\sum_{d\asymp D}|w_D(d)|
\ll
\frac{D}{H}
=
\Delta
\asymp X^{1/4}.
$$

Thus the zero mode is already at the conjectural scale. The nonzero modes are controlled by the same product-count bound above.

#### Both signs of frequency

The residual is bounded by the nonnegative even Fejer kernel $K_H$. The positive and negative Fejer frequencies are included in $K_H$ by definition, so no separate sign cancellation is used. This is important: R5-Full is a positive majorant argument, not a signed Fourier-tail estimate.

#### Dyadic weights and logarithmic losses

For a bounded-overlap dyadic partition over $1\le d\le y$, apply the residual estimate to $|w_D|$. The number of dyadic blocks is $O(\log X)$, so after summing over blocks,

$$
\sum_D R_\star(D;X)
\ll_\epsilon
X^{1/4+\epsilon}.
$$

#### Short blocks

If $D<X^{1/4}$, do not apply Vaaler. Since

$$
|\psi_F(t)|\le\frac12
$$

for all noninteger $t$, and the integer value is also $-1/2$, a block of length $D$ contributes $O(D)$. Summing over $D<X^{1/4}$ with bounded overlap gives

$$
O(X^{1/4})
$$

up to logarithmic factors. This is acceptable.

### 3. Bridge-R18: conditional proof skeleton

**Bridge-R18.** Assume H1--H3, H4-R18, R5-Full-R18, and M9-R18. Then

$$
\text{H1--H3}+\text{H4}+\text{R5-Full}+\text{M9}
\Longrightarrow
P(X)\ll_\epsilon X^{1/4+\epsilon}.
$$

Proof sketch. Start from H3:

$$
P(X)=W_1(X)+W_2(X)+O(1),
$$

where

$$
W_1(X)
=
-4\sum_{d\le y}\chi_4(d)\psi_F(X/d)
$$

and

$$
W_2(X)
=
4\sum_{d\le y}
\left[
\psi_F\left(\frac{X/d+1}{4}\right)
-
\psi_F\left(\frac{X/d+3}{4}\right)
\right].
$$

Decompose $d$ dyadically. Blocks with $D<X^{1/4}$ are bounded trivially. On $X^{1/4}\le D\le X^{1/2}$, apply H4-R18 with

$$
H_D\asymp D X^{-1/4}.
$$

For each block, H4 produces a finite Vaaler main term plus a residual. R5-Full-R18 bounds all residuals by

$$
\ll_\epsilon X^{1/4+\epsilon}
$$

after dyadic summation. The finite main terms are exactly $\mathcal M_1(D;X)$ and $\mathcal M_2(D;X)$ below. Assuming M9-R18 bounds each dyadic main term by $X^{1/4+\epsilon}$, and absorbing logarithmic block-count losses into $X^\epsilon$, we obtain

$$
P(X)\ll_\epsilon X^{1/4+\epsilon}.
$$

This bridge is conditional. It does not prove M9.

$$
X^{1/4}\le D\le X^{1/2}.

A_h(D;X)=

$$
B_h(D;X)=
\sum_d
w_D(d)e(hX/(4d)).

$$

the paired real form for the first leg is:

$$
\mathcal M_1(D;X)

\frac4\pi

For the second leg,

\mathcal M_2(D;X)

**Regression warning.** These paired formulas are implementation conveniences only. Every numerical computation and theorem-comparison reduction must be checked against the raw two-sided complex definitions of $\mathcal M_1$ and $\mathcal M_2$. If $w_D$ is complex, asymmetric, or if the positive/negative $h$ ranges are truncated differently, the paired real formulas may fail.

### 6. M9b shifted-$F$ theorem-extension statement

The second main term can be rewritten without treating $\chi_4(h)$ as a rough periodic $h$-weight:

$$
e(hX/(4d))(e(h/4)-e(3h/4))
=
e\left(h\left(\frac{X}{4d}+\frac14\right)\right)
-
e\left(h\left(\frac{X}{4d}+\frac34\right)\right).
$$

Set

$$
d=Dz.
$$

Then

$$
h\left(\frac{X}{4d}+\frac{\rho}{4}\right)
=
\frac{hX}{D}
\left(
\frac1{4z}+\frac{\rho D}{4X}
\right),
\qquad
\rho\in\{1,3\}.
$$

Thus the shifted phase functions are

$$
F_{\rho,D}(z)
=
\frac1{4z}+\frac{\rho D}{4X},
\qquad
\rho\in\{1,3\}.
$$

Their derivatives are independent of the additive shift:

$$
F_{\rho,D}'(z)=-\frac1{4z^2},
$$

$$
F_{\rho,D}''(z)=\frac1{2z^3},
$$

$$
F_{\rho,D}'''(z)=-\frac3{2z^4}.
$$

The Li--Yang nondegeneracy expression is

$$
F'F'''-3(F'')^2
=
-\frac3{8z^6},
$$

which is bounded away from zero on $1\le z\le2$.

A theorem that covers M9b in this shifted-$F$ form must allow all of the following:

1. **Parameter-dependent additive shift.** The theorem must permit $F$ to depend on $D,X$ through the constant $\rho D/(4X)$, uniformly in $X^{1/4}\le D\le X^{1/2}$. Derivative hypotheses are unchanged, but the proof must not use an absolute bound on $F$ beyond what this shift preserves.

2. **Derivative constants.** The constants in

$$
C_r^{-1}\le |F^{(r)}(z)|\le C_r,
\qquad r=1,2,3,
$$

and

$$
|F'F'''-3(F'')^2|\ge C_4^{-1}
$$

must be uniform in $\rho,D,X$.

3. **Smooth denominator weight.** The dyadic $d$-weight $w_D(d)$ must fit the allowed $G(m/M)$ class.

4. **Fixed Vaaler $h$ coefficient variation.** On each dyadic $h$ block, the weight induced by

$$
\alpha_{h,H_D}
=
-\frac{\Phi(h/(H_D+1))}{2\pi i h}
$$

must fit the theorem's allowed $g(h/H)$ class. If the theorem only permits bounded variation, one must prove a uniform BV bound for the normalized dyadic coefficient. The endpoint block $h\asymp H_D$ must be checked because $h/(H_D+1)$ approaches $1$.

5. **Endpoint height.** The theorem must cover

$$
H\le H_D\asymp D X^{-1/4},
$$

not merely

$$
H\le D X^{-\theta^*}.
$$

6. **Positive and negative frequencies.** The theorem is usually stated for positive $h$. The raw M9 definitions are two-sided. One must either use the paired real formulas or split positive and negative $h$ and invoke conjugacy.

### 8. One-page H13 falsification checklist

Start with

$$
S_\chi(h,D)=
\sum_d \chi_4(d)w_D(d)e(hX/d).

$$

and use the Fourier transform convention

$$
\widehat f(\xi)=\int_{\mathbb R}f(u)e(-\xi u)\,du.
$$

Splitting modulo $4$ and applying Poisson gives

$$
\sum_{d\equiv r\pmod4}f(d)
=
\frac14\sum_{n\in\mathbb Z}e(nr/4)\widehat f(n/4).
$$

Since $\chi_4(1)=1$ and $\chi_4(3)=-1$,

$$
S_\chi(h,D)
=
\frac14\sum_{n\in\mathbb Z}
\left(e(n/4)-e(3n/4)\right)\widehat f(n/4).
$$

Using

$$
e(n/4)-e(3n/4)=2i\chi_4(n),
$$

this becomes

$$
S_\chi(h,D)
=
\frac{i}{2}
\sum_{n\in\mathbb Z}
\chi_4(n)
\int_{\mathbb R}
w_D(u)e(hX/u-nu/4)\,du.
$$

The phase is

$$
\phi_n(u)=hX/u-nu/4.
$$

Stationary points satisfy

$$
-\frac{hX}{u^2}-\frac n4=0.
$$

Thus the stationary range has $n=-m<0$, $m>0$, with

$$
u_0=2\sqrt{\frac{hX}{m}}.
$$

Since $u_0\asymp D$, the dual length is

$$
m\asymp \frac{hX}{D^2}.
$$

The leading stationary phase is

$$
\phi_{-m}(u_0)
=
\sqrt{hXm}.
$$

The amplitude scale is

$$
\asymp
D^{3/2}(hX)^{-1/2}
$$

in the interior stationary range.

At maximal Vaaler height

$$
h\asymp H_D\asymp D X^{-1/4},
$$

and $D=X^\delta$, the dual length is

$$
m\asymp X^{3/4-\delta}.
$$

At the endpoint $D\asymp X^{1/2}$, this gives

$$
m\asymp X^{1/4},
$$

comparable to the original Vaaler height. This is the only range where H13 is near-balanced.

**Falsification checklist.**

1. Write the exact post-transform main sum:

$$
\sum_{h\le H_D}\alpha_{h,H_D}
\sum_{m\asymp hX/D^2}
\chi_4(m)a(h,m)
e(\sqrt{hXm}+\sigma)
$$

with the actual stationary amplitude $a(h,m)$ and support.

2. Apply the first intended spacing or Cauchy--Schwarz step explicitly. Do not describe it schematically.

3. If the result has the form

$$
v^*U^*KUv,
\qquad
U=\operatorname{diag}(\chi_4(m)),
$$

and the next estimate is by operator norm, Frobenius norm, Schur/Gershgorin, cyclic trace, or absolute-value matrix, then H13 has reduced the dual character to the same Q1-Spectral diagonal-unitary artifact and should be treated as sign-blind at that step.

4. If the result is a non-conjugacy signed statistic, record the exact statistic and the exact bound needed to imply M9. Without such a bound, H13 remains only a transform.

5. Test the endpoint range $D\asymp X^{1/2}$ first. For smaller $D$, the dual length grows as $X^{3/4-\delta}$ and may worsen the geometry.

6. Reject any attempt to apply generic full-rank two-dimensional stationary phase to

$$
\Phi(h,m)=\sqrt{Xhm}.
$$

Its continuous Hessian determinant is zero.

## Hidden assumptions and potential gaps

1. **H4 source transcription.** The theorem, page, and equation labels are now identified, but a final proof draft should quote the exact notation from Vaaler's PDF to avoid OCR sign errors.

2. **Dyadic partition.** The proof assumes bounded-overlap dyadic weights $w_D$ with uniformly bounded size. Residual estimates use $|w_D|$.

3. **Choice of integer $H_D$.** Near $D=X^{1/4}$, $D X^{-1/4}\asymp1$. The proof should define $H_D=\max(1,\lfloor cD X^{-1/4}\rfloor)$ or exclude boundary blocks consistently.

4. **Real $X$.** R5-Full handles real $X$ by summing over integer products $n$ near real $X$. Exact resonance only occurs when $X=n$ is integer, and is handled by $W_\Delta(0)=1$.

5. **Shifted-leg positivity.** The proof uses $\ell=4m-\rho>0$. This is true for large $X$ in the active range; small exceptions must be absorbed explicitly.

6. **M9b theorem extension.** The shifted functions $F_{\rho,D}$ pass derivative tests, but theorem applicability with a $D,X$-dependent additive constant and Vaaler $h$ weights is not automatic.

7. **Paired formula limitation.** The paired real formulas assume real weights and symmetric positive/negative frequency ranges. Raw definitions must remain canonical.

8. **Operator-norm blindness.** Q1-Spectral only blocks methods that reduce the signed form to diagonal-unitary conjugation followed by a unitarily invariant or absolute-value norm. It does not rule out signed non-conjugacy statistics.

## Counterexample or obstruction search

1. **Exact Fejer resonance.** If $X/d\in\mathbb Z$, then $K_H(X/d)=H+1$. R5 still gives

$$
\frac1H K_H(X/d)\asymp1
$$

for that $d$, and the number of such resonances is bounded by divisor multiplicity. No dense obstruction arises.

2. **Shifted exact resonance.** If

$$
\frac{X/d+\rho}{4}\in\mathbb Z,
$$

then

$$
X=d(4m-\rho).
$$

Again the number of representations is divisor-bounded, with an additional congruence restriction on the complementary factor.

3. **Divisor-rich $X$.** Even for highly composite integer $X$, exact divisor alignments contribute at most

$$
\tau(X)\ll_\epsilon X^\epsilon
$$

per resonance layer. They do not create an $X^{1/2}$-scale residual.

4. **Li--Yang endpoint obstruction.** At $D=X^{1/2}$, M9 requires height $H=X^{1/4}$. Li--Yang's final range reaches only $H\le X^{0.1855168\cdots}$, leaving a high-frequency gap.

5. **M9a character erasure.** If the first Cauchy--Schwarz step produces a kernel $U^*KU$ with $U=\operatorname{diag}(\chi_4(d))$, operator-norm extraction cannot exploit $\chi_4$. This is an obstruction to a class of methods, not to all methods.

6. **H13 dual erasure.** If H13 is followed by Cauchy--Schwarz in a way that produces $U=\operatorname{diag}(\chi_4(m))$ and then an operator norm, the transform has not preserved useful signed structure.

## Divergent alternatives and 20% exploration

### Alternative A: signed non-conjugacy statistic for M9a

A possible escape from Q1-Spectral is not to estimate $U^*KU$ by a unitarily invariant norm. A concrete statistic should be finite and falsifiable. For example, after one chosen Cauchy--Schwarz normalization, define residue subspaces

$$
\mathcal D_1=\{d\asymp D:d\equiv1\pmod4\},
\qquad
\mathcal D_3=\{d\asymp D:d\equiv3\pmod4\}.
$$

For the actual kernel $K_{d_1,d_2}$ produced by that normalization, test

$$
\mathcal S_{\rm cross}
=
\sum_{d_1\in\mathcal D_1}
\sum_{d_2\in\mathcal D_3}
K_{d_1,d_2}
-
\sum_{d_1,d_2\in\mathcal D_1}
K_{d_1,d_2}
-
\sum_{d_1,d_2\in\mathcal D_3}
K_{d_1,d_2},
$$

or the exact signed version forced by the quadratic form. This is not yet a lemma. It becomes useful only if one proves that a bound for $\mathcal S_{\rm cross}$ implies M9a, or if numerical tests show stable cancellation not present in the absolute majorant.

Falsification test: compute $\mathcal S_{\rm signed}$, $\mathcal S_{\rm abs}$, and $\|K\|_{\rm op}\|v\|^2$ for dyadic samples. If $\mathcal S_{\rm signed}$ tracks the absolute or operator-norm comparator, deprioritize this statistic.

### Alternative B: signed Fourier tail comparison

Instead of Vaaler's positive residual majorant, one might compare the signed truncated Fourier tail

$$
\sum_{|h|>H_D}
\frac{1}{h}
\sum_{d\sim D}\chi_4(d)e(hX/d).
$$

This preserves character longer than the Fejer majorant. The obstacle is that absolute convergence fails, and any absolute tail estimate likely recreates an H5r-like or M9-like problem.

Falsification test: apply summation by parts in $h$ and determine whether the needed partial sums are stronger than M9. If they are, this is not an escape route.

### Alternative C: Mellin--Perron comparison

The Mellin--Perron route keeps the arithmetic object

$$
4\zeta(s)L(s,\chi_4)
$$

visible. But after truncation and functional equation, it likely reconstructs Voronoi/Bessel-type sums of comparable difficulty.

Falsification test: write a smoothed Perron formula with explicit height $T_P$, shift contour, and compute the dual length after the functional equation. If reaching $X^{1/4+\epsilon}$ requires moment/subconvexity estimates equivalent to M9, keep this route as comparison only.
