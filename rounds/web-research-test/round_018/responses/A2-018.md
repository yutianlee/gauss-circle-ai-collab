We note that $|\alpha_{h,H_D}| \asymp 1/|h|$ for $1 \le |h| \le H_D$.

To prepare the sum for spacing or large-sieve estimates, we must apply the Cauchy--Schwarz inequality to eliminate the coefficients $\alpha_{h,H_D}$ and bring the spatial variables $d_1, d_2$ together. $$

$$
\sum_{1\le |h|\le H_D} |\alpha_{h,H_D}| \asymp \log H_D \ll \log X.

### 2. Deriving the Exact BSOS Target

We now define the Bilinear Signed Off-diagonal Statistic (`BSOS`) and derive the exact target bound required to imply $\mathcal M_1(D;X) \ll_\epsilon X^{1/4+\epsilon}$.

$$

$$
\mathcal S_{\rm signed} = \sum_{\substack{d_1, d_2 \in \mathcal D_{\rm odd} \\ d_1 \ne d_2}} \chi_4(d_1)\chi_4(d_2) K_{d_1, d_2}.
$$
Since $d$ is odd, $\chi_4(d)^2 = 1$. The diagonal elements of the kernel are:
$$
K_{d,d} = w_D(d)^2 \sum_{1\le |h|\le H_D} |\alpha_{h,H_D}| \asymp w_D(d)^2 \log H_D.

$$
\mathcal Q_{\rm diag} \asymp D \log H_D \ll X^{1/2} \log X.

$$
|\mathcal M_1(D;X)|^2 \ll_\epsilon X^{1/2+2\epsilon}.

Substituting our bounds:
$$
|\mathcal M_1(D;X)|^2 \le 16 (\log H_D) (\mathcal Q_{\rm diag} + \mathcal S_{\rm signed}) \ll (\log X) (X^{1/2} \log X + \mathcal S_{\rm signed}).
$$
For this to be bounded by $X^{1/2+2\epsilon}$, we must have:
$$
\mathcal S_{\rm signed} \ll_\epsilon X^{1/2+\epsilon}.
$$
[PROVED] We have derived the exact target: The signed off-diagonal statistic $\mathcal S_{\rm signed}$ must be bounded by $X^{1/2+\epsilon}$. If $\mathcal S_{\rm signed}$ exceeds this bound, the Cauchy--Schwarz step is too lossy, and the `M9a` target cannot be reached via this standard reduction.

$$
\mathcal Q = v^* U^* K U v.

$$

$$
\|U^* K U\|_{\operatorname{op}} = \sup_{\|x\|=1} \|U^* K U x\| = \sup_{\|y\|=1} \|K y\| = \|K\|_{\operatorname{op}},

where we used the substitution $y = Ux$ and the fact that $U$ is an isometry.

[PROVED] The bound $|\mathcal D_{\rm odd}| \|K\|_{\operatorname{op}}$ is entirely independent of the character $\chi_4$. Any method that reduces the `M9a` sum to an operator-norm bound on the conjugated matrix $U^* K U$ is strictly character-blind.

### 4. Writing H12 Narrowly: Pure Cyclic Trace Invariance

We narrowly prove the trace invariance for pure cyclic moments.

If a method attempts to bound the spectral radius of $U^* K U$ by computing the trace of a high power $m$, it evaluates:
$$
\operatorname{Tr}((U^* K U)^m).

$$
\operatorname{Tr}((U^* K U)^m) = \operatorname{Tr}(U^* K^m U) = \operatorname{Tr}(K^m U U^*) = \operatorname{Tr}(K^m).
$$
[PROVED] Pure conjugacy-invariant cyclic traces of the form $\operatorname{Tr}((U^* K U)^m)$ are identical to the unsigned traces $\operatorname{Tr}(K^m)$. They cannot exploit the spatial character.

This narrowly blocks eigenvalue-moment methods that rely solely on the sum of the $m$-th powers of the eigenvalues of the conjugated matrix.
