## Possible errors or hidden assumptions

- **A1's H4 statement might contain a sign error.** The coefficient $\alpha_{h,H}$ is given as $-\frac{\Phi(|h|/(H+1))}{2\pi i h}$. Vaaler's Theorem 6 (2.28) states $\widehat J(t)=\pi t(1-|t|)\cot(\pi t)+|t|$ for $0<|t|<1$, and the approximation uses a convolution with $j_N$; the exact sign depends on the definition of $j_N$ and the convention $e(t)=e^{2\pi i t}$. Without a verbatim check, the sign could be opposite, which would modify all paired real formulas (sign of imaginary part in $\mathcal M_1$, sign of real part in $\mathcal M_2$) but would not affect the coarser $|\alpha_h|\asymp 1/|h|$ estimates used in many diagnostics.
- **A2's BSOS derivation uses only the odd denominator space $\mathcal D_{\rm odd}$.** The M9a sum originally runs over all $d\asymp D$, but even denominators are omitted because $\chi_4(d)=0$ for even $d$. This is correct; however, the dyadic weight $w_D$ may vanish outside $[D,2D]$, and the definition of $\mathcal D_{\rm odd}$ as $\{d: D\le d<2D,\ 2\nmid d\}$ is valid. The implicit assumption is that $D$ and $2D$ are chosen so that the interval contains enough odd integers; this is unproblematic for large $X$.
- **A2's H13-Dual analysis assumes the dual amplitude $A(h,m)$ depends on $h$ in a sufficiently benign way** that the CS step with $|\alpha_h|$ weights can be carried out. If $A(h,m)$ has $h$-dependent decay that is not compatible with the weight function, the simple decomposition into a quadratic form $v^*\widetilde U^*\widetilde K\widetilde Uv$ might not hold in the stated form. However, even if the CS step is more complicated, the diagonal $\chi_4(m)$ factor will still be a unitary diagonal operator acting on the $m$-space, and any operator-norm bound that passes through $U^* K U$ will remain blind.
- **The paired real formulas for $\mathcal M_2$ with $\Re B_h$** rely on $\chi_4(-h)=-\chi_4(h)$ and the conjugate pair $\alpha_{-h,H}=\overline{\alpha_{h,H}}$. This is correct under the stated conventions. However, A1 correctly notes that these formulas are valid only for **real dyadic weights**; if the weights become complex, the raw complex definitions must be used.
