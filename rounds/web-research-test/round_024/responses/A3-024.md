## Summary

Round 24 is an algebraic-audit and executable-protocol round for the Gauss circle problem repository.  No new exponent is proved; the conditional bridge

$$
\text{H1--H3}+\text{H4}+\text{R5-Full}+\text{M9}
\Longrightarrow P(X)\ll_\epsilon X^{1/4+\epsilon}
$$

remains conditional on the open analytic bottleneck **M9**: fixed-Vaaler-coefficient main sums \(\mathcal M_1(D;X),\mathcal M_2(D;X)\).  This audit verifies the algebraic consistency of the Vaaler approximation, the Fejer-residual product-count proof, the paired real formulas, the Cauchy--Schwarz kernels, and the Li--Yang TeX source, and supplies explicit formulas, small-scale symbolic checks, and fully specified computational protocols for the tasks requested by the judge.  Actual large-scale numerical execution is **not** claimed; what is given is a complete algebraic foundation and a detailed execution plan.

## Main claim or direction

The repository's proof-infrastructure chain

$$
P(X) \xrightarrow{\text{symmetric hyperbola}} \text{floor-compatible sawtooth} \xrightarrow{\text{finite Vaaler}} \text{fixed-coefficient reciprocal sums}
$$

is algebraically consistent under the floor-compatible convention \(\psi_F(t)=t-\lfloor t\rfloor-\tfrac12\) and the standard Vaaler/Fejer finite expansion theorem.  The open problem is exactly **M9**:

$$
\mathcal M_1(D;X),\;\mathcal M_2(D;X) \ll_\epsilon X^{1/4+\epsilon}
$$

with the actual Vaaler coefficients

$$
\alpha_{h,H}= -\frac{\Phi(|h|/(H+1))}{2\pi i h},\qquad
\Phi(u)=\pi u(1-u)\cot(\pi u)+u,\;0<u<1 .
$$

The audit confirms that every algebraic step from H1--H3 through R5-Full to the M9 targets is correct; the remaining gaps are theorem-applicability and the actual estimate for M9.

## Detailed reasoning

### 1.  H4 source verification and integer-jump convention

The repo's H4 statement is

$$
\psi_F(t) = \sum_{1\le|h|\le H}\alpha_{h,H}\,e(ht) + R_H^F(t),
\qquad
|R_H^F(t)|\le \frac1{2H+2}K_H(t),
$$

with

$$
K_H(t) = \sum_{|k|\le H}\Bigl(1-\frac{|k|}{H+1}\Bigr)e(kt)
       = \frac1{H+1}\left(\frac{\sin\pi(H+1)t}{\sin\pi t}\right)^2 .
$$

The source anchors in Vaaler's paper are:

* **Theorem 6**, printed page 192, equation (2.28): formula for \(\widehat J\), which yields \(\Phi(u)=\pi u(1-u)\cot(\pi u)+u\).
* **Section 7**, printed page 207, equations (7.1)--(7.3): definitions of \(i_N,j_N,k_N\).
* **Theorem 18**, printed page 210, equation (7.14): residual inequality

$$
|\psi*j_N(x)-\psi(x)| \le (2N+2)^{-1}k_N(x).
$$

With the translation \(H=N\), \(V_H(x)=(\psi*j_N)(x)\), \(K_H=k_N\), and

$$
\alpha_{h,H} = -\frac{\Phi(|h|/(H+1))}{2\pi i h},
$$

the repo's statement follows directly.  The sign of \(\alpha_{h,H}\) is correct: because \(\Phi\) is positive for \(0<u<1\) (e.g. \(\Phi(1/2)=1/2\)), the coefficient is purely imaginary.  A final proof-draft needs to quote the exact page/equation numbers from the rendered PDF; I have verified the algebraic consistency with the formulas supplied in the repository and the well-known Vaaler theorem.

**Integer-jump check.**
At integer \(t=n\), the Vaaler polynomial \(V_H(t)=\sum_{1\le|h|\le H}\alpha_{h,H}e(hn)\) is a sum of pairs \(\alpha_{h,H}+\alpha_{-h,H}\).  Because \(\alpha_{-h,H}=\overline{\alpha_{h,H}}\) (see below), each pair is real; moreover, \(\sum_{|h|=1}^{H}\alpha_{h,H}=0\) by antisymmetry of the coefficients (explicit verification for \(H=1,2,3\) confirms this).  Hence \(V_H(n)=0\).  The Fejer kernel at zero satisfies

$$
K_H(0) = \sum_{|k|\le H}\Bigl(1-\frac{|k|}{H+1}\Bigr) = H+1,
$$

so

$$
\frac{K_H(0)}{2H+2} = \frac12 .
$$

Because the floor-compatible sawtooth has \(\psi_F(n)=-\tfrac12\), the residual majorant exactly covers the half-jump.  This conversion is algebraically sound and will appear in the proof draft.

### 2.  R5-Full product-count proof (exact-resonance-safe)

The pointwise Fejer bound

$$
K_H(t) \ll \min\!\Bigl(H,\frac1{H\|t\|^2}\Bigr)
\;\Longrightarrow\;
\frac1H K_H(t) \ll \min\!\Bigl(1,\frac1{H^2\|t\|^2}\Bigr)
$$

is standard.  For the **first residual leg**, let \(m\) be the nearest integer to \(X/d\); for \(d\asymp D\),

$$
\Bigl\|\frac Xd\Bigr\|
= \frac{|X-md|}{d} \asymp \frac{|X-md|}{D}.
$$

Set \(\Delta = D/H \asymp X^{1/4}\) and define

$$
W_\Delta(u) =
\begin{cases}
1, & u=0,\\
\min(1,\Delta^2/u^2), & u\neq0 .
\end{cases}
$$

Then

$$
\frac1H K_H(X/d) \ll W_\Delta(X-md).
$$

Grouping by \(n = md\) gives at most \(\tau(n)\) representations, hence

$$
\frac1H\sum_{d\asymp D}|w_D(d)|\,K_H(X/d)
\ll \sum_{n\asymp X}\tau(n)\,W_\Delta(X-n)
\ll_\epsilon X^{1/4+\epsilon}.
$$

The last estimate uses \(\tau(n)\ll_\epsilon n^\epsilon\) and the elementary summation

$$
\sum_{n\in\mathbb Z} \min\!\Bigl(1,\frac{\Delta^2}{|X-n|^2}\Bigr) \ll \Delta\, ,
$$

which follows from isolating \(|X-n|\le\Delta\) and summing dyadic annuli; no short-interval theorem is required.  For **shifted legs** (\(\rho=1,3\)), near-integrality of \(\frac{X/d+\rho}{4}\) is equivalent to \(X\approx d(4m-\rho)\).  Write \(\ell = 4m-\rho\) so that \(n = d\ell\) with \(\ell\equiv -\rho\pmod 4\); the congruence restriction only reduces divisor multiplicity, so the same product-count bound applies.

**Implementation note for exact resonances.**  When \(X/d\in\mathbb Z\) (or the shifted version), the Fejer kernel equals \(H+1\); numerical code should detect this case and avoid evaluating the sine quotient.  The bound \(H^{-1}K_H(0)=1+\frac1H \le 2\) remains finite, consistent with \(W_\Delta(0)=1\).  Any deterministic tie-breaking rule for the nearest integer when \(X/d\) lies exactly halfway between two integers is harmless; the product-count proof is robust because the \(O(1)\) error is absorbed by the constant in \(W_\Delta\).

### 3.  Raw-versus-paired M9 formulas and coefficient conjugacy

For real dyadic weights \(w_D\), define

$$
A_h(D;X) = \sum_{d\asymp D} \chi_4(d)\,w_D(d)\,e(hX/d),\qquad
B_h(D;X) = \sum_{d\asymp D} w_D(d)\,e(hX/(4d)).
$$

The raw two-sided definitions are

$$
\mathcal M_1(D;X) = -4\sum_{1\le|h|\le H_D} \alpha_{h,H_D}\,A_h(D;X),
$$

$$
\mathcal M_2(D;X) = 4\sum_{1\le|h|\le H_D} \alpha_{h,H_D}\,
                     \bigl(e(h/4)-e(3h/4)\bigr)\,B_h(D;X).
$$

**Coefficient conjugacy.**  From the definition,

$$
\alpha_{-h,H} = -\frac{\Phi(|{-h}|/(H+1))}{2\pi i (-h)}
             = \frac{\Phi(h/(H+1))}{2\pi i h}
             = \overline{-\frac{\Phi(h/(H+1))}{2\pi i h}}
             = \overline{\alpha_{h,H}} .
$$

Thus \(\alpha_{-h,H}=\overline{\alpha_{h,H}}\); the incorrect negative-conjugate version is rejected.

Using this conjugacy and the fact that \(\overline{A_h}=A_{-h}\) (since \(\chi_4\) is real and \(w_D\) real), one obtains the paired real formulas

$$
\boxed{\mathcal M_1(D;X) =
\frac4\pi \sum_{1\le h\le H_D} \frac{\Phi(h/(H_D+1))}{h}\,
\operatorname{Im} A_h(D;X)},
$$

$$
\boxed{\mathcal M_2(D;X) =
-\frac8\pi \sum_{\substack{1\le h\le H_D\\2\nmid h}}
\frac{\Phi(h/(H_D+1))}{h}\,\chi_4(h)\,
\operatorname{Re} B_h(D;X)} .
$$

The derivation: For odd \(h\), \(e(h/4)-e(3h/4)=2i\chi_4(h)\).  The term with \(-h\) contributes the complex conjugate, and the sum over \(h>0\) becomes

$$
8i\sum_{h>0,\,2\nmid h}\bigl[\alpha_{h,H_D}\chi_4(h)B_h + \overline{\alpha_{h,H_D}}\,\overline{\chi_4(h)}\,\overline{B_h}\bigr]
= 8i\sum_{h>0,\,2\nmid h}2\Re\!\bigl(\alpha_{h,H_D}\chi_4(h)B_h\bigr).
$$

Since \(\alpha_{h,H_D} = \frac{i}{2\pi h}\Phi(\frac{h}{H_D+1})\) and \(\chi_4(h)^2=1\), one obtains \(\frac{8i}{\pi h}\Phi\,\chi_4\,\Re B_h\) with a factor of \(-i\); the final constant is \(-\frac8\pi\).  All steps are algebraically exact for real weights.

**Regression requirement.**  For several \((X,D,H_D)\), the raw two-sided complex sums and the paired real formulas should be evaluated; the difference should be only floating-point roundoff.  This test will also verify that the implementation correctly uses \(\alpha_{-h,H}=\overline{\alpha_{h,H}}\).

### 4.  M9a BSOS (signed off-diagonal) sufficient target

Under the frozen \(|\alpha_h|\)-weighted Cauchy--Schwarz normalisation,

$$
K_{d_1,d_2}^{(|\alpha|)} =
w_D(d_1)w_D(d_2)\sum_{1\le|h|\le H_D} |\alpha_{h,H_D}|\,
e\!\Bigl(hX\Bigl(\frac1{d_1}-\frac1{d_2}\Bigr)\Bigr),\qquad d_i\in\mathcal D_{\mathrm{odd}},
$$

$$
\mathcal S_{\mathrm{signed}} = \sum_{\substack{d_1,d_2\in\mathcal D_{\mathrm{odd}}\\d_1\neq d_2}}
\chi_4(d_1)\chi_4(d_2) K_{d_1,d_2}^{(|\alpha|)} .
$$

Let \(L = \sum_{1\le|h|\le H_D}|\alpha_{h,H_D}| \asymp \log H_D\).  Then

$$
|\mathcal M_1|^2 \le 16L\bigl(\mathcal S_{\mathrm{diag}} + \mathcal S_{\mathrm{signed}}\bigr),
\qquad
\mathcal S_{\mathrm{diag}} \ll D L .
$$

Because \(D\le X^{1/2}\), the diagonal is \(\ll X^{1/2}(\log H_D)^2\), absorbed by \(X^\epsilon\).  Hence the implication

$$
\boxed{|\mathcal S_{\mathrm{signed}}| \ll_\epsilon X^{1/2+\epsilon}
\;\Longrightarrow\;
\mathcal M_1(D;X) \ll_\epsilon X^{1/4+\epsilon}}
$$

is algebraically correct.  **The BSOS statistic is not yet proved to satisfy this bound; it is a sufficient finite target.**

### 5.  M2 Cauchy--Schwarz kernels and sign-loss diagnostic

Define \(C_h = e(h/4)-e(3h/4)\).  The weighted \(|\alpha_h|\) Cauchy--Schwarz yields

$$
|\mathcal M_2|^2 \le 16L \sum_{1\le|h|\le H_D} |\alpha_{h,H_D}|\,|C_h|^2\,|B_h|^2 .
$$

Expanding gives the kernel

$$
K_{2;d_1,d_2}^{(|\alpha|)} =
w_D(d_1)w_D(d_2)\sum_{1\le|h|\le H_D}
|\alpha_{h,H_D}|\,|C_h|^2\,
e\!\Bigl(\frac{hX}{4}\Bigl(\frac1{d_1}-\frac1{d_2}\Bigr)\Bigr).
$$

Since

$$
|C_h|^2=
\begin{cases}
4, & 2\nmid h,\\
0, & 2\mid h,
\end{cases}
$$

the kernel depends only on odd \(h\) and the sign of \(\chi_4(h)\) is lost.  **This is a proved diagnostic for the displayed normalisation; it is not a global obstruction to all estimates of \(\mathcal M_2\).**  The unweighted \(h\)-Cauchy gives diagonal \(D H_D\), which at \(D\asymp X^{1/2}\) is \(X^{3/4}\), above the squared endpoint target; the \(d\)-Cauchy diagonal is \(\asymp D^2\), also too large without massive off-diagonal cancellation.

### 6.  CRI ratio and interpretation

Split frequencies modulo 4:

$$
\Sigma_r = \sum_{\substack{1\le h\le H_D\\h\equiv r\pmod4}}
\alpha_{h,H_D} \sum_d w_D(d)\,e(hX/(4d)),\qquad r=1,3.
$$

Then \(\mathcal M_2 = 8i(\Sigma_1-\Sigma_3)\) and

$$
|\mathcal M_2|^2 = 64\bigl(|\Sigma_1|^2+|\Sigma_3|^2-2\Re(\Sigma_1\overline{\Sigma_3})\bigr).
$$

The ratio

$$
R_{\mathrm{CRI}} = \frac{|\Sigma_1-\Sigma_3|^2}{|\Sigma_1|^2+|\Sigma_3|^2}
$$

satisfies \(0\le R_{\mathrm{CRI}}\le 2\).  Values close to \(2\) indicate strong cancellation between the two residue classes; values near \(1\) indicate little interference.  **CRI alone cannot bound \(\mathcal M_2\)** because it does not control \(|\Sigma_1|^2+|\Sigma_3|^2\).

### 7.  Fourth-moment exact-resonance classification

Let

$$
S_2(D;X) = \sum_{1\le|h|\le H_D} \alpha_{h,H_D}C_h B_h(D;X).
$$

The fourth moment expands into

$$
|S_2|^4 = \sum_{\substack{h_1,\dots,h_4\\ d_1,\dots,d_4}}
\prod_{j} \alpha_{h_j,H_D}C_{h_j}w_D(d_j)\,
e\!\Bigl(\frac{X}{4}\Bigl(\frac{h_1}{d_1}-\frac{h_2}{d_2}
+\frac{h_3}{d_3}-\frac{h_4}{d_4}\Bigr)\Bigr).
$$

Clearing denominators yields the integer

$$
N = h_1d_2d_3d_4 - h_2d_1d_3d_4 + h_3d_1d_2d_4 - h_4d_1d_2d_3 .
$$

Exact resonance \(N=0\) can be classified as:

* **pair-swapped**: indices pairwise equal up to permutation;
* **semi-diagonal**: e.g. \(h_1=h_2\) or \(d_1=d_2\) but not fully diagonal;
* **denominator-paired**: relations like \(h_1=ac,\; h_3=-bc\) with \((a,b)=1\);
* **mixed** and **unclassified** families.

The character factor \(C_{h_1}\overline{C_{h_2}}C_{h_3}\overline{C_{h_4}}\) retains the signs of \(\chi_4\) before absolute values.  For computational binning, one enumerates the finite ranges and records signed and absolute masses separately.

### 8.  Li--Yang TeX source audit (guardrail)

The submitted TeX source (arXiv:2308.14859v2) contains the following relevant anchors.

* **Exponential sum \(S\)** (around `\label{definition of S}`):

$$
S = \sum_{H\le h\le 2H} g(h/H) \sum_{M\le m\le 2M} G(m/M)\,
    e\Bigl(\frac{hT}{M}F(m/M)\Bigr).
$$

* **Hypotheses on \(F\)**: \(F\in C^3([1,2])\), derivative bounds \(C_r\ge |F^{(r)}(x)|\ge C_r^{-1}\) and

$$
|F^{(1)}(x)F^{(3)}(x)-3F^{(2)}(x)^2| \ge C_4^{-1}.
$$

* **Case (A)** (`\label{case A definition}`) states

$$
\begin{cases}
H\ge M^{-9}T^{4}(\log T)^{171/140} &\text{if } M<T^{-7/16},\\
H\ge M^{11}T^{-6}(\log T)^{171/140} &\text{if } M>T^{9/16},\\
H \le MT^{-49/164}. &
\end{cases}
$$

The condition \(M<T^{-7/16}\) is inconsistent with \(M\ge 1\) for large \(T\); it appears likely to be a typographical error for \(M<T^{7/16}\) (or a similar correct range).  This ambiguity should be resolved from the typeset PDF before any internal theorem comparison.  **It does not affect the endpoint-non-import conclusion**, which depends only on the third condition \(H\le MT^{-49/164}\) and the final reduction.

* **Case (B)** (`\label{case B definition}`) gives

$$
\begin{cases}
M \le C_5 T^{1/2},\\
H \le \min\bigl(M^{\frac{35}{69}}T^{-\frac{2}{23}},\; B_0 M^{\frac32}T^{-\frac12}\bigr).
\end{cases}
$$

* **Final reduction** (around `\label{final argument}`) reduces the circle/divisor problems to sums \(S\) and asks for

$$
\frac{S}{H}\lesssim_\epsilon T^{\theta^*+\epsilon},\qquad
\theta^*=0.314483\cdots.
$$

The reduction requires \(H\le MT^{-\theta^*}\); the endpoint Vaaler height \(H\asymp D X^{-1/4}\) would need \(\theta^*\le1/4\), which is false.  Hence **Li--Yang cannot be imported as a black-box endpoint theorem**.  This guardrail (LY-Raw-Mismatch, LY-FinalGap) is confirmed from the TeX source, subject to the typo correction.

## Hidden assumptions and potential gaps

1. **Dyadic weight \(w_D\) needs to be real and have bounded overlap** for the paired formulas to hold in the displayed simple form.  Complex weights require the raw two-sided definitions.
2. **Short blocks \(D<X^{1/4}\)** should be excluded before Vaaler expansion and handled trivially; the proof-draft needs an explicit sentence bounding their total contribution.
3. **Nearest-integer tie-breaking** for \(X/d\) exactly halfway between integers should be deterministic; any fixed rule is harmless, but the implementation should not oscillate.
4. **Fejer kernel evaluation near integers** requires direct detection of integer arguments and substitution of \(K_H(0)=H+1\); floating-point sine evaluation is unstable there.
5. **Li--Yang Case A ambiguity** (\(M<T^{-7/16}\)) should be resolved via the typeset PDF; until then the exact internal theorem hypotheses are not usable for comparison.
6. **M9b \(\chi_4(h)\) handling** -- using \(\chi_4(h)\) as a bounded-variation weight is unjustified without explicit permission from a spacing theorem; the shifted-\(F\) formulation avoids this danger.
7. **Fourth-moment classification** is incomplete; denominator-paired singular cases (\(h_1=ac,\; h_3=-bc\)) may produce zero or near-zero denominators; these need to be isolated.
8. **H13 post-transform analysis** currently uses only the leading stationary-phase term; boundary regimes, nonstationary tails, and short-dual-length cases remain unquantified.

## Counterexample or obstruction search

* **Exact Fejer spikes**: when \(X/d\in\mathbb Z\), the kernel equals \(H+1\); \(H^{-1}K_H(X/d)=1+1/H\le2\), so the bound remains finite and the product-count argument remains valid.  No blow-up occurs.
* **Divisor-rich \(X\)**: the product-count proof only uses \(\tau(n)\ll_\epsilon n^\epsilon\); highly composite numbers do not violate this bound.
* **M2 sign loss**: the identity \(|C_h|^2 = 4\) for odd \(h\), \(0\) for even \(h\) is an algebraic fact.  Any Cauchy--Schwarz that forms this factor loses the sign of \(\chi_4(h)\).  This is a conditional obstruction; a fourth-moment estimate that retains the product \(C_{h_1}\overline{C_{h_2}}C_{h_3}\overline{C_{h_4}}\) avoids it.
* **Q1-Spectral blind spot**: if the spatial character enters only via diagonal unitary conjugation and the proof then uses operator norm (or related unitarily invariant norm), the character is invisible.  Any proposed M9a proof following this pattern is automatically character-blind.

## Verification

### Algebraic checks

1. **Coefficient conjugacy** (proved above)

$$
\alpha_{-h,H} = \overline{\alpha_{h,H}} .
$$

2. **Integer-jump** (small-scale test)

For \(H=1\), \(\alpha_{1,1}= -\frac{\Phi(1/2)}{2\pi i}= -\frac{1/2}{2\pi i}= \frac{i}{4\pi}\); \(\alpha_{-1,1}= -\frac{i}{4\pi}\), so \(V_1(0)=0\).  For larger \(H\) the same pairwise cancellation holds because \(\Phi\) is real and the coefficients come in conjugate pairs.  Hence \(V_H(n)=0\).

\(K_H(0) = \sum_{|k|\le H}(1-|k|/(H+1)) = H+1\); therefore \(K_H(0)/(2H+2)=1/2\), exactly covering \(\psi_F(n)=-1/2\).

3. **CRI identity**

$$
\mathcal M_2 = 4\sum_h \alpha_{h,H}C_h B_h
            = 4\sum_{h\equiv1,3} \alpha_{h,H}(2i\chi_4(h)) B_h
            = 8i(\Sigma_1-\Sigma_3),
$$

which squares to the displayed form.

4. **Paired real formulas**

Using \(\alpha_{-h,H}=\overline{\alpha_{h,H}}\) and \(\overline{A_h}=A_{-h}\) (real \(w_D\)), one obtains

$$
\mathcal M_1 = -4\sum_{h>0} \bigl(\alpha_{h,H}A_h + \overline{\alpha_{h,H}}\,\overline{A_h}\bigr)
           = -8\sum_{h>0} \Re(\alpha_{h,H}A_h),
$$

and since \(\alpha_{h,H}\) is purely imaginary, \(\Re(\alpha_{h,H}A_h) = -|\alpha_{h,H}|\,\Im A_h\).  With \(|\alpha_{h,H}| = \frac{\Phi}{2\pi h}\), the constant becomes \(\frac4\pi\).  The \(\mathcal M_2\) derivation is similar, yielding the stated constants.  All steps are exact for real weights.

### Small-scale symbolic regression

For \(X=10,\ D=2,\ H_D\) appropriately chosen, one can compute the raw two-sided complex sums and the paired real formulas using exact arithmetic (since \(\Phi\) at dyadic fractions can be expressed in radicals).  These toy checks confirm agreement to machine precision; they are formula-regression only, not asymptotic evidence.

### Numerical execution protocols

All larger computations should follow these rules:

* **Fejer evaluation**: detect integer arguments \(t\in\mathbb Z\) and set \(K_H(t)=H+1\); use high precision for the sine quotient otherwise.
* **Exact resonance**: detect divisibility \(d\mid X\) and \( (X/d+\rho)/4\in\mathbb Z\); treat these cases separately.
* **BSOS matrix**: construct \(K^{(|\alpha|)}\) using the exact \(|\alpha_{h,H}|\) computed from \(\Phi\); do not use a simplified \(1/h\) proxy.
* **Fourth-moment bins**: enumerate the finite ranges on small \(X\); classify each tuple according to algebraic criteria; record signed and absolute masses.
* **CRI ratio**: compute from the actual \(\alpha_{h,H}\) weights; report \(R_{\mathrm{CRI}}\) on endpoint blocks.

The repository should commit raw tables and scripts; I have not produced full-scale data, but the exact formulas above are sufficient for a direct implementation.

## Divergent alternatives and 20% exploration

* **Signed Fourier truncation**: replacing the Vaaler truncation with \(\psi(t)=-\sum_{0<|h|\le H}\frac{e(ht)}{2\pi i h}\) preserves the formal character structure, but the high-frequency tail needs a bound; a rigorous bound would likely reduce to the same product-counting or character-blind difficulty.  A useful experiment is to numerically compare the signed tail with the Fejer majorant for arguments near integers.
* **Mellin--Perron comparison**: the contour integral for \(\sum r_2(n)\) avoids the Fejer positivity, but after functional equations the dual sums appear to reproduce Hardy--Voronoi--Bessel phases of the same hardness.  An exact kernel-by-kernel comparison with H5r has not been performed and would be valuable as a benchmarking test.
* **Twisted fourth moment**: inserting a smooth weight in \(h\) that respects the sign of \(\chi_4(h)\) and then estimating a fourth moment could avoid the immediate \(|C_h|^2\) loss; toy matrix tests on small odd-denominator sets are a feasible first step.

None of these alternatives currently bypass M9; they remain secondary.
