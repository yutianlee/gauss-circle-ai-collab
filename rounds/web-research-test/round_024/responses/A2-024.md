## Detailed reasoning: Frozen $h$ Convention and $\mathcal M_2$ Expansion

$$
\alpha_{h,H_D} = -\frac{\Phi(|h|/(H_D+1))}{2\pi i h}, \qquad \Phi(u) = \pi u(1-u)\cot(\pi u) + u.
$$

$$
\alpha_{-h,H_D} = \overline{\alpha_{h,H_D}}.
$$
Define the frequency character factor:
$$
C_h = e(h/4) - e(3h/4).
$$
For odd $h$, $C_h = 2i\chi_4(h)$. For even $h$, $C_h = 0$. The conjugacy relation for the character factor is:
$$
C_{-h} = e(-h/4) - e(-3h/4) = -C_h = \overline{C_h}.

Thus, the combined coefficient $\beta_h = \alpha_{h,H_D} C_h$ satisfies $\beta_{-h} = \overline{\beta_h}$.

We define the base sum $S_2(D;X)$ over the two-sided symmetric range:
$$
S_2(D;X) = \sum_{1 \le |h| \le H_D} \alpha_{h,H_D} C_h \sum_{d \asymp D} w_D(d) e\left(\frac{hX}{4d}\right).

$$

## Detailed reasoning: Cross-Residue Interference (CRI) Statistic

To isolate the sign cancellation, we formalize the CRI statistic. Define the residue-restricted sums:
$$
\Sigma_r(D;X) = \sum_{\substack{1 \le |h| \le H_D \\ h \equiv r \pmod 4}} \alpha_{h,H_D} \sum_{d \asymp D} w_D(d) e\left(\frac{hX}{4d}\right), \qquad r \in \{1,3\}.
$$
Under the two-sided convention, $C_h = 2i$ for $h \equiv 1 \pmod 4$ and $C_h = -2i$ for $h \equiv 3 \pmod 4$. Thus:
$$
S_2(D;X) = 2i \Sigma_1(D;X) - 2i \Sigma_3(D;X) = 2i (\Sigma_1 - \Sigma_3).
$$
The official main term is $\mathcal M_2 = 4 S_2 = 8i (\Sigma_1 - \Sigma_3)$. The squared magnitude is exactly:
$$
|\mathcal M_2(D;X)|^2 = 64 |\Sigma_1 - \Sigma_3|^2 = 64 \left( |\Sigma_1|^2 + |\Sigma_3|^2 - 2\Re(\Sigma_1 \overline{\Sigma_3}) \right).
$$
`[PROVED]` If a method bounds only $|\Sigma_1|^2 + |\Sigma_3|^2$, it is performing a standard residue-separated square estimate, which yields a character-blind reciprocal sum. The target $|\mathcal M_2|^2 \ll_\epsilon X^{1/2+\epsilon}$ would follow from sufficiently strong cancellation in the full expression, including the cross-residue interference term $2\Re(\Sigma_1 \overline{\Sigma_3})$.

We define the falsification metric:
$$
R_{\text{CRI}} = \frac{|\Sigma_1 - \Sigma_3|^2}{|\Sigma_1|^2 + |\Sigma_3|^2}.
$$
If $R_{\text{CRI}} \ll 1$, the character signs are providing useful cancellation in this statistic. If $R_{\text{CRI}} \approx 1$, the CRI approach is not capturing the required interference in that tested range.

## Boundary-case stress tests

This response leaves several proof obligations open, but those obligations can be stress-tested with small exact computations before a full theorem is attempted. The goal of these tests is to distinguish three possibilities: a false heuristic, a true absolute-count obstruction with signed cancellation, or a true absolute-count obstruction without signed cancellation.

**Stress test 1: coefficient endpoint taper.** The estimate $|\alpha_h|\asymp 1/|h|$ is accurate away from the endpoint of the Vaaler range, but the exact coefficient is

**Stress test 2: low-frequency dominance.** The same coefficient size can make small $|h|$ disproportionately important. **Stress test 4: parity and residue bias.** The signed factor is

If the near-collision condition biases the tuple set toward one product sign, cancellation may be weaker than expected.
