## Possible errors or hidden assumptions

A3's CRI interpretation needs correction. For

$$
R_{\rm CRI}
=
\frac{|\Sigma_1-\Sigma_3|^2}{|\Sigma_1|^2+|\Sigma_3|^2},
$$

one has $0\le R_{\rm CRI}\le2$. Values near $0$ indicate strong cancellation in the difference $\Sigma_1-\Sigma_3$; values near $1$ indicate little useful interference; values near $2$ indicate anti-alignment that makes the difference large. A3's statement that values close to $2$ indicate useful cancellation is backwards.

A2's $X^{5/4}$ warning should stay explicitly heuristic. It assumes typical $|h_i|\asymp H_D$, roughly uniform $N$ fibers, and no strong structural sparsity. Low-frequency dominance could make the $H_D^{-4}$ coefficient penalty too optimistic; concentrated fibers could also make the absolute mass larger or smaller than predicted.

A2's CRI statistic should be kept convention-consistent. The two-sided $\Sigma_r$ convention differs from the one-sided paired real convention. Both are legitimate, but constants and conjugations should be frozen before A3 computes ratios.

A3's H4 integer-jump explanation should be written more cleanly. The reason $V_H(n)=0$ is that for $h>0$, $\alpha_{h,H}$ is purely imaginary and $\alpha_{-h,H}=\overline{\alpha_{h,H}}=-\alpha_{h,H}$, so the pair cancels at integer arguments. Saying only that the pair is real is insufficient; the pair sum should be shown to vanish.
