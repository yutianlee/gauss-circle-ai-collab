## Claims that look correct

The following claims from the Stage A outputs are mathematically sound and should be retained in the repository's lemma bank or diagnostic register:

1. **Vaaler Coefficient Conjugacy (`CCoef-R22`)**:
   *Claim*: For $1 \le h \le H$, $\alpha_{-h,H} = \overline{\alpha_{h,H}}$.
   *Reason*: The coefficient is defined as $\alpha_{h,H} = -\frac{\Phi(|h|/(H+1))}{2\pi i h}$. Since $\Phi(u)$ is real-valued for $u \in (0,1)$, we have $\alpha_{h,H} = \frac{i}{2\pi h} \Phi(|h|/(H+1))$. Substituting $-h$ yields $\alpha_{-h,H} = \frac{i}{2\pi (-h)} \Phi(|-h|/(H+1)) = -\frac{i}{2\pi h} \Phi(|h|/(H+1))$, which is exactly the complex conjugate of $\alpha_{h,H}$.

2. **Falsification of $\Phi$ Symmetry**:
   *Claim*: $\Phi(1-u) \neq \Phi(u)$ in general.
   *Reason*: Exact evaluation at $u=1/3$ and $u=2/3$ yields distinct values, as demonstrated by A3. The function $\Phi(1-u) = 1-u - \pi u(1-u)\cot(\pi u)$ is not identically equal to $\Phi(u)$.

3. **Endpoint Jump Convention for `H4`**:
   *Claim*: The Fejer residual majorant exactly covers the floor-compatible half-jump at integers.
   *Reason*: The centered Vaaler polynomial $V_H(t)$ evaluates to $0$ at $t \in \mathbb{Z}$. The floor-compatible sawtooth is defined as $\psi_F(n) = -1/2$. The residual majorant is bounded by $\frac{1}{2H+2} K_H(t)$. At $t=0$, $K_H(0) = \sum_{|k| \le H} 1 = 2H+1$, wait. The definition of $K_H(t)$ in the repo is $K_H(t) = \sum_{|k| \le H} \left(1 - \frac{|k|}{H+1}\right) e(kt)$. At $t=0$, this evaluates to $1 + 2 \sum_{k=1}^H \left(1 - \frac{k}{H+1}\right) = 1 + 2 \left( H - \frac{H(H+1)}{2(H+1)} \right) = 1 + 2(H - H/2) = 1 + H$. Thus, $K_H(0) = H+1$. The majorant bound is $\frac{H+1}{2H+2} = \frac{1}{2}$. This exactly matches $|\psi_F(n) - V_H(n)| = |-1/2 - 0| = 1/2$. The claim is correct.

4. **Paired Real-Weight Formula for $\mathcal M_1$**:
   *Claim*: For real dyadic weights $w_D$, $\mathcal M_1(D;X) = \frac{4}{\pi} \sum_{h=1}^{H_D} \frac{\Phi(h/(H_D+1))}{h} \operatorname{Im} A_h(D;X)$.
   *Reason*: The raw sum is $-4 \sum_{1 \le |h| \le H_D} \alpha_h A_h$. Pairing $h$ and $-h$, and using $A_{-h} = \overline{A_h}$ (since $w_D$ is real) and $\alpha_{-h} = \overline{\alpha_h}$, the $h$-th term is $\alpha_h A_h + \overline{\alpha_h} \overline{A_h} = 2 \operatorname{Re}(\alpha_h A_h)$. Substituting $\alpha_h = \frac{i \Phi}{2\pi h}$, we get $2 \operatorname{Re}\left( \frac{i \Phi}{2\pi h} (\operatorname{Re} A_h + i \operatorname{Im} A_h) \right) = 2 \operatorname{Re}\left( -\frac{\Phi}{2\pi h} \operatorname{Im} A_h + i \frac{\Phi}{2\pi h} \operatorname{Re} A_h \right) = -\frac{\Phi}{\pi h} \operatorname{Im} A_h$. Multiplying by the $-4$ prefactor yields $\frac{4}{\pi h} \Phi \operatorname{Im} A_h$. The claim is algebraically exact.

5. **Paired Real-Weight Formula for $\mathcal M_2$**:

Substituting $\alpha_h = \frac{i \Phi}{2\pi h}$, we get $2i \chi_4(h) \operatorname{Im}\left( \frac{i \Phi}{2\pi h} (\operatorname{Re} B_h + i \operatorname{Im} B_h) \right) = 2i \chi_4(h) \operatorname{Im}\left( -\frac{\Phi}{2\pi h} \operatorname{Im} B_h + i \frac{\Phi}{2\pi h} \operatorname{Re} B_h \right) = 2i \chi_4(h) \left( \frac{\Phi}{2\pi h} \operatorname{Re} B_h \right) = \frac{i \Phi}{\pi h} \chi_4(h) \operatorname{Re} B_h$. Multiplying by the $8i$ prefactor yields $8i \times \frac{i \Phi}{\pi h} \chi_4(h) \operatorname{Re} B_h = -\frac{8}{\pi h} \Phi \chi_4(h) \operatorname{Re} B_h$. The claim is algebraically exact.

## Possible errors or hidden assumptions

1. **Hidden Assumption in R5 Nearest-Integer Tie-Breaking**:
   The R5 product-count proof defines $m$ as the integer nearest to $X/d$. If $X/d$ is exactly a half-integer, a tie-breaking rule is required. The hidden assumption is that the choice of tie-breaking (e.g., always rounding up) does not systematically align with the congruence restrictions in the shifted legs ($\ell = 4m-\rho \equiv -\rho \pmod 4$) in a way that artificially inflates the divisor multiplicity beyond $\tau(n)$. This should be explicitly addressed in the proof draft.

2. **Failure Mode in $\mathcal Q_4(D;X)$ Semi-Diagonal Configurations**:
   The fourth-moment statistic $\mathcal Q_4(D;X)$ contains semi-diagonal terms where, for example, $h_1 = h_2$ and $d_1 = d_2$, but $h_3 \neq h_4$ and $d_3 \neq d_4$. In these configurations, the character factor $\beta_{h_1} \overline{\beta_{h_2}}$ becomes $|\beta_{h_1}|^2$, losing the sign of $\chi_4(h_1)$. If the sum of these semi-diagonal terms exceeds $X^{1+\epsilon}$, the fourth-moment approach will fail, regardless of the cancellation in the purely off-diagonal terms. The assumption that the semi-diagonals are trivially bounded by $X^{1+\epsilon}$ is a significant risk.

3. **Hidden Assumption in M9b Shifted-$F$ Theorem Applicability**:
   The formulation $F_{\rho,D}(z) = \frac{1}{4z} + \frac{\rho D}{4X}$ successfully matches the derivative non-degeneracy condition $F'F''' - 3(F'')^2 = -\frac{3}{8z^6}$. However, the Li-Yang/Bombieri-Iwaniec theorems require absolute bounds on the derivatives: $C_r \ge |F^{(r)}(z)| \ge C_r^{-1}$. The hidden assumption is that the additive shift $\frac{\rho D}{4X}$ does not violate the upper bound $C_0$ for the function value itself, or that the theorem's proof is strictly invariant under additive constants in the phase. If the spacing lemma relies on the magnitude of $F(z)$ (e.g., in a Taylor expansion remainder), the $D,X$-dependence of the shift could invalidate the uniform constants.

4. **Failure Mode in Li-Yang Case A Ambiguity**:
   A3's audit notes a discrepancy in the Li-Yang TeX source between $T^{-7/16}$ and $T^{7/16}$. If the correct condition for Case A requires $M > T^{7/16}$ rather than $M < T^{7/16}$, then the endpoint block $M \asymp X^{1/2}$ might fall entirely outside the domain of Case A, forcing reliance on Case B or a fallback estimate. This would widen the endpoint gap significantly beyond the currently calculated $\beta_V - \beta_* \approx 0.064$.
