### Denominator-Paired Repair
Consider the family $d_1=d_2$ and $d_3=d_4$ with $d_1 \ne d_3$. The resonance condition $N=0$ reduces to:
$$

$$
Let $\Delta_1 = h_1-h_2$ and $\Delta_2 = h_4-h_3$. If $\Delta_1=0$, then $\Delta_2=0$, which is the exact diagonal. Assume $\Delta_1 \ne 0$ and $\Delta_2 \ne 0$.
Write $\Delta_1 = a c$ and $\Delta_2 = b c$ with $(a,b)=1$ and $c = \gcd(\Delta_1, \Delta_2)$. Then $a d_3 = b d_1$, which implies $d_1 = a k$ and $d_3 = b k$ for some integer $k$.
The singular cases occur when $h_1 = a c$ (forcing $h_2 = 0$, which is excluded since $1 \le |h| \le H_D$) or when variables hit the truncation boundary $H_D$.
The sum over this family is bounded by:
$$

$$
Because $h_i \ne 0$, the sum over $h_2, h_3$ converges and is bounded by $O(\frac{\log^2 H_D}{a b c^2})$. Summing over $a,b,c,k$ such that $ak \asymp D, bk \asymp D$ gives a total mass bounded by $D \log^C X \ll X^{1/2+\epsilon} \ll X^{1+\epsilon}$. Status: [PROVED].

$$

$$

The absolute square is:
$$
|S_2(D;X)|^2 = 4 |\Sigma_1 - \Sigma_3|^2 = 4 \left( |\Sigma_1|^2 + |\Sigma_3|^2 - 2\operatorname{Re}(\Sigma_1 \overline{\Sigma_3}) \right).

$$
R_{\rm CRI} = \frac{|\Sigma_1 - \Sigma_3|^2}{|\Sigma_1|^2 + |\Sigma_3|^2}.

If $R_{\rm CRI} \approx 1$, the cross-term $2\operatorname{Re}(\Sigma_1 \overline{\Sigma_3})$ is negligible, indicating no useful interference between the residue classes, and CRI fails to provide a cancellation mechanism. If $R_{\rm CRI} \ll 1$, the cross-term actively cancels the diagonal, justifying further analytic pursuit. Status: [DERIVED-UNDER-ASSUMPTIONS].
