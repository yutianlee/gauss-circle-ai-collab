## Possible errors or hidden assumptions

1. **A2 may overlabel partial taxonomy as proved.** The fourth-moment identity itself is proved algebra, but the statement that all named $N=0$ families are compatible with the target depends on whether the list is disjoint, complete, and edge-case-safe. In particular, denominator-paired singularities and mixed denominator patterns can overlap with sign-symmetric and truncation-edge cases.

2. **A2's semi-diagonal description needs convention repair.** The phrase "$d_1=d_2=d_3=d_4=d$ forces $h_1-h_2+h_3-h_4=0$" is correct, but any later shorthand such as $h_1+h_2=h_3+h_4$ would be wrong unless signs have been changed by a separate substitution. The exact sign pattern must remain

$$
h_1-h_2+h_3-h_4=0.
$$

3. **A2's mass estimates may ignore $C_h=0$ for even $h$.** This is usually harmless because it reduces support, but if a taxonomy counts all $h$ before applying $C_h$, it may misstate constants and residue-class structure.

4. **A2's near-collision probability model assumes a roughly uniform distribution of $N$.** That is not justified. The map

$$
(\mathbf h,\mathbf d)\mapsto N
$$

has gcd clustering, product structure, parity restrictions, and coefficient concentration at small $|h|$. A2 acknowledges this partly, but the review should keep it as a central gap.

5. **A2's "Hardy-collapse length" can be misleading.** If the B-process gives $m\asymp hX/D^2$, then $q=hm$ has

$$
q\asymp h^2X/D^2.
$$

At maximal height $h\asymp H_D\asymp DX^{-1/4}$ this reaches $q\asymp X^{1/2}$, but the coefficient is supported on a local divisor window. It is not a uniform one-variable Hardy series without additional work.

6. **A3's R5 pseudocode normalizes oddly.** It returns a quantity divided by $D X^{1/4}$ in the displayed code, whereas the proof-level R5 target is the normalized residual itself compared to $X^{1/4+\epsilon}$ per dyadic block. The code may be useful for per-term average diagnostics, but the table should also report the raw block quantity

$$
\frac1{H_D}\sum_{d\asymp D}|w_D(d)|K_{H_D}(\cdots)
$$

and the ratio to $X^{1/4}$.

7. **A3's Fejer formula needs exact modular handling for real $X$.** Integer detection is straightforward when $X$ is an integer or rational, but for real $X$ near an integer resonance, a high-precision threshold should not silently classify near-resonance as resonance. Tables should separately label exact rational resonance, near-resonance, and generic real cases.

8. **A3's "Li--Yang typo is confirmed" is too strong unless the typeset PDF is checked.** It should be "consistent with the TeX/final-argument audit; rendered PDF verification pending."

9. **Both A2 and A3 rely on H4 as correctly quoted.** Vaaler's publication metadata is verified externally, but the exact theorem/page/equation transcription still belongs in the proof draft as a checked dependency.
