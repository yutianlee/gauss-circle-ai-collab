## Possible errors or hidden assumptions

* **A2's Hardy-collapse "isomorphism"** overstates the evidence.  The phase $\sqrt{Xq}$ is of Hardy/Voronoi type, but the coefficient sequence $a_D(q)$ is far from a classical divisor sum; it depends on the dyadic window $D$ and carries the Vaaler weights.  Calling it an isomorphism invites over-interpretation.  The claim status `[CONJECTURED]` is appropriate, but the surrounding language sometimes reads as if a proof strategy is already in hand.

* **A2's near-collision heuristic** assumes that variables $h_i,d_i$ act as independent uniform random variables, which ignores the strict $1/h$-weight concentration, parity restrictions, and correlations in the resonance condition.  The $X^{5/4}$ weighted absolute mass should be treated as a worst-case warning, not as a reliable prediction.

* **A1's stationary-phase constant $e(1/8)$** is correct for a phase with positive second derivative under the convention $e(x)=e^{2\pi i x}$; however, the Fourier convention in the B-process (Poisson) must be matched exactly to avoid sign errors.  A1 does not spell out the Fourier transform convention for the full transform; this is a subtle but critical gap if the H13 route is ever to be tested numerically.

* **A1's R5-Full proof** implicitly assumes $m>0$ and $\ell>0$ for large $X$; a small $X$ threshold is mentioned but not made quantitative.  If $X$ is below some constant, the residual can be absorbed into an $O(1)$ term, but the proof draft should state the threshold explicitly.

* **Vaaler source dependence:** both agents treat H4 as an "external theorem dependency, source-normalized in substance".  The final citation from the rendered PDF is still pending; until then H4 is not fully immune to a sign or constant oversight.  This is a small but real vulnerability.

* **A2's denominator-paired status `[PROVED]`** might be over-optimistic.  While the sum over $a,b,c,k$ can be bounded, the estimate uses $1/h$-weights and may hide a logarithmic divergence that exceeds the $X^\epsilon$ tolerance; a rigorous verification with explicit constants is advisable.

* **Two-sided vs. one-sided conventions:** A2 freezes a two-sided $h$ range, while A1 sometimes refers to paired real formulas (one-sided).  The pair-up formulas are correct only for real $w_D$.  The review should insist that all numerical codes and formulas for M9b/CRI/fourth moment explicitly declare which convention they are using, and that mixed conventions are not applied inadvertently.
