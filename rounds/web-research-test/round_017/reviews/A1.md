$$

## Claims that look correct

The global status remains correct: no agent has proved `M9`, and therefore no new Gauss circle exponent has been proved. The only defensible bridge remains

$$
\text{H1--H3}+\text{H4}+\text{R5-Full}+\text{M9}

This is a conditional reduction, not a theorem resolving the conjectural bound. The project should keep `M9` as the active bottleneck.

`A2`'s Q1-Spectral lemma is correct under its stated hypothesis. On odd denominators, $\chi_4(d)^2=1$, so

$$
U=\operatorname{diag}(\chi_4(d))
$$

is a unitary involution. Therefore, for any finite matrix $K$ on that space,

$$
\|U^*KU\|_{\operatorname{op}}=\|K\|_{\operatorname{op}}.
$$

The same reasoning applies to spectral radius and Frobenius norm, and absolute-value row-sum bounds erase the signs entrywise. This is a rigorous diagnostic for methods that reduce to unitarily invariant or absolute-value matrix estimates.

`A2`'s H12 trace identity is correct in the pure conjugacy form:

$$
\operatorname{Tr}((U^*KU)^m)=\operatorname{Tr}(K^m).
$$

The output correctly says this does not block open-path moments or non-conjugacy expressions such as $\operatorname{Tr}(KU)$ or signed bilinear forms.

`A3`'s coefficient conjugacy is correct:

$$
\alpha_{-h,H}=\overline{\alpha_{h,H}}.
$$

This is important because several earlier toy computations used or risked using the wrong sign. The paired real formulas for $\mathcal M_1$ and $\mathcal M_2$ follow from this identity and should be adopted for all numerical work.

`A3`'s corrected R5 kernel is also right in form:

$$
W_\Delta(u)=
\begin{cases}
1,&u=0,\\
\min(1,\Delta^2/u^2),&u\ne0.
\end{cases}
$$

This avoids division by zero at exact resonances and is the correct product-counting cap for the Fejer residual.

`A3`'s H13 stationary phase constants look consistent under the convention

$$
\phi_n(u)=hX/u-nu/4.
$$

For $n=-m$,

$$
u_0=2\sqrt{\frac{hX}{m}},
\qquad
\phi_{-m}(u_0)=\sqrt{hXm},
\qquad
m\asymp\frac{hX}{D^2}.
$$

The Hessian of the resulting two-variable phase $\sqrt{hXm}$ is degenerate, so generic full-rank two-dimensional stationary phase or full-rank decoupling should not be invoked.

## Possible errors or hidden assumptions

The largest issue in `A2` is the target normalization for `BSOS`. In the excerpt, `A2` states that after Cauchy--Schwarz the full quadratic form should be bounded by something like

$$
X^{1/2+\epsilon}H_D^{-1}.
$$

That mixes normalizations. With the weighted Cauchy--Schwarz displayed earlier,

$$
|\mathcal M_1|^2
\le
\left(\sum_h|\alpha_h|\right)
\left(\sum_h|\alpha_h||S_h|^2\right),
$$

the required quadratic-form target is essentially $X^{1/2+\epsilon}$ up to logarithms, not automatically $X^{1/2+\epsilon}H_D^{-1}$. If one instead uses unweighted Cauchy--Schwarz, the kernel has $|\alpha_h|^2$ and a different $H_D$ factor. `A2` must freeze one Cauchy--Schwarz normalization and derive the exact BSOS target from it. This is a likely mathematical normalization error, not just a presentation issue.

`A2` sometimes describes C3 parity collapse as "proved" in a way that may be too broad. The algebra

$$
(-1)^{m_1}(-1)^{m_2}=(-1)^q
$$

for translation differencing is elementary, but its relevance to `M9` or `H13` depends on the actual dual variables and differencing operation. Keep it as a diagnostic unless the variable map is explicit.

`A3`'s R5 toy computation is not valid as evidence at the stated scale. In the excerpt, the product-count comparison for $X=100$ notes $\tau(100)=9$ while $X^{1/4}\approx3.16$, yet concludes the total is well under $X^{1/4}$. That finite-size assertion is not reliable. The asymptotic proof uses $\tau(n)\ll_\epsilon n^\epsilon$ and absorbs finite constants into $X^\epsilon$; a tiny $X=100$ check should be treated only as formula debugging, not evidence for the bound.

`A3`'s H4 table is useful but not complete. It claims an "exact source-notation dictionary," but the displayed table still has unresolved page entries. This should be marked "source-located / notation map drafted," not "fully source-normalized."

`A3`'s Li--Yang comparison is useful, but its shifted-$F$ comments include an assumption that a small $D,X$-dependent additive shift can likely be absorbed into uniform constants. That may be true, but it is a theorem-hypothesis issue, not a derivative-calculus issue. The line-by-line theorem audit must decide whether the theorem permits a family $F_{\rho,D}$ rather than a fixed $F$.

Neither agent gives actual endpoint-scale numerical tables. `A2` proposes finite tests; `A3` gives protocols and small symbolic checks. The next round should produce executable scripts or tables, not more prose protocols.
