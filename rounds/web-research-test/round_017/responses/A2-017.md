## Detailed reasoning: Q1-Spectral-R17 and the M9a First-Step Kernel

We formalize the operator-norm character-blindness diagnostic for the actual `M9a` first-step kernel.

The `M9a` target is:
$$
\mathcal M_1(D;X) = -4\sum_{1\le |h|\le H_D} \alpha_{h,H_D} \sum_{d\asymp D} \chi_4(d)w_D(d)e(hX/d).
$$
A standard Bombieri--Iwaniec or large-sieve approach begins by applying the Cauchy--Schwarz inequality over the frequency variable $h$ to eliminate the Vaaler coefficients $\alpha_{h,H_D}$ and prepare the spatial variables for a spacing theorem.

Let the finite index set be the odd denominators in the dyadic block:
$$
\mathcal D_{\rm odd} = \{d \in \mathbb{Z} : D \le d < 2D, \; 2 \nmid d\}.
$$
Let $\mathcal V_D = \ell^2(\mathcal D_{\rm odd})$ be the associated finite-dimensional Hilbert space.
Applying Cauchy--Schwarz over $1 \le |h| \le H_D$ yields:
$$
|\mathcal M_1(D;X)|^2 \le 16 \left( \sum_{1\le |h|\le H_D} |\alpha_{h,H_D}| \right) \left( \sum_{1\le |h|\le H_D} |\alpha_{h,H_D}| \left| \sum_{d \in \mathcal D_{\rm odd}} \chi_4(d)w_D(d)e(hX/d) \right|^2 \right).
$$
Expanding the absolute square in the second factor, we obtain the signed quadratic form:
$$
\mathcal Q = \sum_{d_1, d_2 \in \mathcal D_{\rm odd}} \chi_4(d_1)\chi_4(d_2) w_D(d_1)w_D(d_2) \sum_{1\le |h|\le H_D} |\alpha_{h,H_D}| e\left( hX \left( \frac{1}{d_1} - \frac{1}{d_2} \right) \right).
$$
Define the actual first-step Gram matrix $K$ on $\mathcal V_D \times \mathcal V_D$ by its entries:
$$
K_{d_1, d_2} = w_D(d_1)w_D(d_2) \sum_{1\le |h|\le H_D} |\alpha_{h,H_D}| e\left( hX \left( \frac{1}{d_1} - \frac{1}{d_2} \right) \right).
$$
Define the diagonal operator $U$ on $\mathcal V_D$ by:
$$
U = \operatorname{diag}(\chi_4(d))_{d \in \mathcal D_{\rm odd}}.
$$
Since $d$ is odd, $\chi_4(d) \in \{1, -1\}$, which implies $\chi_4(d)^2 = 1$. Therefore, $U$ is a real diagonal unitary involution: $U = U^* = U^{-1}$.
Let $v \in \mathcal V_D$ be the all-ones vector, $v_d = 1$ for all $d \in \mathcal D_{\rm odd}$. The signed quadratic form can be written exactly as:
$$
\mathcal Q = v^* U^* K U v.
$$
If a proof attempts to bound $\mathcal Q$ by extracting the operator norm of the conjugated matrix, it uses the inequality:
$$
|\mathcal Q| \le \|v\|^2 \|U^* K U\|_{\operatorname{op}} = |\mathcal D_{\rm odd}| \|U^* K U\|_{\operatorname{op}}.
$$
However, because $U$ is unitary, the operator norm is unitarily invariant:
$$
\|U^* K U\|_{\operatorname{op}} = \|K\|_{\operatorname{op}}.
$$
**Conclusion [PROVED]:** The bound $|\mathcal D_{\rm odd}| \|K\|_{\operatorname{op}}$ is entirely independent of the character $\chi_4$. Any method that reduces the `M9a` sum to an operator-norm bound on the conjugated matrix $U^* K U$ is strictly character-blind.

This diagnostic explicitly blocks:
- Spectral radius bounds on $U^* K U$.
- Schur test or Gershgorin circle bounds applied to the absolute values $|(U^* K U)_{d_1, d_2}| = |K_{d_1, d_2}|$.
- Frobenius norm bounds, since $\|U^* K U\|_F = \|K\|_F$.

This diagnostic does **not** block:
- Direct evaluation of the signed bilinear form $v^* U^* K U v$ (e.g., exploiting the sign of $\chi_4(d_1)\chi_4(d_2)$ on specific off-diagonal rational-collision elements).
- Cross-residue statistics that do not factor through the all-ones vector $v$.

## Detailed reasoning: H12-R17 Pure Cyclic Trace Invariance

We narrowly prove the trace invariance for pure cyclic moments.
If a method attempts to bound the spectral radius of $U^* K U$ by computing the trace of a high power $m$, it evaluates:
$$
\operatorname{Tr}((U^* K U)^m).
$$
By the cyclic property of the trace and the involution property $U U^* = I$, we have:
$$
\operatorname{Tr}((U^* K U)^m) = \operatorname{Tr}(U^* K^m U) = \operatorname{Tr}(K^m U U^*) = \operatorname{Tr}(K^m).
$$
**Conclusion [PROVED]:** Pure conjugacy-invariant cyclic traces of the form $\operatorname{Tr}((U^* K U)^m)$ are identical to the unsigned traces $\operatorname{Tr}(K^m)$. They cannot exploit the spatial character.

This narrowly blocks eigenvalue-moment methods that rely solely on the sum of the $m$-th powers of the eigenvalues of the conjugated matrix. It does **not** block open-path moments such as $v^* (U^* K U)^m v$, nor does it block traces of non-conjugacy expressions like $\operatorname{Tr}(K U)$.

$$
S(h, D) = \sum_{r=1}^3 \chi_4(r) \sum_{n \in \mathbb{Z}} \frac{1}{4} \int_{\mathbb{R}} w_D(v) e(hX/v) e(-n(v-r)/4) dv

$$
= \frac{1}{4} \sum_{n \in \mathbb{Z}} \left( \sum_{r=1}^3 \chi_4(r) e(nr/4) \right) \int_{\mathbb{R}} w_D(v) e(hX/v - nv/4) dv.

The Gauss sum is exactly $e(n/4) - e(3n/4) = 2i \chi_4(n)$. Thus:
$$
S(h, D) = \frac{i}{2} \sum_{n \in \mathbb{Z}} \chi_4(n) \int_{\mathbb{R}} w_D(v) e(hX/v - nv/4) dv.

$$

where $A(h,m)$ absorbs the amplitude and weight functions.

$$

## Detailed reasoning: BSOS Falsification Statistic

To provide an executable metric for signed cancellation that avoids the `Q1-Spectral` trap, we define the Bilinear Signed Off-diagonal Statistic (`BSOS`).

Let $K_{d_1, d_2}$ be the actual `M9a` first-step kernel defined in Section 3.
The full signed quadratic form is $\mathcal Q = \sum_{d_1, d_2 \in \mathcal D_{\rm odd}} \chi_4(d_1)\chi_4(d_2) K_{d_1, d_2}$.
The diagonal contribution is:
$$
\mathcal Q_{\rm diag} = \sum_{d \in \mathcal D_{\rm odd}} \chi_4(d)^2 K_{d,d} = \sum_{d \in \mathcal D_{\rm odd}} w_D(d)^2 \sum_{1\le |h|\le H_D} |\alpha_{h,H_D}| \asymp D \log H_D.
$$
Define the finite signed off-diagonal statistic:
$$
\mathcal S_{\rm signed} = \sum_{\substack{d_1, d_2 \in \mathcal D_{\rm odd} \\ d_1 \ne d_2}} \chi_4(d_1)\chi_4(d_2) K_{d_1, d_2}.
$$
Define the absolute-majorant comparator:
$$
\mathcal S_{\rm abs} = \sum_{\substack{d_1, d_2 \in \mathcal D_{\rm odd} \\ d_1 \ne d_2}} |K_{d_1, d_2}|.
$$
Define the operator-norm comparator:
$$
\mathcal S_{\rm op} = |\mathcal D_{\rm odd}| \|K - \operatorname{diag}(K)\|_{\operatorname{op}}.
$$
**Target Bound:** To imply $\mathcal M_1(D;X) \ll_\epsilon X^{1/4+\epsilon}$, the Cauchy--Schwarz inequality requires the full quadratic form to be bounded by $X^{1/2+\epsilon} H_D^{-1}$. Since the diagonal is $O(D \log H_D) \ll X^{1/2}$, we require:
$$
\mathcal S_{\rm signed} \ll_\epsilon X^{1/2+\epsilon} H_D^{-1}.
$$
**Falsification Criterion [CONJECTURED]:** If numerical evaluation at moderate scales ($D \asymp 10^3, X \asymp 10^6$) shows that $|\mathcal S_{\rm signed}| \asymp \mathcal S_{\rm abs}$ or $|\mathcal S_{\rm signed}| \asymp \mathcal S_{\rm op}$, then the character signs $\chi_4(d_1)\chi_4(d_2)$ are not providing asymptotic cancellation over the off-diagonal elements. In this case, `BSOS` fails to improve upon character-blind bounds, indicating that Cauchy--Schwarz over $h$ is a fundamentally lossy step that destroys the arithmetic structure of `M9a`.

## Detailed reasoning: C3 Parity Diagnostics and M9/H13 Mapping

We map the `C3` parity-collapse diagnostics directly to the dual of the `H5r` parity residual to determine if spacing methods can exploit the alternating sign.

The `H5r` parity residual is:
$$

Let $\sigma(m) = (-1)^m$.
If we apply Cauchy--Schwarz over $k$ to the dual sum, the off-diagonal terms involve a translation shift $q = m_1 - m_2$.
The coefficient product is:
$$
\sigma(m_1) \sigma(m_2) = (-1)^{m_1} (-1)^{m_2} = (-1)^{m_1 + m_2}.
$$
Since $m_1 + m_2 \equiv m_1 - m_2 \pmod 2$, we have:
$$
\sigma(m_1) \sigma(m_2) = (-1)^{m_1 - m_2} = (-1)^q.
$$
**Conclusion [PROVED]:** In the dual of the `H5r` parity residual, translation-invariant differencing (where the shift $q$ is fixed and we sum over the location $m_2$) completely collapses the alternating sign into a constant $(-1)^q$ that factors out of the inner summation. For odd dilations (rational spacing steps where $m_1 = a m_2$ with $a$ odd):
$$
\sigma(m_2) \sigma(a m_2) = (-1)^{m_2(a+1)}.
$$
Since $a$ is odd, $a+1$ is even, so $(-1)^{m_2(a+1)} = 1$.
**Conclusion [PROVED]:** Odd-dilation spacing steps completely erase the parity sign. `C3` is a severe, proved obstruction for dual methods applied to the `H5r` parity residual.
